Agent Misalignment and AI Alignment Issues

Authors: Unknown, Unknown

This document explores the various consequences stemming from misalignment within agentic AI systems, a critical area of concern within broader AI alignment research. The core issue revolves around the potential for agents to be exploited or exhibit unintended behavior, leading to a range of detrimental outcomes. A primary concern is 'Agent action abuse,' where malicious actors leverage an agent's ability to interact with its environment to carry out harmful tasks, often facilitated by adversarial attacks. This can result in data exfiltration and the introduction of a secondary, adversarial intent alongside the agent's original programming. Another significant risk is 'Agent denial of service,' where an agent's intended functionality is deliberately disrupted or degraded, rendering it effectively useless. Furthermore, the document highlights 'Incorrect decision-making,' arising from flaws in the information processed or the agent's decision-making process, potentially leading to flawed outcomes. A related issue is 'User trust erosion,' where a user's confidence in the system diminishes, impacting future engagement. Finally, the document addresses 'Impact outside intended environment,' recognizing that agents can have unforeseen consequences, regardless of whether they are positive or negative. The key factor is the occurrence of this impact beyond the system's designed boundaries. The document emphasizes that these misalignments are not isolated incidents but rather represent a systemic challenge within the development and deployment of agentic AI, demanding careful consideration of safeguards and alignment strategies. It draws upon research, including the paper '[2209.00626] The Alignment Problem from a Deep Learning Perspective,' to underscore the importance of addressing these issues proactively. The document's focus aligns with the broader Responsible AI Standard, as outlined in Microsoft's 'Responsible AI - Standard - v2 - General - Requirements'.
