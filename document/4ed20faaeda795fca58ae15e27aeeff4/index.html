The Stanford Emerging Technology Review 2025: A Report on Ten Key Technologies and Their Policy Implications

Authors: Condoleezza Rice, John B. Taylor, Jennifer Widom, Amy Zegart, Herbert S. Lin

The Stanford Emerging Technology Review 2025 is a comprehensive report examining ten key technologies – Artificial Intelligence, Biotechnology and Synthetic Biology, Cryptography, Lasers, Materials Science, Neuroscience, Robotics, Semiconductors, Space, and Sustainable Energy Technologies – alongside their significant policy implications. Published by Stanford University, the report highlights the crucial role of universities, particularly Stanford, in navigating the evolving landscape of technological advancement and shaping future policy. It emphasizes the need for cross-sector collaboration and intergenerational engagement to harness technology for societal benefit. The document details key observations regarding how technologies evolve over time, identifying common innovation enablers and inhibitors. Furthermore, it explores technology applications across various policy areas including economic growth, national security, environmental and energy sustainability, health and medicine, and civil society. The report underscores the importance of government involvement in setting technological trajectories and advocates for a holistic approach to technology development and governance. The document's structure includes an executive summary, introduction, detailed analyses of each technology field, crosscutting themes, and a conclusion. It’s a forward-looking assessment designed to inform policy decisions and strategic investments in emerging technologies. The report’s focus on Stanford University’s role reflects the institution’s commitment to research and innovation in these critical areas.
