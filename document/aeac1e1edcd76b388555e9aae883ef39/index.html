Deep Learning in Production

Authors: Sergios Karagiannakos

This book, "Deep Learning in Production," authored by Sergios Karagiannakos, provides a comprehensive guide to deploying and managing deep learning models in a production environment. The book begins with an introductory section outlining its goals and intended audience, addressing potential learning difficulties and explaining how to effectively utilize the material. It then delves into the core aspects of designing a machine learning system, covering crucial areas such as data engineering, model engineering, and DevOps engineering. The book emphasizes the importance of integrating these disciplines to create robust and scalable deep learning solutions. A significant portion is dedicated to setting up a deep learning workstation, specifically focusing on laptop configurations and requirements. The book aims to equip readers with the knowledge and practical skills needed to navigate the complexities of bringing deep learning models from research to real-world applications. It's structured to guide users through the entire lifecycle of a deep learning project, from initial design to deployment and ongoing maintenance. The book's approach is geared towards providing a hands-on understanding of the challenges and best practices involved in building and operating deep learning systems in a production setting. The initial chapters establish the foundational concepts and the subsequent sections progressively build upon this knowledge, culminating in a practical guide to workstation setup. The book's overall goal is to demystify the process of deploying deep learning models and to provide readers with the tools and techniques necessary to succeed in this rapidly evolving field. It's a practical resource for anyone involved in the development, deployment, and management of deep learning systems.
