Risk Management Process: An Interagency Security Committee Standard 2021 Edition

Authors: U.S. Department of Homeland Security Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency, Interagency Security Committee

This document, the 2021 Edition of the Risk Management Process: An Interagency Security Committee Standard, is produced by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security’s Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) and the Interagency Security Committee (ISC). The ISC, chaired by the DHS, comprises 64 departments and agencies with the mission of developing security policies, standards, and recommendations specifically for nonmilitary federal facilities within the United States. The standard outlines the criteria and processes that organizations must adhere to. The document’s change history reflects updates from 2013 (Revision 1.0) and 2016 (Revision 2.0), indicating ongoing refinement and adaptation of the security protocols. A key focus of the ISC is the protection of federal employees and private citizens who work within or visit federally owned or leased facilities. The 2021 edition represents a current iteration of this critical security framework, highlighting the ongoing commitment to safeguarding national assets and personnel. The distribution of this document is authorized to federal, state, local agencies, and private individuals or enterprises, signifying its importance across a broad range of stakeholders involved in security operations and risk management within the U.S. government and related sectors.
