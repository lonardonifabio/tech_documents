Oversight Structures for Agentic AI in Public-Sector Organizations

Authors: Chris Schmitz, Jonathan Rystrøm, Jan Batzner

This research paper addresses the significant challenges posed by the introduction of agentic AI systems within public sector organizations. It argues that existing oversight mechanisms, traditionally based on siloed compliance units and infrequent approvals, are inadequate for the continuous and integrated supervision required by agentic AI. The study identifies five key governance dimensions crucial for responsible deployment: cross-departmental implementation, comprehensive evaluation, enhanced security protocols, operational visibility, and systematic auditing. The research employs a mixed-methods approach, combining a literature review with interviews of civil servants involved in AI-related roles, to assess the capacity of current oversight structures. The findings reveal that agent oversight exacerbates three core governance issues: the need for continuous oversight, the deeper integration of governance and operational capabilities, and improved interdepartmental coordination. The authors propose adaptable institutional structures and agent oversight designs specifically tailored to the constraints of the public sector. The paper emphasizes the urgency of revising existing frameworks to effectively manage the risks and opportunities presented by agentic AI, advocating for a more proactive and holistic approach to governance. The research highlights the importance of moving beyond reactive compliance models to embrace a dynamic and integrated system of oversight. Furthermore, the study underscores the need for collaboration and knowledge sharing across government agencies to ensure consistent and effective agentic AI governance. The implications of this work extend to policymakers, regulators, and public sector organizations seeking to leverage the potential of agentic AI while mitigating associated risks. The research contributes to the growing body of literature on AI governance and provides practical recommendations for building robust oversight structures in the public sector. Ultimately, the paper calls for a fundamental shift in how public sector organizations approach AI governance, recognizing the unique challenges and opportunities presented by agentic AI systems.
