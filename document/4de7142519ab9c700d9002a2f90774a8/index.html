Generative AI and the Nature of Work

Authors: Manuel Hoffmann, Sam Boysel, Frank Nagle, Sida Peng, Kevin Xu

This working paper, titled "Generative AI and the Nature of Work", explores the potential impact of recent advancements in generative AI technology on the nature of work, particularly within the knowledge economy. The authors, affiliated with Harvard Business School, Microsoft Corporation, and GitHub Inc., investigate how individuals adjust their work practices when utilizing AI tools. The research focuses on the context of open-source software development to examine individual-level effects. The paper acknowledges the growing body of literature surrounding AI's productivity effects but highlights a relative lack of attention to the fundamental shifts in how people work. It addresses the question of how individuals, especially those involved in knowledge-based activities, adapt their workflows when AI becomes integrated into their tasks. The study utilizes the open-source software environment as a case study to analyze these changes. The working paper is presented in draft form and is intended for comment and discussion purposes, with a copyright held by the authors. Funding for this research was partially provided by Harvard Business School. The authors aim to provide insights into the evolving relationship between humans and AI in the workplace, considering the specific challenges and opportunities presented by generative AI. The document emphasizes the need for further investigation into the broader implications of AI on work processes and the skills required for success in an AI-augmented environment. It’s a preliminary exploration of a critical and rapidly developing area of research.
