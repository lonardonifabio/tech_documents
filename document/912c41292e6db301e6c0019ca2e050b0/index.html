EU AI Act and UNESCO Recommendations on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence: A Mapping Exercise

Authors: Sean Musch, Michael Charles Borrelli, Charles Kerrigan, Thomas Hoon, Markus Krebsz

This report, published by AI & Partners, provides a comparative analysis of the UNESCO Recommendations on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence and the EU AI Act. The document aims to map the key similarities and differences between these two significant frameworks governing the development and deployment of artificial intelligence. AI & Partners, a non-profit organization, is dedicated to defending and extending the digital rights of users globally, particularly those at risk. Their approach combines technical support, policy engagement, advocacy, professional services, regulatory interventions, and participation in industry groups like AI Commons. The report specifically addresses the core questions surrounding the UNESCO AI Ethics Principles, examining their alignment with the EU AI Act. It highlights the importance of the UNESCO principles as a foundational set of guidelines for ethical AI development. The document’s purpose is to facilitate a deeper understanding of the evolving landscape of AI governance, emphasizing the need for a human-centric approach to AI development and deployment. The report’s findings contribute to the ongoing dialogue surrounding responsible AI innovation and the protection of fundamental rights in the age of artificial intelligence. AI & Partners’ work underscores the critical role of organizations like theirs in navigating the complex ethical and regulatory challenges posed by rapidly advancing AI technologies. The report’s structure includes an introduction and a detailed exploration of the UNESCO AI Ethics Principles and their relationship to the EU AI Act, offering a valuable resource for policymakers, researchers, and anyone interested in the future of AI governance.
