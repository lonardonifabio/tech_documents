AI Governance by Design for Agentic Systems: A Framework for Responsible Development and Deployment

Authors: Himanshu Joshi

This concept paper, titled "AI Governance by Design for Agentic Systems: A Framework for Responsible Development and Deployment," proposes a proactive approach to Artificial Intelligence (AI) development, termed AI Governance by Design (AIGD). The paper argues for embedding ethical, legal, and societal considerations directly into AI system development from the initial stages, rather than as an afterthought. It analyzes existing frameworks and organizational practices to identify core principles, implementation strategies, and the significant challenges organizations encounter when adopting this approach. The research specifically focuses on agentic AI systems – systems designed to operate autonomously and make decisions – and examines how to ensure these systems are developed responsibly. The paper highlights the importance of integrating these considerations throughout the entire lifecycle of an AI system, from design to deployment. It addresses the complexities of autonomous decision-making within AI, recognizing the potential risks and the need for robust governance mechanisms. The work contributes to the growing field of Responsible AI, advocating for a shift towards a more proactive and holistic approach to AI development. The concept paper aims to provide a foundational framework for organizations seeking to build trustworthy and ethically aligned agentic AI systems.
