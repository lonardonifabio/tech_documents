GenAI and the psychology of work

Authors: Erik Hermann, Stefano Puntoni, Carey K. Morewedge

This opinion piece explores the significant impact of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) on the evolving landscape of work. It argues that the rapid adoption of GenAI is fundamentally altering workplaces by challenging established human-machine boundaries and redefining the nature of work itself. Unlike previous technologies, GenAI possesses cognitive, creative, and interpersonal capabilities, prompting a reevaluation of traditional roles and responsibilities. The document highlights the potential benefits of GenAI for worker productivity and performance, while simultaneously acknowledging its potential psychological threats to fundamental human needs – competence, autonomy, and relatedness. These threats trigger coping strategies among workers. The authors emphasize the need to proactively address these psychological risks and foster human-centered workplaces that effectively balance the advantages and disadvantages of GenAI integration. The piece underscores that work is not solely an economic activity but also a crucial source of identity, meaning, and social connection. The research presented aims to understand how GenAI is reshaping work dynamics and to provide insights into creating workplaces that can successfully navigate this technological shift. The document provides a foundational overview of the topic, setting the stage for further investigation into the complex relationship between GenAI and the human experience of work. It’s a timely exploration of a rapidly changing field, considering both the opportunities and the potential challenges posed by this new technology.
