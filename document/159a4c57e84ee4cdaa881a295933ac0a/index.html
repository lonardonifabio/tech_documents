Analysis of EU AI Office stakeholder consultations: defining AI systems and prohibited applications

Authors: CEPS, European Commission

This final study report, commissioned by the European Commission’s Directorate-General for Communications Networks, Content and Technology (CNECT) Artificial Intelligence Office, delves into the stakeholder consultations conducted by the EU AI Office. The primary objective of the analysis is to establish a clear definition of AI systems and identify prohibited applications within the context of the EU's AI strategy. The report meticulously examines the input received from various stakeholders, including industry representatives, researchers, civil society organizations, and policymakers. It aims to provide a robust framework for understanding the diverse perspectives surrounding AI development and deployment. The study focuses on translating the complex discussions into actionable insights, contributing to the development of effective regulatory measures. A key component of the analysis involves categorizing the different types of AI systems discussed and pinpointing applications that raise significant ethical, legal, and societal concerns. The report’s findings are crucial for informing the EU’s approach to AI governance, ensuring that regulations are both effective and proportionate. Furthermore, the document highlights the importance of ongoing dialogue and collaboration between the AI Office and its stakeholders. The study’s conclusions are intended to guide the Commission in shaping a future where AI benefits society while mitigating potential risks. The report’s methodology incorporates both qualitative and quantitative data analysis, providing a comprehensive assessment of the consultation process. It addresses critical questions related to bias, transparency, accountability, and safety in AI systems. The document’s findings are presented with a strong emphasis on clarity and accessibility, aiming to facilitate informed decision-making across various sectors. The report’s conclusions are intended to support the development of a harmonized approach to AI regulation across the European Union. It underscores the need for continuous monitoring and adaptation as the field of AI continues to evolve. The study’s recommendations are grounded in a deep understanding of the technical, legal, and ethical challenges associated with AI.
