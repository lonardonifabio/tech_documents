AI Trust and Acceptance: A Global Perspective

Authors: The University of Queensland, KPMG International

This document analyzes global perceptions of artificial intelligence, focusing on trust, acceptance, and concerns surrounding its development and implementation. The research highlights a significant disparity in attitudes across different countries, with China consistently demonstrating the highest levels of optimism and acceptance towards AI. Notably, China ranks alongside India in positively perceiving AI and exhibits the most relaxed attitude (91%) regarding AI, coupled with a high level of confidence in regulatory entities. Globally, 67% of respondents express willingness to trust AI, while 33% remain unsure or unwilling. Acceptance of AI is exceptionally high at 95%, surpassing other nations. Despite this, concerns persist, particularly regarding cybersecurity (79%) and job displacement due to automation (75%). A significant 70% acknowledge various risks associated with AI, with cybersecurity and automation being the most frequently cited. A notable 81% believe the benefits of AI outweigh the risks, a sentiment shared by fewer countries. The study reveals a concerning uncertainty regarding the societal impact of AI, with 63% perceiving it as unpredictable. The document underscores a global trend of acceptance, but also highlights the need for careful consideration of potential risks, particularly cybersecurity and automation, and the uncertainty surrounding AI's long-term societal consequences. The research emphasizes the importance of understanding diverse perspectives on AI and addressing concerns proactively to foster responsible development and deployment.
