Guidelines for the Responsible Use of Artificial Intelligence in the Public Service

Authors: Public Expenditure Department, NDP Delivery and Reform

This document provides comprehensive guidelines for the responsible implementation of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the Public Service Department. It begins with an executive summary and an introduction defining AI and outlining the intended audience – public service professionals. The core of the document details various aspects of AI’s application in public services, including the types of tasks AI can address, the potential benefits, and crucially, the associated risks. A central focus is placed on establishing principles for responsible AI use, covering key areas such as human agency and oversight, technical robustness and safety, privacy and data governance, transparency, diversity, non-discrimination, and fairness, alongside societal and environmental well-being and accountability. The document then presents a decision framework to guide users in determining whether AI is the appropriate solution and, if so, selecting the best fit, considering factors like cost and licensing. It emphasizes the importance of diversity and inclusion from the outset of AI projects. A key tool introduced is the ‘Responsible AI Canvas,’ a visual aid designed to facilitate a structured assessment of AI initiatives. Finally, the document outlines a lifecycle approach to responsible AI adoption, incorporating the seven principles across the entire process, from initial concept to ongoing monitoring. The overall aim is to ensure that AI is deployed ethically and effectively within the public sector, maximizing its benefits while mitigating potential harms. This document is a critical resource for public service organizations seeking to navigate the complexities of AI adoption and align their practices with best-practice guidelines.
