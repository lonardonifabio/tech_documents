Quick Start Guide to Large Language Models

Authors: Sinan Ozdemir

This document provides a comprehensive quick start guide for utilizing Large Language Models (LLMs), primarily focusing on ChatGPT and other similar models. It's structured around two main parts: an introduction to LLMs and strategies for maximizing their potential. Part I begins with an overview of LLMs, defining them and exploring various types, including popular modern models like ChatGPT, domain-specific LLMs, and their diverse applications. It then delves into launching applications using proprietary models and introduces prompt engineering with GPT3, outlining best practices for crafting effective prompts. The section on optimizing LLMs emphasizes fine-tuning techniques, particularly utilizing the OpenAI Fine-Tuning API and the OpenAI CLI for preparing custom examples and creating fine-tuned LLMs. A case study on Amazon review category classification is included to illustrate the practical application of fine-tuning. Part II expands on these concepts, focusing on advanced prompt engineering techniques, such as prompt injection awareness, and strategies for moving beyond foundational models. It covers customizing embeddings and model architectures, and provides guidance on deploying custom LLMs to the cloud. The guide aims to equip readers with the foundational knowledge and practical steps needed to effectively leverage LLMs for various applications, from building conversational AI bots to customizing models for specific use cases. The document is designed as a practical guide, offering a blend of theoretical explanations and hands-on instructions, making it suitable for both beginners and those seeking to deepen their understanding of LLMs.
