Artificial Intelligence in the Military Domain and its Implications for International Peace and Security: An Evidence-Based Roadmap for Future Policy Action

Authors: Giacomo Persi Paoli, Yasmin Afina, Sarah Grand-Clement, Ioana Puscas, Shimona Mohan, Jessica Abowitz

This document, produced by UNIDIR’s Security and Technology Programme (SECTEC), examines the impact of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the military domain and its subsequent implications for international peace and security. It presents an evidence-based roadmap designed to guide future policy actions related to this increasingly complex area. The research is underpinned by support from a consortium of nations including Czechia, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, the Republic of Korea, and Switzerland, alongside significant backing from Microsoft. UNIDIR, a voluntarily funded and autonomous institute within the United Nations, plays a crucial role in generating knowledge and fostering dialogue and action concerning disarmament and security challenges. The Institute’s work is centered in Geneva and focuses on providing practical, innovative solutions to critical security problems. The publication highlights the importance of a structured approach to managing the development and deployment of AI in military contexts, recognizing the potential for both positive and negative consequences. It emphasizes the need for international cooperation and the establishment of clear guidelines to mitigate risks and maximize the benefits of AI while upholding international peace and security. The document’s creation is a collaborative effort, drawing on the expertise of a team of researchers and contributors. Furthermore, the text explicitly states that the designations used within the document do not reflect any UN opinion on legal status or territorial boundaries, ensuring neutrality in its presentation. The core mission of UNIDIR is to assist the international community in developing effective strategies for addressing complex security issues, and this publication represents a key component of that effort.
