Large Language Models and Emotional Intelligence Tests

Authors: Katja Schlegel, Nils R. Sommer, Marcello Mortillaro

This research investigates the capabilities of large language models (LLMs) in assessing and generating emotional intelligence. The study centers around examining whether LLMs, specifically ChatGPT-4, ChatGPT-o1, Gemini 1.5 flash, Copilot 365, Claude 3.5 Haiku, and DeepSeek V3, can effectively perform on and create emotional intelligence tests. The initial findings revealed a significant advantage for the LLMs, with an average accuracy of 81% across five standard emotional intelligence tests, surpassing the 56% accuracy achieved by human participants in previous validation studies. To further explore the LLMs' potential, ChatGPT-4 was utilized to generate new test items for each of the original emotional intelligence tests. A subsequent study involving 467 human participants administered both the original and ChatGPT-generated tests, confirming statistically equivalent test difficulty. The research concludes that LLMs demonstrate a notable capacity for emotional intelligence assessment and test generation, though the differences are relatively minor. The study provides valuable insights into the evolving role of AI in psychological testing and evaluation. The findings contribute to a better understanding of how LLMs can be leveraged for assessing human cognitive abilities, particularly in the domain of emotional intelligence. The research highlights the potential for AI to augment traditional psychological assessment methods, while also acknowledging the need for continued scrutiny and validation of AI-generated tests. The use of multiple LLMs and a comprehensive set of evaluation metrics strengthens the validity of the conclusions. The study's design, incorporating both original and newly generated tests, offers a robust approach to assessing the performance of LLMs in this context.
