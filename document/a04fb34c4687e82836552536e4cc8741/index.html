An Introduction to Google’s Approach to AI Agent Security

Authors: Santiago Díaz, Christoph Kern, Kara Olive

This document, published in May 2025, provides an introduction to Google’s strategy for securing AI agents. It addresses the significant security challenges posed by the increasing autonomy and complexity of AI agents. The core argument centers around the inherent risks associated with AI agent behavior, particularly stemming from their unpredictable nature, emergent behaviors, and elevated decision-making capabilities. The paper highlights key risks including rogue actions, sensitive data disclosure, and the difficulty in ensuring alignment between agent actions and user intent, especially when dealing with ambiguous instructions or untrusted inputs. A primary focus is on the need for robust Agent Security, a specialized field dedicated to mitigating these novel vulnerabilities. Google’s approach is described as a ‘hybrid defense-in-depth,’ suggesting a layered security strategy. The document emphasizes the challenges of managing agent identity and privileges effectively, recognizing that the very characteristics of AI agents – their non-deterministic behavior and potential for complex, emergent actions – create significant security concerns. It positions this work as part of Google’s broader effort to share best practices for building secure AI systems, referencing the Google Secure AI Framework (SAIF). The document implicitly advocates for a proactive and comprehensive security strategy tailored to the unique risks of AI agent technology. The paper’s intent is to educate and guide developers and security professionals in understanding and addressing these emerging threats. The core message is that securing AI agents requires a specialized approach, acknowledging the inherent unpredictability and potential for misuse within these systems.
