The Data Science Design Manual

Authors: Steven S. Skiena

The Data Science Design Manual by Steven S. Skiena is a comprehensive guide focused on the practical aspects of data science. It emphasizes a design-oriented approach, encouraging readers to think critically about the entire data science process, from problem definition to solution implementation. The book moves beyond simply presenting algorithms and techniques, instead focusing on the underlying principles and best practices for building effective data science solutions. It covers a wide range of topics including data collection, data cleaning, data exploration, feature engineering, model selection, and model evaluation. A key element of the book is its emphasis on the importance of understanding the business context of a data science project. Skiena stresses the need to clearly define the problem, identify the key stakeholders, and ensure that the solution aligns with the business goals. The manual provides a framework for systematically approaching data science challenges, promoting a structured and rigorous methodology. It also includes numerous examples and case studies to illustrate the concepts and techniques discussed. The book is geared towards both novice and experienced data scientists, offering a valuable resource for anyone seeking to improve their data science skills and build successful data-driven projects.
