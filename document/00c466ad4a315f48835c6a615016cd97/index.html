Chart Suggestions - A Thought-Starter

Authors: A. Abela

This document excerpt presents a collection of chart suggestions and considerations for data visualization. It’s essentially a thought-starter designed to guide users in selecting the appropriate chart type based on various data characteristics and presentation goals. The document outlines a wide range of chart types, including circular area charts, line charts, column charts, scatter charts, 3D area charts, pie charts, waterfall charts, stacked charts (both 100% and stacked), bubble charts, column histograms, line histograms, and more. A key focus is on understanding the data's nature – specifically, the number of variables, the number of periods, the number of categories, and the overall data volume. The document prompts the user to consider whether the data is cyclical or non-cyclical, and whether it represents a single variable, multiple variables, or a combination of variables. It also addresses the need to compare data points, understand data distributions, and represent relationships between variables. The document emphasizes the importance of tailoring chart selection to the specific needs of the presentation, taking into account factors like the number of items being displayed, the granularity of the data (few periods vs. many periods), and the desired level of detail. The document’s purpose is to stimulate thinking about the best way to visually communicate data, offering a diverse set of options and prompting the user to critically assess their data and presentation objectives. It’s a practical guide for anyone involved in data analysis and presentation, encouraging a thoughtful approach to chart selection.
