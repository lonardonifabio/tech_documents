SPECIAL EUROBAROMETER 554 Artificial Intelligence and the future of work

Authors: European Commission, DGEMPL

This document, a special Eurobarometer report, investigates public perceptions and attitudes surrounding Artificial Intelligence (AI) and its impact on the future of work. Conducted at the request of the European Commission’s Directorate-General for Employment, Social Affairs and Inclusion (DGEMPL), the survey explores a range of topics including general perceptions of digital technologies, proficiency with digital skills, and attitudes towards AI and robots in the workplace. The report delves into how citizens view the current and future effects of digital technologies on employment, the economy, society, and quality of life. It specifically examines digital skills across various aspects of daily life and their connection to learning opportunities. Furthermore, the study analyzes public opinions regarding the integration of robots and AI within the labor market, assessing concerns and potential benefits. The findings are presented in a structured format, beginning with an introduction and key findings, followed by a detailed breakdown of perceptions across several categories. The report aims to provide valuable insights for policymakers and stakeholders interested in understanding public sentiment and preparing for the transformative changes brought about by AI and automation. The survey was coordinated by the European Commission’s Directorate-General for Communication, and the interpretations and opinions are solely those of the authors. The document is a comprehensive analysis of the evolving relationship between technology and the workforce, offering a snapshot of European citizens' views on this critical topic. The report’s findings are crucial for informing strategies related to digital skills development, workforce adaptation, and the responsible implementation of AI.
