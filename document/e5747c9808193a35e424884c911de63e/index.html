Governance of Artificial Intelligence in the Military Domain: A Multi-stakeholder Perspective on Priority Areas

Authors: Yasmin Afina, Giacomo Persi Paoli

This policy brief, produced by the United Nations Institute for Disarmament Research (UNIDIR)’s Security and Technology Programme, addresses the governance of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the military domain. It’s a crucial output supporting the 2024 Summit on Responsible AI in the Military Domain (REAIM), scheduled for September 9-10, 2024, in Seoul, Republic of Korea. The brief highlights the need for a multi-stakeholder approach to managing the complex ethical and security implications of AI’s increasing role in defense and security operations. It’s directly linked to UNIDIR’s RAISE Roundtable, also supported by the Republic of Korea and Microsoft, indicating a concerted effort to foster dialogue and develop practical solutions. The document emphasizes the importance of international collaboration, given the global nature of the challenges posed by AI in the military. The brief’s creation is underpinned by funding from a diverse group of governments – Czechia, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, the Republic of Korea, Switzerland, and the United Kingdom – alongside Microsoft, demonstrating a broad commitment to addressing this critical issue. UNIDIR itself is a vital institution, operating autonomously within the United Nations and specializing in disarmament research and promoting security solutions. The brief’s preparation reflects a strategic alignment with key international initiatives and a dedication to generating knowledge and driving action on disarmament and security challenges, particularly those related to the intersection of AI, security, and ethics. The document’s focus on the 2024 REAIM summit underscores the urgency and importance of establishing robust governance frameworks for AI in the military sector.
