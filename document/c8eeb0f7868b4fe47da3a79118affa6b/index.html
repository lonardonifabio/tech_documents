Data Science Cheat Sheet for Business Leaders

This document provides a concise overview and cheat sheet targeted at business leaders seeking to understand and implement data science initiatives within their organizations. It outlines key roles and responsibilities within a data science team, categorizing them based on their skillsets and reporting structures. The document highlights the importance of a well-structured data team, differentiating between various organizational models – centralized, isolated, embedded, and hybrid – to effectively leverage data for decision-making. It emphasizes the distinction between descriptive and predictive analytics, detailing how data science can be applied through Business Intelligence (BI) dashboards, reports, and emails for understanding past trends (e.g., customer churn analysis, sales trends by location) and through Machine Learning models for continuous prediction and proactive decision support (e.g., predicting customer churn, optimizing marketing strategies for property sales). The document stresses the need for a data team to own data and respond to requests from other teams, and the importance of data being a priority across the organization. It suggests that data science team members require different skills for different purposes, with roles including Data Engineers (responsible for data storage and maintenance using SQL, Java, Scala, and Python), Data Analysts (focused on writing production-level code for prediction using Python, Java, and R), Machine Learning Engineers (building custom models to drive business decisions), and Data Scientists (building custom models to drive business decisions). The document also touches upon the need to build a data science team with different skills for different purposes. The document serves as a foundational guide for business leaders looking to understand the core components of a data science operation and how it can be applied to solve business problems.
