Being Human in 2035 How Are We Changing in the Age of AI?

Authors: Janna Anderson, Lee Rainie

This research report, "Being Human in 2035 How Are We Changing in the Age of AI?", explores the anticipated transformations in human capabilities and behaviors as artificial intelligence becomes increasingly integrated into daily life. Authored by Janna Anderson and Lee Rainie, the document examines expert predictions regarding the impact of AI on fundamental human traits such as empathy, social-emotional intelligence, complex thinking, independent action, and a sense of purpose. The report acknowledges widespread concerns about the potential erosion of these qualities due to human reliance on AI systems over the next decade. However, it also considers optimistic viewpoints, suggesting that AI could positively influence human curiosity, decision-making processes, and creative endeavors. The research is produced by Elon University’s Imagining the Digital Future Center, a non-partisan research initiative established in 2000 (originally as "Imagining the Internet") and expanded in 2023. The center’s mission is to investigate and disseminate a diverse range of perspectives on the digital revolution’s future impact, informing policy discussions and promoting a positive future for humanity. The report highlights the center's ongoing efforts to gather and share insights through various research methods. The document’s publication date is April 2, 2025, indicating a forward-looking analysis of the evolving relationship between humans and AI. It underscores the importance of proactive consideration of the ethical and societal implications of AI development and deployment. The research aims to stimulate critical dialogue and shape informed decisions regarding the future of human-AI interaction. The report’s focus on expert opinions suggests a reliance on predictive analysis and a recognition of the uncertainty surrounding the long-term consequences of AI adoption.
