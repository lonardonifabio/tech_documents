A Guide to Using Artificial Intelligence in the Public Sector

This document provides a foundational guide for leaders and delivery leads within public sector organizations seeking to understand and implement artificial intelligence solutions. It’s designed to assist those responsible for technology selection and project management, offering a structured approach to evaluating AI’s suitability for addressing user needs. The core focus is on a pragmatic, adaptable methodology, emphasizing the importance of flexibility and continuous assessment. The guidance stresses that AI projects, like all technology initiatives, require the ability to adjust course as understanding of user requirements evolves. Specifically, it outlines key stages: initially assessing whether AI is the appropriate solution, followed by detailed planning and preparation for AI system implementation. This includes considerations for risk management and governance, crucial elements often overlooked in technology projects. The document highlights the need for effective project management, team building, and proactive risk mitigation. Furthermore, it acknowledges the critical role of ethics and safety considerations, referencing the Alan Turing Institute’s more detailed guidance on this subject. The intended audience includes individuals involved in technology selection, project planning, risk management, and governance within public sector organizations. The document promotes a responsible and adaptable approach to AI adoption, ensuring alignment with user needs and ethical standards. It’s a practical resource for navigating the complexities of integrating AI into public services, encouraging a thoughtful and iterative process.
