EU Artificial Intelligence Act: A Guide for Businesses

Authors: Matheson

This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the EU Artificial Intelligence Act (Regulation 2024/1689), a European Regulation designed to establish a legal framework for regulating AI across the entire European Union (EU). The Act’s immediate applicability extends to all 27 Member States, eliminating the need for individual national implementing legislation. However, the Irish Government is anticipated to introduce supplementary legislation, specifically concerning the appointment of regulators and their enforcement powers. The Act’s overarching goal is to foster the adoption of human-centric and trustworthy AI systems, prioritizing the protection of fundamental rights, including democracy, the rule of law, and environmental protection, against potential harms stemming from AI. Key elements of the AI Act include harmonized rules governing the placement on the market, deployment, and usage of AI systems within the EU. Furthermore, it establishes prohibitions for certain AI practices and imposes specific requirements on high-risk AI systems, placing obligations on their operators. The document highlights the significant impact of the AI Act on businesses operating in Europe and globally. It anticipates further development through delegated and implementing acts by the European Commission, alongside supporting documentation such as guidelines and codes of practice. The Act represents a crucial step in shaping the future of AI development and deployment within the EU, aiming to balance innovation with robust safeguards.
