GenAI vs. Agentic AI: Security Implications

Authors: Cloud Security Alliance

This document excerpt outlines the key distinctions between Generative AI (GenAI) systems and Agentic AI systems, focusing on their operational differences and, critically, the resulting security challenges. GenAI systems are characterized by their reliance on single-turn interactions; a user provides a prompt, and the model generates a response without any autonomous action or interaction with external systems. The primary security concerns associated with GenAI revolve around prompt injection attacks, the potential for data leakage through generated content, the creation of harmful or misleading information, and inherent biases present in the model's outputs. Conversely, Agentic AI systems represent a significant advancement, designed for sustained autonomous operation. These systems are capable of planning complex goals, reasoning within their environment, executing actions through external integrations (such as APIs, databases, and physical devices), orchestrating multiple actions, and learning and adapting their behavior based on feedback. This expanded functionality introduces new security vulnerabilities. Agentic AI systems can interact with and manipulate the real world, increasing the attack surface and potential for misuse. The document highlights the need for red teamers to adapt their strategies, leveraging existing knowledge while recognizing the necessity for novel approaches to address the unique security risks posed by Agentic AI. The core difference lies in the ability of Agentic AI to not just respond to prompts, but to actively engage and influence the environment, demanding a more proactive and comprehensive security posture. The Cloud Security Alliance (CSA) emphasizes the importance of understanding these differences to effectively manage security risks.
