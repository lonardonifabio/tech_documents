Data Science from Scratch

Authors: Steven Cooper

This comprehensive guide, "Data Science from Scratch," serves as a foundational resource for individuals seeking to enter the field of data science. It covers a broad range of essential topics, starting with the core concepts and progressing to practical implementation. The book begins with an introduction to data science, defining its importance and outlining the key differences between data science and big data. It then delves into the data science process, highlighting the responsibilities and qualifications of a data scientist, and emphasizes the critical role of coding and hacking. A significant portion is dedicated to the technical aspects, including a detailed exploration of programming languages such as Python, SQL, R, SAS, Java, Scala, and Julia. The book provides practical guidance on data cleaning, manipulation, and rescaling, utilizing popular libraries like Pandas. It then moves into the core of machine learning and analytics, covering linear algebra, statistical distributions, and various algorithms, including decision trees and neural networks. Furthermore, it addresses scalable data processing techniques, introducing systems like Apache Hadoop, Apache Storm, Apache Samza, Apache Spark, and Apache Flink. The book emphasizes the importance of testing and accuracy analysis for data science models. Finally, it concludes with a summary of the key concepts and provides a section on the author and references. The book's focus on a 'from scratch' approach makes it suitable for beginners while still providing sufficient depth for those seeking a solid understanding of the field. The inclusion of diverse programming languages and processing systems reflects the evolving landscape of data science. The copyright date of 2018 indicates the publication date of this resource.
