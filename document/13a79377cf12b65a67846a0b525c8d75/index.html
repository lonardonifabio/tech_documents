Insights, Big Data and the Future of Sustainability

Authors: Amanda Jaeger

This document explores the growing importance of sustainability within corporations and the role of Consumer and Market Insights (CMI) functions in driving data-driven decisions. It highlights a significant shift where sustainability is no longer just a PR initiative but a core business strategy, fueled by increasing consumer concern for the environment. The analysis draws upon data from the Ipsos Global Trends Survey 2019 & 2020, Think with Google, and an EDF & Ipsos study conducted in 2020. Specifically, the document emphasizes that a substantial majority (68%) of consumers globally believe corporations have a responsibility to address climate change. The research indicates that concern for the environment is at an all-time high, persisting even amidst the COVID-19 pandemic and subsequent economic downturn. Google search data corroborates this trend, revealing a 92% increase in searches related to carbon neutrality within the UK over a one-year period. The document underscores the need for businesses to leverage data, particularly through CMI functions, to identify effective sustainability efforts and ultimately achieve their environmental goals. The analysis suggests a strong correlation between consumer sentiment and corporate action regarding sustainability, driven by a global awareness of the climate emergency. The document implicitly argues for a strategic approach to sustainability, grounded in data and consumer understanding, as a critical component of modern business operations. It positions insights as a key driver in navigating this evolving landscape.
