Quick Reference Guide for Uncertainty in Models

This document serves as a concise reference guide focusing on quantifying and managing uncertainty within machine learning models. It primarily addresses strategies for understanding and mitigating uncertainty, a critical aspect of model reliability and performance, particularly in real-world applications. The guide outlines several key approaches, including recruiting and managing annotators (both in-house, outsourced, crowdsourced, and volunteers) to assess model confidence. It delves into various methods for calculating uncertainty, such as Softmax, Temperature, Least Confidence, Margin of Confidence, and Ratio of Confidence, alongside concepts like Entropy and Ensembles of Models. A significant portion of the document is dedicated to managing annotation quality, emphasizing the importance of ground truth data and employing techniques like Krippendorff's alpha to assess dataset reliability. The guide also tackles the issue of model-based outliers and the need for representative sampling, incorporating strategies like Cluster-based Sampling and Representative Sampling. Furthermore, it provides guidance on aggregating annotations and utilizes active learning strategies, including combining Uncertainty Sampling and Diversity Sampling. The document highlights the importance of expert review and multistep workflows for quality control, ultimately aiming to equip users with the knowledge to effectively address and reduce expected error in their models, especially when dealing with real-world diversity and diverse datasets. The reference guide is a practical tool for anyone involved in developing or deploying machine learning models where understanding and controlling uncertainty is paramount.
