A guide to Artificial Intelligence at the workplace Your rights on algorithms

Authors: Franca Salis Madinier, Cécile Dejoux, Laurent Berger

This document, titled ‘A guide to Artificial Intelligence at the workplace Your rights on algorithms,’ produced by the European Economic and Social Committee, presents a comprehensive overview of the burgeoning field of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and its implications, particularly within the context of the workplace. The report addresses critical global challenges and opportunities surrounding AI’s rapid growth. It begins with a call for a robust social debate concerning the deployment of AI, emphasizing the need for ethical considerations and societal impact assessments. The core of the document is structured around ‘Part I: Global Context and challenges on artificial intelligence,’ which examines the significant rise in the global AI market, highlighting the intense international competition driving innovation. A key concern addressed is the data landscape, specifically focusing on data storage and the importance of European autonomy in managing and controlling data. The report underscores the need for European nations to maintain control over their data assets to avoid being dominated by global tech giants. Furthermore, the document implicitly advocates for a proactive approach to AI regulation and governance, recognizing the potential risks associated with unchecked technological advancement. The guide’s purpose is to inform stakeholders – including policymakers, businesses, and workers – about the current state of AI, the associated challenges, and the importance of shaping its development in a way that benefits society as a whole. The report’s existence signals a growing awareness of the need for a social dialogue surrounding AI, aiming to ensure that its implementation aligns with European values and priorities. It’s a foundational resource for understanding the complexities of AI’s impact on the workplace and the broader societal implications.
