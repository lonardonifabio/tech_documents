DMA versus EU AI Act: A Mapping Exercise

Authors: Sean Musch, Michael Borrelli, Charles Kerrigan, Priya Guliani, Robert Lienhard, Michael Tchuindjang, Signature S.A.S.

This report, published by AI & Partners, provides a mapping exercise comparing the Digital Markets Act (DMA) and the EU AI Act. The core mission of AI & Partners is to defend and extend the digital rights of users globally, particularly those considered at risk, through a multifaceted approach encompassing technical support, policy engagement, advocacy, professional services, regulatory interventions, and participation in industry groups like AI Commons. The document specifically addresses key questions surrounding the DMA, including its primary purpose and the criteria for identifying ‘gatekeepers’ – entities subject to the DMA’s regulations. It delves into the definition of ‘core platform services’ within the context of the DMA. The report highlights AI & Partners’ commitment to safeguarding digital rights in the age of artificial intelligence, emphasizing their role in advocating for fundamental rights and influencing regulatory landscapes. The publication details the organization's strategy for addressing concerns related to the DMA, aiming to provide a comparative analysis for stakeholders navigating the evolving regulatory environment surrounding AI and digital markets. The report’s creation involved a collaborative effort, with multiple contributors from AI & Partners, demonstrating a comprehensive approach to understanding and responding to the implications of both the DMA and the EU AI Act. It serves as a valuable resource for anyone seeking to understand the interplay between these two significant pieces of legislation and AI & Partners’ stance on digital rights.
