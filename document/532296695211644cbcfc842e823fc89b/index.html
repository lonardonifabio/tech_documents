Digital Transformation Agency AI Model Clauses Version 2.0

Authors: Digital Transformation Agency

This document, Version 2.0, produced by the Digital Transformation Agency of Australia, outlines key clauses related to Artificial Intelligence (AI) usage. It serves as a framework for agreements, specifically addressing the use of AI by sellers. The core principle established is that any seller's utilization of AI requires prior approval from the buyer. Furthermore, the document emphasizes the seller's responsibility to verify the accuracy of the AI model being deployed. The Digital Transformation Agency acknowledges potential inaccuracies and advises against relying solely on this document for critical commercial decisions. The agency is dedicated to ensuring accessibility of its information, offering support via email at communications@dta.gov.au. This document represents a foundational element within the agency's broader efforts to govern and manage AI applications, aligning with broader government initiatives focused on responsible technology adoption. The inclusion of a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International Licence indicates an openness to sharing information while retaining attribution rights. The document's focus on approval and accuracy highlights a cautious approach to AI implementation, prioritizing due diligence and risk mitigation. It’s a foundational document within the DTA’s framework for managing AI-related agreements and processes. The version number (1801) and the explicit disclaimer regarding potential inaccuracies underscore the importance of ongoing review and validation. The document’s purpose is to provide a standardized approach to AI usage, promoting clarity and accountability within agreements.
