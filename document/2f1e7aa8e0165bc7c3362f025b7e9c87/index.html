Superintelligence Strategy: Expert Version

Authors: Dan Hendrycks, Eric Schmidt, Alexandr Wang

This document outlines the urgent need for a strategic approach to navigating the potential risks and opportunities presented by rapidly advancing artificial intelligence, particularly the emergence of superintelligence. It argues that the current trajectory of AI development, characterized by competitive advancements and the potential for destabilizing proliferation, mirrors the dynamics of nuclear deterrence. The core concept introduced is Mutual Assured AI Malfunction (MAIM), a strategy analogous to Mutual Assured Destruction (MAD) designed to deter unilateral AI dominance. MAIM proposes that any state attempting to achieve AI supremacy will face preemptive sabotage from rival nations, utilizing methods ranging from cyberattacks to physical destruction of data centers. The document emphasizes the importance of states bolstering their economies and militaries through AI investment, alongside proactive nonproliferation efforts to prevent weaponizable AI capabilities from falling into the hands of rogue actors. It posits that the strategic landscape for AI superpowers is already defined by this inherent risk of sabotage, creating a new era of competition and potential conflict. The document highlights the need for a coordinated global response to manage the risks associated with superintelligence, recognizing that the potential consequences of unchecked AI development could be catastrophic. The authors advocate for a proactive, deterrence-based strategy to mitigate these risks and ensure a more stable and secure future in an age of increasingly powerful artificial intelligence. The document’s central argument is that a failure to develop a coherent superintelligence strategy will leave nations vulnerable to destabilizing AI developments and increase the likelihood of great-power conflict.
