Hands-On Large Language Models

Authors: Jay, Maarten

This book, "Hands-On Large Language Models," is a highly recommended guide designed to provide readers with a comprehensive understanding of large language models (LLMs) and their practical applications within the industry. Authored by Jay and Maarten, the book emphasizes a visually-rich approach, utilizing illustrations and clear examples to simplify complex concepts. It covers a wide range of topics, including generative, representational, and retrieval applications of LLMs, alongside key techniques like transformer models, tokenizers, semantic search, and RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation). The book is bolstered by working code, timelines, and references to pivotal research papers, making it a valuable resource for both beginners and those seeking to deepen their knowledge. The authors' established tradition of providing insightful descriptions of complex topics is evident in this new release. It's praised for its ability to quickly empower readers to understand, utilize, and refine LLMs. The book's structure takes the reader from a foundational "zero" understanding to a proficient level of expertise, encompassing the history and latest advancements in the field. The inclusion of code labs and real-life examples further enhances the learning experience. The book's significance is underscored by endorsements from prominent figures in the AI community, including Nils Reimers, Andrew Ng, and Josh Starmer, who highlight its importance in navigating the current landscape of language models and achieving success in this rapidly evolving domain. The book effectively demystifies the intricacies of LLMs, offering a practical and accessible pathway to mastery.
