This comprehensive handbook, ‘The Cambridge Handbook of THE LAW, ETHICS AND POLICY OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE,’ offers a detailed exploration of the legal, ethical, and policy considerations surrounding artificial intelligence (AI) and algorithmic systems. Primarily focused on the European context, the book addresses the increasingly significant impact of these technologies across diverse sectors of society. It delves into the complex challenges and opportunities presented by AI, examining issues such as bias in algorithms, data privacy, accountability, and the potential for misuse. The handbook draws upon contributions from experts in various disciplines – including law, ethics, computer science, and policy – to provide a multi-faceted understanding of the subject. It’s designed to equip readers with the knowledge necessary to critically assess the advancements in AI and to contribute to informed discussions and decision-making regarding its development and deployment. The book emphasizes the need for proactive measures to mitigate potential risks and to ensure that AI systems are developed and used responsibly, aligning with ethical principles and legal frameworks. The content includes theoretical insights alongside practical examples, aiming to bridge the gap between academic research and real-world applications. The scope of the book extends beyond simply identifying problems; it seeks to foster a deeper understanding of the intricate relationships between technology, law, and society, particularly within the European Union’s regulatory landscape. Ultimately, the handbook serves as a valuable resource for policymakers, researchers, legal professionals, and anyone interested in navigating the rapidly evolving world of artificial intelligence.
