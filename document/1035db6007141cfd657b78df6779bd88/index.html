AI Act Simplification: For Innovation and Feasibility

Authors: Bitkom

This document outlines Bitkom’s recommendations for simplifying the EU’s AI Act, driven by concerns that the current framework presents significant implementation challenges and unclear requirements, potentially hindering AI innovation in Europe. The core argument is that the initial design of the AI Act is overly complex and risks stifling the very innovation it intends to promote. Bitkom advocates for a more pragmatic approach, focusing on feasibility and aligning the AI Act with existing sectoral regulations. Specifically, the recommendations center around a phased implementation strategy. The primary suggestion is to integrate all high-risk requirements currently detailed in Annex I A into specific sectoral legislation. This would effectively reduce the burden on AI developers and businesses operating within those sectors. Bitkom proposes streamlining Annex I by consolidating its two sections into a single, more flexible framework. The ‘Section B’ approach, which allows for a controlled adaptation of existing regulations to align with the AI Act, should be broadened to encompass the entire Annex. This signifies a move towards a ‘maximum harmonization’ principle, ensuring that any adjustments to sectoral regulations remain within the scope of the AI Act’s requirements. Furthermore, Bitkom strongly recommends postponing the entry into application of high-risk requirements by at least 24 months. This extended timeline is deemed crucial for ensuring the development and implementation of high-quality standards and for providing businesses with sufficient time to adapt to the new regulations. The document clearly positions Bitkom as a supporter of the EU Commission’s competitiveness agenda, emphasizing the need for a practical and adaptable AI regulatory framework. The overall goal is to balance risk mitigation with the encouragement of innovation, recognizing that overly restrictive regulations can have detrimental effects on the European AI landscape. The document’s tone is proactive and solution-oriented, aiming to constructively contribute to the ongoing discussions surrounding the AI Act’s implementation.
