EU Role in the Global Quantum Race

Authors: Unknown, Unknown

This policy brief examines the European Union's role within the burgeoning global quantum technology landscape. The document highlights the significant presence of quantum companies worldwide, with the EU accounting for a substantial 32% of the global total, comprised of over 440 companies. Notably, EU firms are characterized by their smaller size and relative youth compared to companies in other jurisdictions. Financial investment in the quantum sector has been substantial, reaching €9.6 billion between 2012 and 2024, with €6 billion from private venture capital and €36 billion from the public sector. Private investment predominantly comes from domestic, later-stage venture capital, directed towards scaling up existing businesses with a cross-border focus. Despite a slowdown in global patenting activity starting in 2021, the EU has demonstrated a positive and stable growth trajectory in this area. A key observation is the dominance of China in quantum patenting (46%), followed by the United States (23%) and Japan (6%), with the EU holding a smaller share (6%). The brief provides an overview of the quantum sector, specifically focusing on quantum computing, quantum communication (including quantum key distribution and post-quantum cryptography), and quantum sensing. It analyzes the geographical distribution of quantum companies, their investment patterns, and their engagement with intellectual property protection. The document underscores the dynamic and promising nature of quantum technologies as a whole, presenting a snapshot of the current state of the field. The analysis emphasizes the need for continued investment and innovation within the EU to maintain a competitive position in this rapidly evolving technological domain.
