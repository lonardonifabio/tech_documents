European Union Artificial Intelligence Act: a guide

This document provides a guide to the European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act, a landmark piece of legislation designed to regulate the development and deployment of AI systems within the EU. The Act focuses primarily on mitigating risks associated with AI, particularly those posed by general-purpose AI models and high-risk AI systems. The guide outlines key concepts, including definitions of general-purpose AI models and high-risk AI systems, and details the obligations placed upon providers of these systems. A core element of the Act is the classification of AI systems based on their potential risk, with high-risk systems subject to stringent transparency, accountability, and governance requirements. The document details the timeline for implementation, including application deadlines for Delegated Acts and Implementing Acts, and the role of Commission Guidelines and Codes of conduct and practice. It addresses transparency obligations, emphasizing the need for clear information and accessible explanations regarding AI systems. Furthermore, it covers the establishment of regulatory sandboxes for real-world testing, and the enforcement mechanisms, including market surveillance authorities, procedures for enforcement, and potential penalties. The guide also delves into the governance structure of the Act, highlighting the importance of compliance and accountability. Finally, it addresses the scope of the Act, including material and territorial scope, exclusions, and its relationship with other regulatory frameworks. The document serves as a foundational resource for understanding the EU's approach to AI regulation, aiming to foster innovation while safeguarding fundamental rights and values. The Act's overarching goal is to create a trustworthy and ethical AI ecosystem within the European Union.
