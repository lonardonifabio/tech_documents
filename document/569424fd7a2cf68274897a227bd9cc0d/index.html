AI Governance by Design for Agentic Systems: A Framework for Responsible Development and Deployment

This concept paper, authored by Himanshu Joshi and posted on Preprints.org, investigates the emerging field of AI Governance by Design (AIGD) specifically within the context of agentic AI systems. The document proposes a proactive methodology for developing and deploying AI systems, emphasizing the integration of ethical, legal, and societal considerations from the very beginning of the development lifecycle. It argues that traditional reactive approaches to AI governance are insufficient, necessitating a shift towards embedding these considerations directly into the design process. The paper analyzes existing frameworks and organizational practices to identify core principles guiding AIGD, outlining practical implementation strategies, and acknowledging the significant challenges organizations encounter when adopting this approach. A key focus is on agentic AI systems – systems designed to operate autonomously and make decisions, raising unique governance concerns. The research delves into the complexities of ensuring accountability, transparency, and fairness in these systems. The paper’s findings contribute to the growing body of work on Responsible AI, aiming to provide a roadmap for organizations seeking to build trustworthy and ethically aligned agentic AI systems. The document highlights the importance of anticipating potential risks and proactively addressing them through robust governance mechanisms. It’s a foundational exploration of a critical area within the broader field of AI governance, offering valuable insights for researchers, developers, and policymakers alike.
