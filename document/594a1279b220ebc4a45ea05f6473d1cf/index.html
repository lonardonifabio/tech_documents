Generative AI and the Nature of Work

Authors: Manuel Hoffmann, Sam Boysel, Frank Nagle, Sida Peng, Kevin Xu

This working paper, titled "Generative AI and the Nature of Work," explores the potential impact of recent advancements in generative AI technology on the nature of work, particularly within the knowledge economy. The research focuses on how individuals adapt their work practices when utilizing AI tools, specifically examining the effects within the context of open-source software development. The authors, affiliated with Harvard Business School, Microsoft Corporation, and GitHub Inc., investigate individual-level adjustments to work processes. The paper acknowledges the growing body of literature surrounding AI's productivity effects but highlights a relative lack of research concerning the fundamental changes AI might bring to how people work. The study utilizes the open-source software environment as a case study to analyze these changes. It addresses questions such as how individuals modify their workflows and skillsets when AI tools become integrated into their daily tasks. The working paper is presented in draft form and is intended for comment and discussion purposes, with a copyright held by the authors. Funding for the research was partially provided by Harvard Business School. The document details the authors' affiliations and the date of the current version (April 18, 2025), emphasizing the ongoing nature of the research and the need for further investigation into the evolving relationship between AI and human labor. The core argument centers on the need to understand the behavioral shifts and adaptations that arise when AI becomes a prevalent tool in knowledge-based work environments.
