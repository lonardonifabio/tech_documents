A Guide to Using Artificial Intelligence in the Public Sector

This document provides a foundational guide for leaders and delivery leads within public sector organizations seeking to understand and implement artificial intelligence solutions. It’s designed to assist those responsible for technology selection and project management, offering a structured approach to evaluating AI’s suitability and ensuring responsible implementation. The core focus is on assessing whether AI is the appropriate solution for meeting user needs, emphasizing adaptability and the ability to adjust strategies as understanding evolves – a critical element often overlooked in technology projects. The document outlines key steps for planning and preparing for AI system implementation, covering aspects such as risk management and governance. Specifically, it targets individuals involved in decision-making, team building, and project execution. A significant portion is dedicated to understanding AI ethics and safety, referencing the Alan Turing Institute’s detailed guidance for a comprehensive perspective. The document stresses the importance of proactive risk management and governance throughout the AI system implementation lifecycle. It’s not a prescriptive technical manual, but rather a strategic framework to guide public sector organizations in navigating the complexities of adopting AI, ensuring alignment with ethical considerations and a flexible approach to technological change. The guidance is intended to support those responsible for technology selection, project management, and governance within public sector organizations, promoting responsible and effective AI adoption.
