A Comprehensive Guide to Machine Learning

Authors: Soroush Nasiriany, Garrett Thomas, William Wang, Alex Yang

This document serves as a comprehensive guide to Machine Learning, specifically designed for the CS 189 course at UC Berkeley. Created by Soroush Nasiriany and Garrett Thomas initially in Fall 2017, with contributions from William Wang and Alex Yang, the guide aims to share knowledge with students and the broader public, potentially attracting interest from other universities. The guide is heavily influenced by lectures from Professors Anant Sahai, Stella Yu, and Jennifer Listgarten, and draws inspiration from Jonathan Shewchuk's machine learning notes. It provides definitions and notation related to machine learning, including definitions of 'R' as the set of real numbers and 'Rn' as a vector space. The document includes explanations of key concepts and mathematical notation, such as the Kronecker delta and the gradient and Hessian of a function. The authors encourage reporting of errors and provide contact information for redistribution purposes. The guide's availability is linked at http://www.eecs189.org/ and http://snasiriany.me/cs189/. It represents a valuable resource for understanding fundamental machine learning concepts and techniques.
