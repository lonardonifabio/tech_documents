Secure AI Framework

Authors: Shamik Chaudhuri, Kingshuk Dasgupta, Isaac Hepworth, Michael Le, Mark Lodato, Mihai Maruseac, Sarah Meiklejohn, Tehila Minkus, Kara Olive

This document, "Secure AI Framework," addresses the escalating security challenges associated with integrating AI-powered features into software applications. It highlights the accelerated threat landscape driven by the increasing prevalence of AI and the significant investment required to develop and maintain AI models. The core focus is on securing the AI software supply chain, recognizing that traditional software security approaches are insufficient due to the unique risks and complexities introduced by AI. The authors propose an approach leveraging existing supply chain security solutions, specifically Binary Authorization for Borg (BAB), Supply-chain Levels for Software Artifacts (SLSA), and next-generation cryptographic signing solutions via Sigstore. These tools, previously developed for traditional software, are adapted to the AI context, aiming to provide a practical and efficient solution. The paper emphasizes that a tailored approach is necessary, dependent on an organization's specific internal processes and platforms. The document begins with a broad overview of development lifecycles for both traditional and AI software, setting the stage for the subsequent discussion of AI supply chain security best practices. The overarching goal is to provide guidance and actionable insights for organizations seeking to proactively mitigate risks and ensure the integrity of their AI-driven software products.
