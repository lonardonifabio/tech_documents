AI Risk Frameworks MIT AI Risk Repository

Authors: Critch & Russell, Various (Multiple Authors)

The MIT AI Risk Repository, updated as of January 2025, presents a comprehensive overview of existing frameworks for understanding and categorizing AI risks. This slide deck synthesizes information from 43 documents, primarily focusing on taxonomies and classifications of AI risks developed by researchers and industry experts. The repository was created through a systematic search for relevant literature, prioritizing documents that offer structured approaches to risk assessment. Specifically, the selection criteria included a focus on presenting a taxonomy, coverage of risks across diverse locations and industries, the proposition of an original framework, and a peer-reviewed status (journal papers, preprints, conference papers, or industry reports). The slide deck serves as a valuable resource for researchers, policymakers, and anyone seeking to understand the current landscape of AI risk frameworks. It highlights the variety of ways in which risks are conceptualized and categorized, identifying common themes and gaps in our knowledge. The document emphasizes the need for a holistic understanding of AI risks, providing a starting point for further investigation and collaboration. The included document, 'TASRA: a Taxonomy and Analysis of Societal-Scale Risks from AI' by Critch & Russell, 2023, is a key component of this repository, offering a detailed examination of societal-scale AI risks. The repository’s goal is to facilitate a more informed and coordinated approach to managing the potential challenges posed by rapidly advancing artificial intelligence. The slide deck is designed to be a dynamic resource, allowing users to bookmark relevant frameworks for future reference and contributing to a growing body of knowledge on this critical topic.
