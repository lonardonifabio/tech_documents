Approaches to Responsible Governance of GenAI in Organizations

Authors: Himanshu Joshi, Shabnam Hassani, Dhari Gandhi, Lucas Hartman

This white paper addresses the critical need for responsible governance frameworks surrounding the rapidly expanding use of Generative AI (GenAI) across various industries. Recognizing the significant opportunities GenAI presents for innovation, the document simultaneously acknowledges the complex ethical, accountability, and societal implications that accompany its integration. The research is based on a comprehensive approach, combining a thorough literature review with established governance frameworks, and crucially, insights derived from weekly industry roundtable discussions involving a diverse group of experts from different organizations and professional backgrounds. These discussions provided invaluable practical knowledge, directly shaping the paper's recommendations to ensure they are firmly rooted in real-world challenges and experiences. The core objective of the paper is to furnish organizations with actionable guidance for implementing a balanced, risk-based governance strategy. This strategy seeks to strike a delicate equilibrium between fostering innovation and maintaining effective oversight. The document identifies key principles for integrating responsible GenAI governance, considering the varied structures of different organizations. The research emphasizes a practical, risk-assessed approach, acknowledging that a one-size-fits-all solution is unlikely. The paper’s findings are intended to empower organizations to proactively manage the risks and maximize the benefits of GenAI while upholding ethical standards and societal responsibility. The document’s methodology, combining academic research with direct expert input, strengthens its credibility and relevance for practitioners and policymakers alike.
