Artificial intelligence & democratic values 2025 (CAIDP)

Authors: Center for AI and Digital Policy

This document, produced by the Center for AI and Digital Policy (CAIDP), offers insights into the relationship between artificial intelligence and democratic values. It highlights four key areas of concern and opportunity. Firstly, it emphasizes the crucial role of public awareness and advocacy, asserting that citizens, policymakers, and businesses must actively push for ethical AI governance to safeguard democratic principles. Secondly, the document identifies regulatory gaps as a significant risk, particularly for nations with weak AI policies, which can exacerbate inequalities, facilitate disinformation campaigns, and allow for unchecked AI usage by corporations and governments. Thirdly, it underscores the importance of transparency and accountability in AI governance, arguing that clear frameworks are essential for building public trust and bolstering democratic resilience, while a lack of transparency can lead to misuse and distrust. Finally, the document explores the dual nature of AI ‚Äì it can either strengthen or undermine rights, depending on how it is regulated. When implemented ethically, AI can enhance freedoms, particularly through initiatives like improving access to justice via AI-powered legal tools and combating disinformation through AI-driven fact-checking. The document implicitly calls for a proactive and rights-based approach to AI development and deployment to ensure that AI serves to strengthen, rather than erode, democratic values and fundamental human rights. The core message is a call to action for stakeholders to prioritize ethical considerations and robust governance mechanisms in the rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence.
