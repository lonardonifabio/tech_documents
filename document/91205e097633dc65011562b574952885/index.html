Expert Report on Privacy and Data Protection Risks in Large Language Models (LLMs)

Authors: Isabel Barberá, Murielle Popa -Fabre

This expert report, prepared for the 48th Plenary meeting of the Convention for the Protection of Individuals with Regard to Automatic Processing of Personal Data (Convenio 108), delves into the significant privacy and data protection risks associated with Large Language Models (LLMs). The document provides a foundational analysis of the LLM landscape, outlining the technical aspects of how LLMs represent and process data, particularly focusing on the ‘seeing’ and compression of words within these models. It examines the neural network architecture that underpins this representation, highlighting the potential for personal data extraction. The report systematically maps these risks across the entire AI lifecycle, from development to deployment, and distinguishes between risks inherent in the LLM models themselves and those arising from the broader systems built around them. Specifically, it addresses the latest technological evolutions in LLMs and their associated privacy vulnerabilities. A key focus is on the methods used to extract personal data from LLMs, and the report considers the implications of these techniques. Furthermore, it analyzes the risks associated with the model versus the system, providing a comprehensive overview of the challenges. The document is intended to inform the Convention’s work on protecting individuals’ rights in the context of increasingly sophisticated AI technologies. The report’s opinions are presented as the authors’ responsibility and do not necessarily represent official Council of Europe policy. The document’s scope includes a detailed examination of the technical underpinnings of LLMs and the potential for privacy breaches within this rapidly evolving field.
