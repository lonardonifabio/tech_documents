Rules of Machine Learning: Best Practices for ML Engineering

Authors: Martin Zinkevich

This document provides a collection of best practices for machine learning engineering, drawing from Google's internal guidelines and aiming to assist those with a foundational understanding of machine learning. It’s structured as a series of rules, offering practical advice for various stages of the ML lifecycle, from initial product launches to ongoing monitoring and maintenance. The core philosophy emphasizes a pragmatic approach, prioritizing simplicity, observability, and clear metrics. The document highlights the importance of early infrastructure investment, advocating for a straightforward first model to establish a robust foundation. Key recommendations include avoiding over-complex objectives, focusing on easily measurable metrics, and proactively addressing potential issues through monitoring and data validation. Specifically, it stresses the need to design metrics and implementation as a priority, and to test infrastructure independently. The document also emphasizes the importance of data integrity, particularly regarding dropped data during pipeline copying, and advocates for assigning ownership and providing documentation for feature sets. Furthermore, it cautions against silent failures and encourages a proactive approach to problem detection. The document’s style mirrors that of the Google C++ Style Guide, promoting a consistent and well-structured approach to ML engineering. It’s a concise guide intended to accelerate the development and deployment of effective machine learning systems by focusing on practical, actionable rules.
