MLOps: An Overview

Authors: Deep Learning Italia, eLearning DLI

This document provides an introductory explanation of MLOps (Machine Learning Operations). MLOps is defined as a set of practices designed to reliably and efficiently deploy and maintain machine learning models in production. It combines the principles of DevOps with the specific needs of machine learning development. The core concept revolves around bridging the gap between the experimental, often isolated, environments where machine learning models are initially developed and the operational realities of deploying and maintaining them in production. The document highlights that while academic research often focuses on the initial stages of a machine learning project ‚Äì data preparation and model selection ‚Äì the subsequent phases of the lifecycle, particularly those related to production deployment and ongoing maintenance, are frequently overlooked. This neglect can significantly impact the success of machine learning models when applied in industrial settings. MLOps addresses this by emphasizing collaboration between data scientists, DevOps engineers, and machine learning engineers to ensure a smooth transition of models from development to production. Similar to DevOps or DataOps, MLOps aims to increase automation and improve the quality of production models, while also considering business and regulatory requirements. The document implicitly suggests that a robust MLOps strategy is crucial for organizations seeking to realize the full potential of their machine learning investments by ensuring models are not only accurate but also reliably operationalized and maintained over time. The focus on collaboration and automation reflects a shift towards a more mature and scalable approach to machine learning deployment.
