Belgium's Approach to AI Governance and the AI Act

Authors: Belgium Government, EU Commission (implied)

This document outlines Belgium’s evolving strategy for governing Artificial Intelligence, primarily in the context of the EU AI Act. The Belgian government anticipates a multi-faceted approach, involving sector-specific regulators and the Belgian Data Protection Authority (DPA) in overseeing AI, particularly regarding personal data and automated decision-making. Belgium views AI as a key driver for digital transformation and public benefit, with significant adoption already occurring within large enterprises and growing interest from the public sector, particularly concerning ethical considerations. The country’s governance framework will closely align with EU law, specifically the AI Act, adopting a risk-based regulatory model. Despite lacking a formal legal definition of AI within national legislation, Belgium is leveraging existing frameworks such as the GDPR, product liability laws, and consumer protection regulations to guide AI deployment. Crucially, the AI4Belgium initiative and the Convergence Plan are deeply rooted in ethical principles – fairness, accountability, and proportionality – promoting a cautious and inclusive approach to AI development. The document highlights the importance of transparency and human-centric design in AI systems. Belgium’s strategy emphasizes clear institutional roles and ongoing adaptation to evolving EU regulations. The convergence of national and EU legal frameworks is central to Belgium’s ambition to foster innovation while maintaining robust safeguards and accountability. The document acknowledges the need for continuous monitoring and adjustment as AI technology advances and new legal developments emerge. The anticipated updates to EU product liability directives further contribute to the regulatory landscape surrounding AI systems. Ultimately, Belgium aims to establish a balanced ecosystem where AI can contribute to societal progress while upholding fundamental rights and values.
