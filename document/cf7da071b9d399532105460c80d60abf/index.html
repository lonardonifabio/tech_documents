This document excerpt details "Practical Guide to Logistic Regression", a book focused on providing a foundational understanding and practical application of the logistic regression model. The guide covers the core principles of the model, demonstrating its versatility across a wide range of fields. The book emphasizes the importance of understanding coefficients, odds ratios, and standard errors, alongside the ability to predict probabilities. A key component of the guide is the inclusion of real-world data examples, primarily drawn from health outcomes research, to illustrate the model's application. The book also incorporates Bayesian logistic regression modeling. To facilitate learning and application, the guide utilizes R statistical software for displaying the statistical models, and provides supplementary SAS and Stata code examples for all the presented illustrations. Ultimately, the document serves as a practical resource for analysts seeking to leverage logistic regression for data analysis, prediction, and classification tasks, particularly within domains such as healthcare, business analytics, and various scientific disciplines. The focus is on building a solid understanding of the model's mechanics and its appropriate use in diverse contexts.
