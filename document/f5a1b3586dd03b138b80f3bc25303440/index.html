Digital NSW 2024: NSW AI Assessment Framework

Authors: Digital NSW, Ministers of NSW

This document outlines the NSW Government’s commitment to the safe and responsible implementation of Artificial Intelligence (AI). Released in 2024, the NSW AI Assessment Framework represents an update to a pioneering initiative, designed to address evolving risks and opportunities associated with AI. The core principle driving this framework is the need for a responsible and ethical approach to AI deployment within government services. The framework emphasizes transparency and aims to build public trust by establishing appropriate guardrails for the design, development, and utilization of AI systems. Specifically, the government is leveraging AI across various sectors to improve community safety, environmental monitoring, and educational outcomes. Examples include utilizing AI for bushfire intelligence, monitoring environmental health through smart sensors, and enhancing education through services like NSW EduChat. The framework’s release on 1 July 2024 signifies a proactive stance in navigating the complexities of AI, ensuring alignment with community expectations and upholding the highest ethical standards. The document highlights that the use of AI within the NSW Government is not a novel concept, but rather a strategic investment in leveraging technology to address critical challenges and improve public services. The ongoing development and refinement of the AI Assessment Framework reflects a dedication to continuous improvement and adaptation in the face of rapidly advancing AI capabilities and potential risks. The document underscores the importance of a balanced approach, recognizing both the transformative potential of AI and the need for robust safeguards to mitigate potential negative consequences. Ultimately, the goal is to harness AI’s power to create a safer, more productive, and more efficient NSW for all citizens.
