This document excerpt focuses on the key provisions of Article 4 of the AI Act, specifically addressing the requirement for providers and deployers of AI systems to ensure a sufficient level of AI literacy among their staff and other individuals involved in the operation and use of these systems. It clarifies the definition of 'AI literacy' as outlined in Article 3(56) of the AI Act, emphasizing that it encompasses skills, knowledge, and understanding necessary for informed deployment, awareness of opportunities and risks, and mitigation of potential harm. The document highlights that this literacy requirement applies to anyone within an organization directly interacting with AI systems, considering their technical expertise, experience, education, and the specific context of AI system usage. The concept of 'other persons' is defined as anyone dealing with the operation and use of AI systems on behalf of the provider or deployer. The core purpose of Article 4 is to guarantee that all stakeholders possess the necessary competencies to responsibly utilize AI, aligning with the broader goals of the AI Act.
