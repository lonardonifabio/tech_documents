Transfer Learning and Fine-Tuning in Machine Learning

Authors: Avi Chawla

This document excerpt discusses the techniques of transfer learning and fine-tuning within the context of machine learning. It highlights a common scenario where a model is initially trained on a related task with abundant data, and then adapted for a task with limited data. The core concept involves training a base neural network model on the related task, followed by adjusting the final layers to capture task-specific behavior. This approach leverages the pre-existing knowledge learned during the initial training phase. Specifically, the excerpt details a method where the last few layers of the base model are replaced with new layers, and the network is retrained on the task of interest without updating the weights of the original layers. This is particularly effective when the target task has less data. The document also introduces the concept of knowledge distillation, which involves transferring knowledge from a larger, pre-trained model to a smaller one. Fine-tuning is presented as a related technique where the weights of the entire pre-trained model are adjusted to better suit the new task, typically without replacing the final layers. The excerpt emphasizes the strategic use of pre-trained models to accelerate learning and improve performance, especially in scenarios with limited data.
