A Guide for Ethical Data Science

Authors: Royal Statistical Society (RSS), Institute and Faculty of Actuaries (IFoA)

This document, published in August 2019, serves as a collaborative guide for ethical considerations within the field of data science. Developed by the Royal Statistical Society (RSS) and the Institute and Faculty of Actuaries (IFoA), it outlines key principles designed to ensure data science benefits society while minimizing potential harm. The guide focuses on five core themes: firstly, striving to enhance the value of data science for societal good; secondly, actively avoiding any potential harm resulting from data science applications; thirdly, maintaining and applying professional competence throughout the data science lifecycle; fourthly, prioritizing the preservation and increase of trustworthiness in data-driven insights and models; and finally, establishing and upholding accountability and oversight mechanisms. The document provides a practical implementation checklist to aid in the adoption of these ethical guidelines. It’s important to note that this guidance does not introduce new legal obligations. The guide is intended to be a resource for data scientists, organizations, and anyone involved in the development and application of data science techniques, promoting responsible innovation and fostering public trust in data-driven solutions. The collaborative effort between the RSS and IFoA highlights the diverse perspectives needed to address the ethical challenges inherent in a rapidly evolving field. The document’s focus on accountability and oversight underscores the importance of robust governance structures within data science projects. Ultimately, the goal is to ensure that data science is used responsibly and ethically, contributing positively to society.
