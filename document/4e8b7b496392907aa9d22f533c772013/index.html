Generative AI How People Are Really Using Gen AI in 2025

Authors: Marc Zao-Sanders

This article, published in April 2025, provides an update on the evolving landscape of generative AI usage, building upon a previous HBR piece from the prior year. The core focus is on how individuals and businesses are leveraging generative AI, particularly large language models (LLMs), in 2025. A significant shift has occurred with the introduction of Custom GPTs – AI models specifically designed for more targeted and specialized needs, representing a move beyond general-purpose LLMs. The article highlights the increased competition within the AI space, driven by the emergence of new players like DeepSeek and Grok, alongside established models like NotebookLM. The adoption of generative AI has seen a dramatic increase in user interest, fueled by substantial investment from governments and private companies, and a growing awareness of the profound implications – some even suggesting the future of humanity is at stake. The article emphasizes the rapid evolution of the technology and the need for updated research to understand the current state of its application. The shift reflects a maturing market, moving from initial hype to more focused and practical implementations, with a greater emphasis on tailored solutions and increased scrutiny from regulatory bodies. The article underscores the importance of understanding the diverse use cases, ranging from personal applications to complex business operations, and the ongoing need for innovation and adaptation within the field of generative AI.
