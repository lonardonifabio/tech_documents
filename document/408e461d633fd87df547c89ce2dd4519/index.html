CRISP-DM Methodology

Authors: Fabio Lonardoni

This document outlines the CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) methodology, a widely adopted iterative process for data mining projects. CRISP-DM is structured around four phases: Business Understanding, Data Understanding, Modeling, and Evaluation. The document emphasizes that CRISP-DM is an iterative process, meaning it’s not a linear sequence but rather a cycle of refinement. It begins with a thorough Business Understanding, where the project's intention, goals, and objectives are clearly defined, including identifying the key questions to be answered. The Data Understanding phase focuses on collecting and analyzing the data, determining what the business needs and what data is required. Data Preparation is a crucial step, involving transforming the data into a usable format, often selecting a subset based on the 70% rule. The Modeling phase involves applying appropriate models to extract meaningful insights and potentially generate new knowledge through data mining. Evaluation is then performed, testing the selected model with pre-selected test data. Finally, Deployment involves using the model on new data and with new stakeholders. The document also highlights an analytic approach, incorporating descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive analytics. It stresses the importance of defining the best machine learning algorithm and identifying the patterns needed to address the problem. The overall message is that CRISP-DM provides a robust framework for data mining projects, ensuring a systematic and iterative approach from problem definition to model deployment. The document’s focus on iterative processes and continuous refinement underscores the dynamic nature of data science projects.
