European Commission Guidelines on Obligations for General-Purpose AI Models

Authors: European Commission

This document outlines the European Commission's guidelines regarding the obligations associated with general-purpose AI models, as established by Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, commonly known as the AI Act. The core focus is on providing clarity and operational procedures for entities developing and deploying these models. The document begins with a background section detailing the objectives of the guidelines. It then systematically addresses key aspects of general-purpose AI models, starting with the definition of what constitutes a ‘general-purpose AI model,’ exploring the entire lifecycle of such models, and crucially, identifying those models posing ‘systemic risk.’ The guidelines meticulously detail the classification process for models with systemic risk, including the requirements for notification and the procedures for contesting these classifications. Furthermore, it establishes a designation process for models identified as high-risk, followed by a robust procedure for challenging this designation. The document aims to ensure compliance with the AI Act by offering a practical framework for understanding and meeting the regulatory requirements. It’s a foundational document for organizations operating within the EU’s AI regulatory landscape, providing specific instructions on how to navigate the complexities of the AI Act’s obligations. The detailed sections on notification, designation, and contesting procedures are particularly important for ensuring transparency and accountability in the development and deployment of general-purpose AI models. The document’s structure and content are designed to facilitate a smooth transition for businesses and developers into the new regulatory environment, promoting responsible innovation in the field of artificial intelligence. The systematic approach taken in defining ‘systemic risk’ and the associated procedures are central to the AI Act’s goal of mitigating potential harms.
