Closing Data Gaps to Accelerate Africa's Transformation

Authors: MO IBRAHIM FOUNDATION

This report, published by the MO Ibrahim Foundation in January 2024, focuses on the critical role of data in accelerating Africa’s transformation. It addresses the significant ‘closing data gaps’ challenge, recognizing that a lack of reliable data hinders effective policymaking and development efforts across the continent. The document is part of the IIAG Series Report and highlights the increasing global demand for data, driven by monitoring requirements. It begins by establishing the ‘case for data,’ emphasizing the necessity of good data for effective policymaking and the escalating demand. The second chapter, ‘The status of data in Africa,’ examines the current state of data availability, specifically focusing on Civil Registration and Vital Statistics (CRVS) systems, sex-disaggregated data, local data, and disability data. It incorporates expert perspectives from organizations like the Africa Integrity Indicators – African Institute for Development Policy (AFIDEP), Afrobarometer, and the Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project (ACLED). The report acknowledges the decline in international funding for statistics and stresses the importance of independent National Statistical Offices (NSOs). A key theme is the need for investment in data infrastructure and the challenges NSOs face, particularly in conflict zones. The document advocates for a strategic approach to data collection, incorporating diverse data sources and recognizing the unique needs of different communities. Ultimately, the report argues that closing these data gaps is essential for informed decision-making, sustainable development, and achieving Africa’s transformation goals. The report’s core argument is that data is not just a tool, but a fundamental requirement for good governance and effective development strategies.
