Scalable AI Incident Classification

Authors: Simon Mylius, Winter Fellow at the Centre for the Governance of AI (GovAI)

This document details a proof-of-concept project focused on automating the classification of AI incidents using Large Language Models (LLMs). The work, spearheaded by Simon Mylius in collaboration with the Centre for the Governance of AI (GovAI), aims to improve the efficiency and scalability of analyzing the AI Incident Database. The core of the project involves leveraging an LLM to classify incident reports based on risk taxonomies, specifically utilizing the MIT AI Risk Taxonomies, and assessing harm severities according to the CSET Taxonomy of AI Harm.
