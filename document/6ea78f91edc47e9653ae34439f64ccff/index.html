Earning Trust for AI in Health: A Collaborative Path Forward

Authors: World Economic Forum, Boston Consulting Group

This white paper, published by the World Economic Forum in June 2025, addresses the critical need for collaboration to establish trust in the deployment of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the healthcare sector. The document highlights significant global divergences in the adoption and scaling of AI in health, emphasizing the pivotal role of the private sector in driving innovation and standardization. It argues that AI regulations must evolve dynamically to keep pace with the rapid advancements in the field. A pragmatic approach is advocated, centered around guidelines, regulatory sandboxes, and robust post-market surveillance mechanisms. The paper stresses that legislation can provide a foundational framework for governing AI in healthcare, while sandboxes offer a controlled environment for private sector experimentation. Post-market surveillance is presented as a vital tool for managing the evolving nature of AI technologies. Crucially, the document champions public-private partnerships (PPPs) as essential for regulating medical devices, including software, leveraging private sector capabilities to test and operationalize regulatory processes. It proposes a structured approach to PPPs, incorporating quality assurance resources for independent testing and training. The white paper underscores the importance of ongoing collaboration between public and private entities to ensure responsible and effective AI implementation in healthcare, mitigating potential risks and maximizing the benefits of this transformative technology.
