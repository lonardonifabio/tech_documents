What role for AI skills in (re -)shaping the future European workforce ?

Authors: Lucia Vesnic -Alujevic, Georgios Saitis

This briefing paper, produced by the European Parliamentary Research Service (EPRS) and authored by Lucia Vesnic -Alujevic and Georgios Saitis, examines the crucial role of AI skills in transforming the European workforce. The document highlights the significant skills gap within the EU, with nearly half of the population lacking fundamental digital skills, including AI proficiency, presenting a major obstacle to maintaining European competitiveness and mitigating regional inequalities. It emphasizes the urgency of addressing this gap, particularly in light of the rapid advancements in artificial intelligence. The briefing advocates for a human-centric approach to AI development, stressing the importance of proactive governance, a culture of innovation, and inclusivity within the AI workforce. Key recommendations include supporting diversity and inclusiveness, strengthening digital infrastructure, and aligning AI development with European values to ensure fairness and transparency. Furthermore, the document underscores the need for ethical considerations, accountability, and prioritizing people's wellbeing throughout the AI transformation. The European Union's future economic prosperity is directly linked to successfully harnessing AI's potential while upholding these core principles. The EPRS briefing serves as a critical analysis for policymakers navigating the complex challenges and opportunities presented by AI's evolving role in the European landscape.
