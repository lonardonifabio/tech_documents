AI Explainability in Practice Participant Workbook

Authors: David Leslie, Cami Rincón, Morgan Briggs, Antonella Maia Perini, Smera Jayadeva, Ann Borda, SJ Bennett, Christopher Burr, Mhairi Aitken, Sabeehah Mahomed, Janis Wong, Madeleine Waller, Claudia Fischer

This document is the Participant Workbook for the 'AI Explainability in Practice' programme, designed to support participants engaging in workshops focused on AI explainability. It serves as a facilitator workbook, annotated to assist in delivering the accompanying activities. The workbook was created by a team of researchers from The Alan Turing Institute's Ethics Theme, led by David Leslie and Cami Rincón. Key contributors include Morgan Briggs, Antonella Maia Perini, Smera Jayadeva, Ann Borda, SJ Bennett, Christopher Burr, Mhairi Aitken, Sabeehah Mahomed, Janis Wong, Madeleine Waller, and Claudia Fischer. The project benefited significantly from the support of the Public Policy Programme Co-Directors, Helen Margetts and Cosmina Dorobantu, and the design input of Conor Rigby, who provided extensive feedback throughout the workbook's development. Visual elements were created by Johnny Lighthands, while Alex Krook and John Gilbert offered valuable insights to ensure the workbook's completion. The document highlights the collaborative nature of the project, acknowledging the contributions of various individuals and teams, including the Digital Office for Scottish Local Government and Camden Council. The overall purpose is to equip participants with the knowledge and tools necessary to understand and address the challenges and opportunities presented by AI explainability.
