Superagency in the Workplace: Empowering people to unlock AI’s full potential

Authors: Hannah Mayer, Lareina Yee, Michael Chui, Roger Roberts, Reid Hoffman, Greg Beato

This report, published in January 2025, explores the transformative potential of artificial intelligence (AI) within the workplace, drawing parallels to the impact of the steam engine. Authored by a team at McKinsey, alongside contributions from Reid Hoffman and Greg Beato’s ‘Superagency’ book, the document argues that AI, particularly through large language models (LLMs) developed by major tech companies, represents a significant opportunity for productivity growth. McKinsey estimates a long-term AI market opportunity of $4.4 trillion driven by corporate use cases. The report highlights the current investment trends, with 92% of companies planning to increase their AI investments over the next three years. It addresses the challenges associated with realizing this potential, acknowledging the uncertainty surrounding short-term returns. The document emphasizes the need for leadership to effectively guide the adoption of AI, framing it as a critical step in navigating the future. It references key McKinsey research reports published in June 2024 and June 2023, further solidifying the data-driven approach. The core argument centers on the urgency of preparing for and capitalizing on the AI revolution, advocating for a proactive and strategic approach to implementation. The report’s structure includes an introduction, five chapters detailing key aspects of AI’s impact, a conclusion summarizing the future of AI, acknowledgements, a methodology section, and a glossary. The inclusion of ‘Superagency’ suggests a focus on the broader societal and organizational implications of AI’s advancement.
