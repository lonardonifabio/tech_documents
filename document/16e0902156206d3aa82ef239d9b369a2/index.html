A Review of its Purpose and Structure EU AI Act

Authors: Sean Musch, Michael Borrelli, Charles Kerrigan, Faris Aranki, Shiageto Consulting, Vangelis Zarkadoulas

This document, published by AI & Partners, provides a review of the European AI Board's purpose and structure, specifically in the context of the EU AI Act. The report focuses on understanding the board's role in safeguarding digital rights for users globally who are considered at risk due to the increasing influence of artificial intelligence. AI & Partners operates by combining technical support, policy engagement, advocacy, professional services, regulatory interventions, and participation in industry groups like AI Commons. The core mission is to champion fundamental rights within the evolving landscape of AI. The report is structured around key questions concerning the European Artificial Intelligence Board, including its definition, membership, and the relationship between the AI Board and the AI Office. It highlights AI & Partners' commitment to defending user rights and promoting responsible AI development through a multi-faceted approach. The document serves as a foundational analysis of the European AI Board, intended to inform understanding and potentially contribute to ongoing discussions surrounding AI governance and regulation.
