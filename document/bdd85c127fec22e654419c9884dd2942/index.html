Defining and Operationalizing Risk Tiers for GPAI Code of Practice

Authors: Campos et al.

This document provides guidance on defining and operationalizing risk tiers within the context of the EU General Purpose AI (GPAI) Code of Practice. It leverages a risk management framework that incorporates both risk tolerance (maximum acceptable risk levels) and key risk indicator thresholds. The framework is designed to maintain risks below defined tolerance levels by triggering specific mitigations based on measurable signals, such as model evaluations. Specifically, the document addresses the requirements outlined in the third draft of the GPAI Code of Practice, which mandates the definition of Systemic Risk Tiers and associated mitigations. It expands upon feedback provided on previous drafts of the Code, focusing on establishing acceptable risk levels and operationalizing these tiers. The core concept revolves around a hierarchical approach to risk management, allowing for tailored mitigation strategies based on the severity and nature of identified risks.
