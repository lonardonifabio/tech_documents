The ethics of artificial intelligence: Issues and initiatives

Authors: Eleanor Bird, Jasmin Fox -Skelly, Nicola Jenner, Ruth Larbey, Emma Weitkamp, Alan Winfield

This study, produced by the STOA Panel for the Future of Science and Technology, delves into the critical ethical considerations surrounding the development and deployment of artificial intelligence (AI) technologies. It systematically examines the moral dilemmas and potential societal impacts arising from AI, focusing on a wide range of issues including fairness, accountability, and the broader implications for human relationships. The document provides a comparative analysis of existing AI governance frameworks established by various countries and regions globally. Specifically, it investigates the challenges related to equitable benefit-sharing, the assignment of responsibility for AI-driven decisions, the potential for worker exploitation within AI-powered systems, and the significant environmental consequences, particularly concerning energy consumption and its contribution to climate change. Furthermore, the study addresses more nuanced and uncertain aspects of AI, such as the impact on human connections and social dynamics. The research highlights the gaps in current frameworks, emphasizing the need for robust mechanisms to mitigate risks and ensure responsible innovation. The authors, based at the Science Communication Unit of the University of the West of England, conducted this work at the request of the STOA Panel, managed by the Scientific Foresight Unit within the Directorate-General for Parliamentary Research Services (EPRS) of the European Parliament. The document represents a valuable resource for policymakers, researchers, and anyone interested in navigating the complex ethical landscape of artificial intelligence. It underscores the urgency of proactive governance to harness the benefits of AI while safeguarding human values and the environment.
