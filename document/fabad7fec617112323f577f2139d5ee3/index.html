Test Criteria Catalogue for AI Systems in Finance

Authors: Ulf Menzler, Henry Kleineidam, Cleo Matzken, Todor Dobrikov, Philipp Herrmann, Petra Gutjahr, Alexander Mehlmann, Danos, Vasilios Fabian Langer, Thora Markert, Melanie Bohnen, Pascal Debus, Sebastian Issel, Kilian Tscharke, Alexander Wagner

This document details the 'Test Criteria Catalogue for AI Systems in Finance,' a publication developed collaboratively by d-fine GmbH, TÜV Informationstechnik GmbH, and Fraunhofer AISEC, on behalf of the Federal Office for Information Security (BSI). The catalogue provides a framework for evaluating the trustworthiness of AI systems within the financial sector. It was created to address the growing importance of trustworthy AI, particularly in light of the EU AI Act's international scope. The publication’s development involved significant contributions from experts who participated in interviews, discussions, questionnaires, and workshops, highlighting the collaborative nature of the project. The core purpose is to offer a practical tool for assessing AI systems' security, reliability, and ethical considerations within the financial industry. The document’s accessibility was prioritized through its preparation in English, recognizing the language's dominance in technical, regulatory, and academic discussions concerning artificial intelligence. This choice ensures broader engagement with researchers, industry professionals, and policymakers globally. The catalogue's creation reflects a proactive approach to managing the risks associated with AI adoption in finance, aligning with evolving regulatory standards and best practices. The collaborative effort underscores the need for a multi-faceted approach to AI governance, combining technical expertise with stakeholder input. The document's ultimate goal is to promote the responsible development and deployment of AI systems in the financial sector, fostering trust and confidence among users and regulators alike. The extensive involvement of various organizations and individuals demonstrates a commitment to creating a robust and adaptable framework for evaluating AI in this critical domain.
