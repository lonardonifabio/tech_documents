Reuters Institute Digital News Report 2025

Authors: Nic Newman, Amy Ross Arguedas, Craig T. Robertson, Rasmus Kleis Nielsen, Richard Fletcher

The Reuters Institute Digital News Report 2025, produced by the Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism, offers a comprehensive analysis of the evolving digital news landscape. This report delves into key trends shaping news consumption and production across numerous countries. It examines how audiences are checking information, the role of local news in a platform-dominated world, and the impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on news personalization. The report includes detailed analysis of news podcasts across various nations and explores strategies for engaging audiences without overwhelming them with notifications. A significant portion of the report is dedicated to country-specific data, providing insights into news consumption patterns and media landscapes in Europe and the Americas. The data covers a wide range of countries including the United Kingdom, Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Serbia, Slovakia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, and Turkey, as well as Argentina, Brazil, Canada, Chile, Colombia, and the United States.
