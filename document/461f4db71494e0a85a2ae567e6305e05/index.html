Machine Learning Methods for Malware Detection

Authors: Kaspersky

This document, originating from Kaspersky, presents a summary of their extensive experience utilizing machine learning techniques to develop advanced malware protection solutions. The paper focuses on leveraging machine learning for cybersecurity, detailing various approaches and considerations. It begins with fundamental concepts, outlining both unsupervised and supervised learning methodologies, alongside the introduction of deep learning as a key component. A core tenet of their approach emphasizes the necessity of large, representative datasets for training models. Furthermore, the document stresses the importance of model interpretability and minimizing false positive rates, acknowledging the evolving tactics of malware writers and the need for adaptable algorithms. Kaspersky’s strategy involves a two-stage detection process: pre-execution analysis, which utilizes techniques like executable format descriptions, binary data statistics, and code emulation to identify threats before execution, and post-execution analysis, which monitors the behavior of running processes. The post-execution phase incorporates similarity hashing and decision tree ensembles. The document also explores deep learning's application in detecting rare attacks and its use in analyzing post-execution behavior. A significant aspect of their methodology involves clustering incoming network objects and employing knowledge distillation to efficiently update models. The overall aim is to provide a robust and adaptable defense against malware, constantly evolving to counter new threats. The document highlights the critical role of data collection across both pre- and post-execution phases, emphasizing the need for comprehensive data analysis to effectively identify and mitigate malware risks. The focus on adaptability and continuous learning is central to Kaspersky's machine learning-based approach to cybersecurity.
