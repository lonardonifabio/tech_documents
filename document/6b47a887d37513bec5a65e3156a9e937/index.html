The Direction of AI Innovation in the UK: Insights from a New Database and a Roadmap for Reform

Authors: Carsten Jung, Bhargav Srinivasa Desikan

This report, published in April 2025 by the Institute for Public Policy Research (IPPR), examines the trajectory of artificial intelligence innovation within the United Kingdom. The core of the analysis stems from a newly developed database, providing a detailed overview of AI development trends and activities across the nation. IPPR, a long-standing independent charity dedicated to fostering a fairer, greener, and more prosperous society, commissioned this research to offer insights and recommendations for reform. The Institute’s history of impactful research and policy work, spanning over three decades, includes pioneering efforts such as advocating for the minimum wage and addressing regional inequalities, alongside proposing solutions like a windfall tax on energy companies. This report leverages IPPR's deep understanding of UK societal challenges and its commitment to translating bold ideas into practical policy. The document outlines a roadmap for reform, suggesting strategies to guide and shape AI innovation in a way that benefits the UK. The authors, Carsten Jung and Bhargav Srinivasa Desikan, aim to provide a data-driven perspective on the current state of AI in the UK, identifying key areas for investment and development, and proposing mechanisms to ensure that AI’s growth aligns with broader societal goals. The report’s findings are intended to inform policymakers, businesses, and researchers, contributing to a more strategic and equitable approach to AI innovation. IPPR’s established network and expertise across the UK, including IPPR North and IPPR Scotland, are crucial to the report's relevance and impact. The document’s publication date, April 2025, reflects the urgency of addressing the evolving landscape of AI and its potential consequences.
