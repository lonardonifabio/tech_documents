A Survey of Context Engineering for Large Language Models

Authors: Lingrui Mei, Jiayu Yao, Yuyao Ge, Yiwei Wang, Baolong Bi, Yujun Cai, Jiazhi Liu, Mingyu Li, Zhong-Zhi Li, Duzhen Zhang, Chenlin Zhou, Jiayi Mao, Tianze Xia, Jiafeng Guo, Shenghua Liu

This document presents a survey on Context Engineering, a formal discipline designed to systematically optimize the information payloads utilized by Large Language Models (LLMs) during inference. It moves beyond simple prompt design to encompass a comprehensive approach to managing and refining the contextual data fed to these models. The survey establishes a taxonomy of Context Engineering, broken down into foundational Components and sophisticated System Implementations. The foundational Components are categorized as (1) Context Retrieval and Generation, which includes prompt-based generation and external knowledge acquisition; (2) Context Processing, focusing on long sequence processing, self-refinement, and structured information integration; and (3) Context Management, dealing with memory hierarchies, compression, and optimization strategies. The System Implementations then demonstrate how these components are architecturally integrated. Specifically, the survey details Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) as a key implementation. The overall goal is to improve the performance of LLMs by providing a structured and optimized approach to contextual information, recognizing that the quality and management of context are fundamental determinants of LLM effectiveness. The document highlights the need for a formalized discipline to address the complexities of contextual data within LLMs, suggesting a shift from ad-hoc prompt engineering to a more systematic and controlled approach. The research contributes to a deeper understanding of how LLMs can be effectively leveraged by providing a framework for managing and enhancing the contextual information they process.
