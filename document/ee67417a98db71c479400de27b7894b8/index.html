Blockchain as an Enabler of Trusted AI AI & Blockchain Convergences Task Force Report

Authors: Mariana de la Roche W., BlackVogel Mat Yarger, Prof. Dr. Ingrid Vasiliu-Feltes, Tan Gürpinar, Jim Mason, Paolo Giudici, Sharm in Chougule, Xiaochen Zhang

This report, produced by the AI & Blockchain Convergences Task Force, examines the critical role of blockchain technology in fostering trust within the rapidly evolving field of Artificial Intelligence. It addresses the significant ethical challenges posed by AI's expansion, particularly concerning the need for trustworthy AI systems. The report focuses on establishing core ethical principles – including fairness, transparency, privacy, and accountability – as foundational elements for building reliable and trustworthy AI. The Task Force highlights the potential of blockchain to provide verifiable and immutable records, thereby enhancing transparency and accountability in AI development and deployment. The document outlines the need for a robust framework that leverages blockchain's capabilities to address concerns about bias, data manipulation, and lack of oversight within AI systems. It emphasizes the importance of a collaborative approach involving stakeholders across various sectors to ensure responsible innovation and the ethical utilization of AI. The report’s aim is to provide a roadmap for integrating blockchain solutions to build a more trustworthy and ethical AI ecosystem. The Task Force’s work is particularly relevant given the increasing reliance on AI across industries and the growing public concern about its potential negative impacts. The report’s findings contribute to the ongoing discussion about governance and regulation of AI, advocating for a technology-driven approach that prioritizes ethical considerations and public trust. The document serves as a starting point for exploring the synergies between AI and blockchain, recognizing that both technologies have the potential to transform industries and society, but only if developed and deployed responsibly.
