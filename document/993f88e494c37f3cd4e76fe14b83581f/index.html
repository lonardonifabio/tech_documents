Superintelligence Strategy: Expert Version

Authors: Dan Hendrycks, Eric Schmidt, Alexandr Wang

This document outlines the urgent need for a strategic approach to navigating the potential risks and opportunities presented by rapidly advancing artificial intelligence, particularly the emergence of superintelligence. It argues that the current trajectory of AI development, characterized by competitive advancements and the potential for destabilizing proliferation, mirrors the dynamics of nuclear deterrence. The core concept introduced is Mutual Assured AI Malfunction (MAIM), a strategy modeled after Mutual Assured Destruction (MAD) where any attempt by a nation to achieve unilateral AI dominance is met with preemptive sabotage by rival states. This strategy acknowledges the relative ease with which AI projects can be disrupted – through cyberattacks or physical destruction of data centers – and proposes a framework for deterrence. Beyond deterrence, the document suggests that nations should simultaneously invest in their economies and militaries to maintain competitiveness and actively work to prevent the spread of weaponizable AI capabilities to non-state actors. The document highlights the critical need for a proactive, multi-faceted approach to managing the risks associated with superintelligence, recognizing that the consequences of unchecked AI development could dramatically reshape the global balance of power and increase the likelihood of conflict or catastrophic events. The authors emphasize that this isn't simply a theoretical exercise, but a necessary response to a rapidly evolving technological landscape where the stakes are exceptionally high. The document’s central argument is that a robust, coordinated global strategy is essential to mitigate the dangers and harness the potential benefits of superintelligence, preventing a scenario where AI becomes a source of existential threat.
