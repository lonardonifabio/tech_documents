AI Impact Assessment: The tool for a responsible AI project

Authors: Ministry of Infrastructure and Water Management (I&W), Office of the CIO (CDIB), Human Environment and Transport Inspectorate (ILT IDlab and analysis department), Directorate-General for Public Works and Water Management (RWS Datalab)

This document, "AI Impact Assessment: The tool for a responsible AI project," Version 2.0, published in December 2024, provides a framework for ensuring responsible AI development. Developed in collaboration with various Dutch government agencies – including the Ministry of Infrastructure and Water Management, the Office of the CIO, the Human Environment and Transport Inspectorate, and RWS Datalab – it offers a structured approach to assessing AI projects throughout their lifecycle. The document is divided into two parts: Part A focuses on the assessment phase, detailing the necessary steps to evaluate the system's purpose, necessity, and potential impacts, including fundamental rights, sustainability, and other effects. It emphasizes considerations like bias, accuracy, reliability, and explainability within the technical implementation. Part B then guides the implementation and use of the AI system, covering aspects such as data governance (quality, privacy, and confidentiality), risk management, and accountability. The core aim is to promote responsible AI practices by proactively identifying and mitigating potential risks and ensuring transparency and verifiability. The document is intended for use in all phases of an AI project, providing a comprehensive guide for organizations seeking to deploy AI systems responsibly. The collaboration highlights the importance of inter-agency cooperation in shaping and implementing responsible AI strategies within the Dutch government.
