Automated Machine Learning Methods, Systems, Challenges

Authors: Frank Hutter, Lars Kotthoff, Joaquin Vanschoren

This book, part of the Springer Series on Challenges in Machine Learning, focuses on Automated Machine Learning (AutoML) methods, systems, and the associated challenges. It serves as a comprehensive teaching toolkit for engineers and scientists, providing a complete resource centered around successful machine learning competitions. The book's content includes analyses of the challenges encountered in these competitions, detailed tutorial material, descriptions of the datasets used, and links to relevant data and software. It’s designed to be a valuable learning tool, offering a structured approach to understanding and implementing AutoML techniques. The series itself is a collection of papers presented within the context of these competitions, aiming to advance the field of machine learning through practical application and rigorous analysis. The book's structure and content are geared towards both theoretical understanding and hands-on implementation, making it suitable for a range of skill levels. The inclusion of competition data and software pointers further enhances its utility, allowing readers to directly engage with the challenges and solutions presented. The Springer Series on Challenges in Machine Learning provides a dynamic and evolving collection of research and practical insights, reflecting the ongoing advancements in the field of automated machine learning. The book's open access availability and comprehensive nature make it a key resource for anyone involved in developing or applying AutoML solutions. The series editors, Hugo Jair Escalante, Isabelle Guyon, and Sergio Escalera, contribute to the overall quality and scope of the publications within the series.
