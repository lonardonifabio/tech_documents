AI Safety in Practice Participant Workbook

Authors: David Leslie, Cami Rincón, Morgan Briggs, Antonella Maia Perini, Smera Jayadeva, Ann Borda, SJ Bennett, Christopher Burr, Claudia Fischer

This document is the Participant Workbook for the 'AI Safety in Practice' programme, designed to support participants engaging in workshops related to AI ethics and governance. It serves as a practical guide, facilitating active involvement and understanding of key concepts. The workbook was collaboratively created by a team of researchers, including David Leslie, Cami Rincón, and Morgan Briggs, alongside contributions from numerous individuals within the Public Policy Programme at The Alan Turing Institute. Specifically, Conor Rigby led the design and provided invaluable feedback throughout the iterative development process. Illustrations were created by Johnny Lighthands, while Alex Krook and John Gilbert offered crucial insights that aided in the finalization of the workbook. The document’s creation benefited significantly from the support of the Digital Office for Scottish Local Government, the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE), and the expertise of Carolyn Ashurst, Kamalaruban Parameswaran, and Ruth Drysdale. The workbook represents a tangible output of a multi-faceted research project focused on applying ethical considerations within the rapidly evolving field of AI safety, reflecting the collaborative nature of the Public Policy Programme at The Alan Turing Institute. It’s intended to be a resource for practical application and discussion, rather than a standalone theoretical text.
