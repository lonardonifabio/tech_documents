AI, Children’s Rights, & Wellbeing: Transnational Frameworks

Authors: Sabeehah Mahomed, Mhairi Aitken, Ayça Atabey, Janis Wong, Morgan Briggs

This document, produced by The Alan Turing Institute’s Public Policy Programme, explores the complex intersections between Artificial Intelligence (AI), children’s rights, and wellbeing. Released in November 2023, the report maps thirteen distinct frameworks that address these interconnected areas. The Alan Turing Institute, the UK’s national institute for data science and AI, established the Public Policy Programme in 2018 with the core mission of assisting governments in leveraging data-intensive technologies while prioritizing ethical considerations and governance. The Institute, named after Alan Turing, focuses on advancing world-class research in data science and AI, applying it to global challenges, and fostering a public conversation around these technologies. Specifically, the report delves into frameworks that aim to safeguard children’s rights and wellbeing in the context of rapidly evolving AI systems. It recognizes the potential benefits of AI for improving public services and addressing societal challenges, but emphasizes the critical need for governments to proactively incorporate ethical guidelines and robust governance structures. The document highlights the importance of a collaborative approach, working alongside policymakers to ensure responsible innovation. The team behind this research, including Sabeehah Mahomed, Mhairi Aitken, Ayça Atabey, Janis Wong, and Morgan Briggs, are dedicated to navigating the ethical and societal implications of AI, particularly concerning vulnerable populations like children. The report’s ultimate goal is to contribute to a future where AI is developed and deployed in a manner that respects human rights and promotes the overall wellbeing of individuals, especially children, across national borders. The work is underpinned by the Institute’s broader mission to drive informed public discourse and shape policy decisions related to data science and AI. The document acknowledges the contributions of Joudy Bourghli and Alex Krook, further solidifying the team's commitment to this vital area of research.
