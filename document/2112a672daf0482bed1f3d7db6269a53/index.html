Towards an EU Strategy on Artificial Intelligence in Science - Council conclusions

Authors: Council of the European Union

This document represents the Council of the European Union's conclusions regarding the development of an EU Strategy on Artificial Intelligence in Science. Approved on May 23, 2025, the conclusions stem from a series of prior deliberations, including the 2021 conclusions on the Future Governance of the European Research Area, the 2022 conclusions on the New European Innovation Agenda, and the 2024 conclusions related to the European Court of Auditors’ Special Report on EU Artificial Intelligence ambition. The Council emphasizes the need for coordinated efforts, scaled-up investments, and improved access to digital infrastructure to support AI development. Specifically, it references the Commission’s Coordinated Plan on Artificial Intelligence, aiming to align Member States’ strategies with EU priorities. The document highlights the importance of the Scientific Advice Mechanism and the need for stronger governance and increased investment in AI, reflecting a strategic response to the evolving landscape of artificial intelligence within the European Research Area. The conclusions are a key step in shaping the EU's approach to AI, focusing on collaboration and strategic investment to ensure the EU remains competitive and innovative in this rapidly developing field. The document’s approval signifies a commitment to a coordinated and ambitious strategy for AI within the European context.
