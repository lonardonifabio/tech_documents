Expanded ASEAN Guide on AI Governance and Ethics – Generative AI

Authors: ASEAN, Unknown

This document presents an expanded guide on AI governance and ethics specifically focusing on generative AI (Gen AI) within the ASEAN region. It serves as a supplement to the 2024 ASEAN Guide on AI Governance and Ethics, providing a deeper dive into the opportunities and risks associated with Gen AI. The primary goal is to offer policy recommendations for ASEAN to responsibly adopt Gen AI, acknowledging both its potential benefits and the associated dangers. The guide addresses six key risks identified in the original ASEAN guide: mistakes and anthropomorphism, factually inaccurate responses and disinformation, deepfakes, impersonation, fraudulent and malicious activities, infringement of intellectual property rights, and the propagation of embedded biases. Furthermore, it anticipates and explores longer-term systemic risks stemming from the continued advancement of highly advanced Gen AI models, recognizing that these are not yet prevalent across the ASEAN member states. The document emphasizes the need for regionally interoperable and proportional policy measures to ensure safety and responsible innovation. It’s a proactive approach to navigating the complexities of Gen AI, aiming to balance technological progress with ethical considerations and safeguards. The guide’s recommendations are designed to be adaptable and scalable, reflecting the diverse needs and contexts within the ASEAN region. It’s a crucial resource for policymakers and stakeholders seeking to shape the future of AI governance in the region, promoting a framework that maximizes the positive impact of Gen AI while mitigating potential harms.
