European AI Standards – Technical Standardization and Implementation Challenges under the EU AI Act

Authors: Robert Kilian, Linda Jäck, Dominik Ebel

This research paper offers a preliminary analysis of the technical and standardization challenges associated with the implementation of the EU AI Act. Based on extensive qualitative interviews with 23 leading European organizations – including prominent AI development companies like Mistral and Helsing – the study investigates how businesses, particularly startups and SMEs, are navigating the anticipated standardization efforts. The research delves into the complexities of aligning with the EU AI Act alongside existing sectoral product safety legislation. A key finding highlights the significant constraint imposed by a limited implementation period of roughly 6 months, contrasting sharply with the estimated 12 months required for actual compliance. The study comprehensively covers a diverse range of industry sectors, encompassing mobility, finance, manufacturing, healthcare, enterprise software, and defense, alongside legal tech. It examines the practical difficulties faced by organizations in adapting to the evolving standards landscape. The research underscores the need for a more extended timeframe for effective implementation and suggests that the current 6-month window presents a substantial hurdle for many European AI companies. The paper’s systematic approach provides valuable insights for policymakers, regulators, and businesses seeking to understand and address the challenges of achieving compliance within the framework of the EU AI Act. The study’s focus on startups and SMEs is particularly relevant, given their often-limited resources and capacity for navigating complex regulatory environments. The research contributes to a deeper understanding of the practical implications of standardization and highlights the need for a more balanced approach to ensure that innovation and competitiveness are not unduly stifled.
