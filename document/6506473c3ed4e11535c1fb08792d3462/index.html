A Taxonomy of Trustworthiness for Artificial Intelligence: Connecting Properties of Trustworthiness with Risk Management and the AI Lifecycle

Authors: Jessica Newman, UC Berkeley Center for Long-Term Cybersecurity

This white paper, part of the CLTC White Paper Series, presents a taxonomy of trustworthiness for artificial intelligence, directly linking its properties with risk management and the entire AI lifecycle. The document’s core focus is on establishing a structured approach to understanding and evaluating the trustworthiness of AI systems. It begins with an introduction to the concept of ‘trustworthy AI,’ referencing the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) AI Risk Management Framework (RMF) as a foundational element. The paper comprehensively analyzes existing frameworks for trustworthy AI, which served as the basis for this work. A key contribution is the introduction of the concept of ‘properties of trustworthiness’ for AI, offering a detailed categorization of attributes that contribute to a system’s trustworthiness. The taxonomy is designed to be applicable across the AI lifecycle, from development to deployment and ongoing monitoring. The paper includes an appendix detailing connections to international AI standards and a comprehensive list of the properties of trustworthiness, deliberately avoiding segmentation by lifecycle stage to facilitate a holistic understanding. The document’s purpose is to provide a practical tool for organizations and researchers seeking to build and deploy AI systems that are demonstrably reliable, safe, and aligned with ethical considerations. It emphasizes the importance of proactively managing risks associated with AI and integrating trustworthiness assessments into every stage of the AI development process. The white paper’s ultimate goal is to foster a more robust and responsible AI ecosystem.
