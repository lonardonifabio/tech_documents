A Closer Look at the Existing Risks of Generative AI: Mapping the Who, What, and How of Real-World Incidents

Authors: Megan Li, Wendy Bickersteth, Ningjing Tang, Jason Hong, Lorrie Cranor, Hong Shen, Hoda Heidari

This research paper investigates the existing risks associated with Generative AI, focusing on mapping the various factors contributing to harm. The study acknowledges the widespread adoption of Generative AI across diverse domains, which inherently increases the potential for negative consequences. The authors construct a specific taxonomy for Generative AI failures, building upon existing frameworks for AI risks and harms. The core of the research involves a systematic analysis of 499 publicly reported incidents of Generative AI failures. The analysis meticulously details the types of harms reported, the root causes (failure modes) behind these incidents, and the individuals or groups affected (harmed stakeholders). Key findings reveal that the majority of reported incidents stem from issues related to how the AI systems are used, rather than inherent flaws in the technology itself. Crucially, the research demonstrates that harm extends beyond the immediate end-users of the Generative AI system; the consequences can ripple outwards, impacting broader communities and society. The paper provides a comprehensive overview of the diverse ways in which Generative AI can cause harm, offering valuable insights for developers, policymakers, and anyone concerned about the responsible development and deployment of this rapidly evolving technology. The study's detailed breakdown of incident types, failure modes, and stakeholder impacts provides a robust foundation for understanding and mitigating the risks associated with Generative AI. The research emphasizes the need for a holistic approach to risk assessment, considering the entire lifecycle of the technology and its potential downstream applications. Ultimately, this work contributes to a more informed discussion about the ethical and societal implications of Generative AI.
