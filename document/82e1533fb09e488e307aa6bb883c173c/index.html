OECD Services Trade Restrictiveness Index: Policy Trends up to 2025

Authors: OECD

This document, published by the OECD, presents an analysis of trends in the OECD Services Trade Restrictiveness Index up to 2025. It’s a policy-oriented report examining the barriers and restrictions placed on international trade in services. The report is produced under the responsibility of the Secretary-General of the OECD and represents the opinions of the organization, not necessarily the official views of its member countries. Crucially, the document includes statistical data for Israel, supplied by the relevant Israeli authorities, with specific caveats regarding the inclusion of data related to territories such as the Golan Heights, East Jerusalem, and Israeli settlements in the West Bank, acknowledging the complexities of international law. The analysis is based on regulatory data collection and aims to provide insights into the factors influencing services trade restrictiveness. The report highlights the ongoing efforts to understand and address these restrictions, contributing to discussions on trade liberalization and the facilitation of services trade. The OECD Trade and Agriculture Directorate prepared this report, and the organization expresses gratitude to Fandi Achmad and Kallista Nadia A for their assistance with the regulatory data collection process for the 2024 update. The document provides a comprehensive overview of the current landscape of services trade restrictiveness, offering valuable information for policymakers, researchers, and businesses involved in international services trade. It is accompanied by statistical data and maps, and is accessible via DOI and ISBN, facilitating further research and analysis. The inclusion of data for Israel reflects the organization's commitment to providing a global perspective on trade issues, while acknowledging the sensitive geopolitical context.
