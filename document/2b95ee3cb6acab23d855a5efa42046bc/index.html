Agile Project Management with Kanban

Authors: Eric Brechner, James Watzky

This book, "Agile Project Management with Kanban," authored by Eric Brechner with a contribution from James Watzky, published by Microsoft Press, provides a comprehensive guide to utilizing the Kanban methodology within an Agile project management framework. The book delves into the core principles of Kanban, emphasizing visualization, limiting work in progress, managing flow, and continuous improvement. It explores how Kanban can be effectively applied to various project types and industries, offering practical techniques and real-world examples. The text details the key elements of Kanban, including the Kanban board, WIP limits, flow metrics, and cycle time reduction strategies. It also discusses how to integrate Kanban with other Agile practices, such as Scrum, to create a hybrid approach that best suits a team's needs. Furthermore, the book addresses common challenges and pitfalls associated with Kanban implementation and provides guidance on how to overcome them. It covers topics such as team roles, workflow design, and metrics tracking. The book emphasizes the importance of continuous feedback and adaptation, encouraging teams to regularly review and refine their Kanban processes.
