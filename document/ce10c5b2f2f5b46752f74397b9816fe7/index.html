EU unveils Competitiveness Compass (EC)

Authors: European Commission

The European Commission has released the Competitiveness Compass (EC), a strategic document outlining a blueprint for Europe to become a global leader in innovation, sustainability, and economic growth, primarily through the advancement of Artificial Intelligence. The Compass identifies four key enablers: Regulatory Simplification, aiming to reduce administrative burdens for businesses (25% for firms and 35% for SMEs), a Stronger Single Market focused on removing barriers and accelerating standard-setting, Financing Growth through the establishment of a European Savings & Investment Union to direct capital towards innovation, and Skills & Jobs, which prioritizes building a ‘Union of Skills’ to ensure Europe’s workforce remains adaptable to future technological advancements. A central theme is addressing the innovation gap with the US and China, specifically by fostering start-ups, venture capital, and research and development, particularly within the AI and deep-tech sectors. The document highlights the importance of the AI Act and the AI Continent Initiative in driving European leadership in AI, emphasizing ethical development, the creation of necessary infrastructure, and the application of AI across various industrial sectors. Furthermore, the strategy addresses concerns regarding economic security by reducing reliance on critical resources and bolstering supply chains to enhance resilience in a globally fragmented economic landscape. The document’s overall goal is to position Europe as a frontrunner in technological innovation, particularly in AI, while simultaneously safeguarding its economic interests and values. The Compass represents a comprehensive approach to strategic development, integrating technological advancement with broader economic and societal considerations.
