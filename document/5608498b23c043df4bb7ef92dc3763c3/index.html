AI Security Risk Assessment Best practices and guidance to secure AI systems

Authors: Will Pearce, Hyrum Anderson, Ram Shankar Siva Kumar, Neil Coles, Andrew Paverd, Andrew Marshall, Aashish Bhateja, Alex Sutton

This document provides best practices and guidance for securing AI systems, focusing on a comprehensive risk assessment approach. It begins with an executive summary and an introduction, outlining the key areas of concern for AI security. The core of the document is structured around a detailed examination of various stages within an AI system's lifecycle. It starts with data collection, emphasizing the importance of identifying and managing sensitive data types, ensuring data integrity, and controlling data access. The document then delves into data processing, covering processing pipelines and dataset aperture. A significant portion is dedicated to model training, including model design, training methodologies, and model selection, alongside crucial model versioning practices. Following training, the document addresses model deployment, advocating for rigorous security testing and compliance reviews. Furthermore, it stresses the need for continuous system monitoring through log analysis. Finally, it outlines incident management procedures, defining roles and responsibilities, and incorporates business continuity planning. The document is presented as a ‘living document’ and encourages ongoing feedback and collaboration. The authors include a diverse team of experts, encompassing software development, architecture, data science, systems engineering, and research, reflecting a holistic approach to AI security. The document's purpose is to equip organizations with the knowledge and strategies necessary to mitigate risks associated with AI systems, promoting responsible AI development and deployment. It’s a practical guide designed to be used throughout the entire AI system development and operational lifecycle.
