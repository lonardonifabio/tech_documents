AIGN AI Governance Culture Framework 1.0

Authors: Patrick Upmann

The AIGN AI Governance Culture Framework 1.0, released in July 2025, addresses the growing concern of AI system failures stemming from organizational and cultural deficiencies rather than technical issues. The framework recognizes that current governance models based solely on compliance are insufficient, highlighting that 72% of AI-related failures originate from cultural or organizational gaps (PwC, 2024), with only 19% of organizations actively measuring or training their governance culture (Accenture, 2023). This framework emphasizes the critical role of embedded values, reflexes, and accountability within daily practice to mitigate risk. It positions organizational culture as the primary risk factor, a driver of innovation, and a key trust signal in the rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence. The document directly responds to the increasing demands for demonstrable governance culture driven by global regulations such as the EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 42001, and the OECD. The framework represents a shift from traditional compliance-focused approaches to a more holistic strategy, aiming to create a measurable and lived governance culture. It's a foundational document for organizations seeking to responsibly develop and deploy AI systems, acknowledging the significant impact of cultural factors on AI success and risk management. The framework's goal is to provide a structured approach to building a robust and accountable AI governance culture, ultimately fostering trust and innovation within the field.
