Impact Assessment Report: Proposal for a Regulation on Harmonised Rules on Fair Access to and Use of Data (Data Act)

Authors: European Commission

This document presents the Executive Summary of the Impact Assessment Report accompanying the proposal for a Regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council on harmonised rules on fair access to and use of data, commonly known as the Data Act. The report identifies a critical problem within the European economy: the insufficient availability of data for use due to a complex interplay of factors. These include a lack of clarity surrounding data rights, significant power imbalances in negotiations, limited access to reliable cloud services, and a deficiency in cross-sector data interoperability across the EU. This situation negatively impacts a wide range of economic sectors, leading to the underutilization of data and consequently hindering consumer choice, innovation, and the effective delivery of public services. The proposal aims to address this by maximizing the value of data within the economy and society. Specifically, it seeks to empower a broader range of stakeholders to control their data while simultaneously incentivizing investment in data generation. The report emphasizes the need for EU-level action due to the predominantly cross-border nature of data value chains, highlighting the dispersed locations of data holders and users. The document underscores the importance of establishing harmonized rules to facilitate data access and utilization, ultimately contributing to a more data-driven and competitive European economy. The report's findings support the subsidiarity argument, asserting that a coordinated EU approach is necessary to overcome the challenges posed by fragmented data ecosystems.
