Artificial intelligence in asylum procedures in the EU

Authors: Costica Dumbrava

This briefing paper, produced by the European Parliamentary Research Service, examines the growing use of artificial intelligence (AI) technologies within the EU's asylum procedures. The document highlights the potential benefits of AI – namely, increased efficiency, reduced uncertainty, and improved decision-making – in managing migration and asylum cases. Specifically, AI applications are intended to alleviate pressure on national asylum systems and promote more consistent and equitable asylum decisions. However, the briefing emphasizes significant risks associated with these technologies. Inaccurate or biased AI systems pose a serious threat to the fundamental right to asylum, potentially exacerbating existing discrimination and eroding established procedural safeguards. The impact of AI depends heavily on the specific applications employed, their integration into asylum workflows, and the interaction between decision-makers and these technologies. Currently, several Member States are experimenting with AI for tasks such as dialect recognition to verify applicant information and origin, as well as name transliteration. The document underscores that while the deployment of AI in EU asylum systems is nascent, a robust debate concerning the legal, social, and ethical ramifications is already underway. The report implicitly calls for careful consideration and regulation to mitigate the risks and ensure that AI is used responsibly and ethically within the asylum process, upholding human rights and procedural fairness. It suggests a need for ongoing monitoring and evaluation of AI applications to identify and address potential biases and unintended consequences. The document’s core message is that while AI offers possibilities, its implementation in asylum contexts demands a cautious and rights-based approach.
