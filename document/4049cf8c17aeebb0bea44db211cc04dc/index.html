The AI Risk Repository: A Comprehensive Meta-Review, Database, and Taxonomy of Risks From Artiﬁcial Intelligence

Authors: Peter Slatter, Alexander K. Saeri, Emily A. C. Grundy, Jess Graham, Michael Noetel, Risto Uuk, James Dao, Soroush Pour, Stephen Casper, Neil Thompson

This research paper introduces "The AI Risk Repository," a comprehensive resource designed to address the significant lack of shared understanding surrounding the risks associated with Artificial Intelligence (AI). Recognizing that this ambiguity hinders effective discussion, research, and response strategies, the authors have created a living database containing 1612 risks derived from 65 taxonomies. The repository utilizes a systematic review process, combining structured classifications with expert consultation, to develop its taxonomies. The core of the repository is a searchable database accessible through a dedicated website and online spreadsheets, allowing for flexible filtering and ongoing updates. The project’s origins lie within the MIT FutureTech and Ready Research teams, with contributions from the School of Psychology at The University of Queensland, the Future of Life Institute, KU Leuven, Harmony Intelligence, and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology’s Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory. The paper emphasizes the need for a standardized framework to navigate the complex and evolving landscape of AI risks, aiming to facilitate collaboration among academics, policymakers, AI companies, and the public. The methodology employed—a best-fit framework synthesis—highlights a rigorous approach to consolidating diverse risk classifications into a readily accessible and adaptable resource. Ultimately, the repository represents a crucial step towards proactive risk management within the rapidly advancing field of AI. The project's creation directly responds to the growing concern among various stakeholders regarding the potential negative consequences of unchecked AI development and deployment.
