Your Brain on ChatGPT: Accumulation of Cognitive Debt when Using an AI Assistant for Essay Writing Task

Authors: Nataliya Kosmyna, Eugene Hauptmann, Ye Tong Yuan, Jessica Situ, Xian-Hao Liao, Ashly Vivian Beresnitzky, Iris Braunstein, Pattie Maes

This research investigates the cognitive impact of utilizing Large Language Models (LLMs), specifically ChatGPT, within the context of essay writing. The study, authored by researchers from MIT Media Lab and Wellesley College, explores the concept of 'cognitive debt' – the mental strain incurred when relying on AI assistance for complex tasks. Participants were divided into three groups: an LLM group utilizing ChatGPT, a Search Engine group, and a 'Brain-only' group who wrote essays without any external tools. The experiment involved three sessions with consistent group assignments, followed by a fourth session where the LLM group was instructed to abstain from using any tools, effectively transitioning from LLM-assisted writing to a purely cognitive process. The core methodology centers around analyzing brainwave activity, specifically the alpha band, using a Direct Transfer Function (dDTF) EEG analysis. The researchers aim to quantify the differences in cognitive load and neural responses between these groups, providing insights into the potential drawbacks of over-reliance on AI tools for creative and demanding tasks. The study’s findings, presented through p-values, highlight the significance of the observed differences, suggesting a substantial cognitive burden associated with using LLMs for essay writing. The research contributes to a broader understanding of the human-AI interaction dynamics and the potential for cognitive fatigue when outsourcing cognitive processes to artificial intelligence.
