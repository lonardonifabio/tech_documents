A playbook for crafting AI strategy

Authors: Adam Green, Teresa Elsey

This report, "A playbook for crafting AI strategy," produced by MIT Technology Review Insights in partnership with Boomi, provides guidance for organizations seeking to move beyond initial AI pilots and experiments towards comprehensive, enterprise-wide AI deployments. The report’s foundation is built upon a global survey of 205 C-suite and senior data executives across a diverse range of industries and countries. The survey, conducted in March 2024, captured insights from leaders within eleven key sectors, including financial services, manufacturing, IT and telecommunications, consumer goods and retail, pharmaceutical and health care, government, and others. The research methodology incorporates both quantitative data from the survey and qualitative insights gleaned from in-depth interviews with business leaders specializing in data and AI. The report emphasizes the importance of strategic planning and execution for successful AI adoption. It highlights the need for organizations to shift their focus from isolated projects to a holistic approach that integrates AI across their operations. The document is editorially independent, reflecting the views of MIT Technology Review Insights, and acknowledges contributions from various executives and experts, including Kevin Collins, Amy Machado, Matt McLarty, and SP Singh, who provided valuable insights during the research process. The survey sample represents a significant effort to gather perspectives from a broad spectrum of organizations, ensuring the report’s relevance and applicability to a wide range of industries and business contexts. The report’s findings and recommendations are intended to assist organizations in navigating the complexities of AI implementation and maximizing its potential for driving business value.
