EU AI Act: Codifying Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI

Authors: EU, Stanford University

This document excerpt outlines key aspects of the EU AI Act, a landmark piece of legislation aimed at regulating artificial intelligence systems within the European Union. It focuses on the removal of prohibited AI systems from the EU market, establishing a deadline of February 2, 2025, for this action. The document defines AI as a ‘machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy and that may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment and that, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers, from the input it receives, how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments.’ The core of the document centers around a risk-based approach to AI regulation, categorizing AI systems into three tiers: prohibited, high-risk, and minimal or no risk. Prohibited AI systems include those employing subliminal techniques and certain scraping activities. High-risk AI systems, such as those used in recruitment or medical devices, require compliance with the AI Act and undergo ex-ante conformity assessments. The document highlights the ‘Transparency’ and ‘Impersonation’ risks associated with AI, particularly concerning chatbots and deepfakes. It details the regulatory framework, including the need for transparency and information obligations for high-risk systems. The document emphasizes the importance of compliance with the EU AI Act, which is designed to foster trustworthy AI development and deployment while mitigating potential harms. The excerpt also touches upon the different categories of AI systems – prohibited, permitted with transparency obligations, and permitted without restrictions – indicating a nuanced approach to regulation. The document’s purpose is to provide a foundational understanding of the EU AI Act and its implications for AI development and deployment within the EU.
