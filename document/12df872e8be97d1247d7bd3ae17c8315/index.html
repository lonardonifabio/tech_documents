AI Risk Repository Update - April 2025

Authors: MIT

This document announces an update to the AI Risk Repository, a comprehensive database established at MIT (airisk.mit.edu) designed to identify and classify risks associated with AI systems. The repository, as of April 2025, contains over 1600 identified AI risks compiled from 65 different published frameworks. The update introduces several new frameworks, including the International Scientific Report on the Safety of Advanced AI, a collaborative, human-centred taxonomy of AI, algorithmic, and automation harms, and a multi-agent risk assessment. Furthermore, the repository includes a taxonomy of systemic risks from general-purpose AI, a framework for hazard management, and a benchmark developed by MLCommons called AILUMINATE (v1.0). The database is structured around three key components: a core database of risks, a collection of frameworks, and a benchmark. The document provides access to the updated repository database and associated taxonomies, as well as information on the Paperpile folder for bibliographic resources. The goal of the AI Risk Repository is to provide a centralized resource for understanding and mitigating the potential risks posed by advanced AI systems, supporting research, development, and deployment efforts. The inclusion of human-centered taxonomies and a hazard management framework highlights a commitment to responsible AI development. The MLCommons benchmark, AILUMINATE, adds a quantitative element to risk assessment, furthering the repository's utility. This update signifies ongoing efforts to maintain a robust and evolving resource for the AI risk landscape.
