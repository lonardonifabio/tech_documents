Analysis of EU AI Office stakeholder consultations: defining AI systems and prohibited applications

Authors: CEPS, European Commission

This final study report, commissioned by the European Commission’s Directorate-General for Communications Networks, Content and Technology (CNECT) Artificial Intelligence Office, delves into the stakeholder consultations conducted by the EU AI Office. The primary objective of the analysis is to establish a clear definition of AI systems and identify prohibited applications within the context of the EU's AI strategy. The report meticulously examines the input received from various stakeholders, including industry representatives, researchers, civil society organizations, and policymakers. It aims to provide a robust framework for understanding the diverse perspectives surrounding AI development and deployment. The study focuses on translating the complex discussions into actionable insights, contributing to the development of effective regulatory measures. A key component of the analysis involves categorizing the different types of AI systems discussed, assessing the associated risks, and proposing potential safeguards. The report also considers the ethical implications of AI, addressing concerns related to bias, fairness, transparency, and accountability. Furthermore, it investigates the potential impact of AI on employment and the economy, offering recommendations for mitigating negative consequences and fostering innovation. The research incorporates a detailed examination of the legal and regulatory landscape surrounding AI, aligning with the broader goals of the European Union's AI strategy. The document’s findings are intended to inform policy decisions and guide the development of a harmonized approach to AI governance across the EU. It’s crucial to note that this report represents the authors' views and does not necessarily reflect the official opinion of the European Commission. The Commission does not guarantee the accuracy of the data presented. The report is subject to the Commission’s reuse policy, allowing for its use under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International license, provided appropriate credit is given. The study’s conclusions are presented with a strong emphasis on providing a practical and evidence-based approach to shaping the future of AI within the European Union.
