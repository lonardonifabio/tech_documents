AI for Teachers: an Open Textbook

Authors: Colin Delahiguera, Jotsna Iyer

This open textbook, "AI for Teachers," explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into education. It's divided into four parts, beginning with foundational concepts like why teachers should learn about AI, followed by an examination of how AI is already present in education and its potential benefits. The second part focuses on practical information gathering techniques, specifically utilizing search engines and machine learning. The core of the book then delves into managing learning with smart learning management systems and learning analytics, incorporating data mining and data-based systems. A significant portion is dedicated to the ethical considerations surrounding data, including personal identity and bias. Finally, the book explores personalization of learning through adaptive learning systems and analyzes how platforms like YouTube utilize AI to learn user behavior. The text provides a comprehensive overview of AI concepts and their application within the educational landscape, emphasizing both the opportunities and challenges presented by this rapidly evolving technology. The book aims to equip educators with the knowledge and understanding necessary to leverage AI effectively and responsibly in their teaching practices.
