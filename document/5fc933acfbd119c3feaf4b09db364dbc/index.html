AI Liability Along the Value Chain

Authors: Beatriz Botero Arcila

This policy report, titled "AI Liability Along the Value Chain," delves into the complex legal and ethical considerations surrounding liability in the development, deployment, and use of artificial intelligence. The document systematically examines the challenges posed by the multi-faceted nature of AI value chains, where numerous actors – including developers, manufacturers, distributors, and users – contribute to the creation and operation of AI systems. It begins with an overview of current approaches to AI accountability and liability, highlighting the difficulties in assigning responsibility when AI systems cause harm. A core focus is on the ‘many hands’ problem, where the distributed nature of AI value chains makes it difficult to pinpoint accountability. The report then explores the roles and responsibilities of each participant within these chains, considering the influence of technical factors, contractual agreements, and market power dynamics. It critically analyzes different liability frameworks, including fault liability versus strict liability, and investigates strategies for dividing liability among multiple parties. A significant portion is dedicated to the challenges of information asymmetries and the burden of proof in AI-related legal disputes. The report specifically addresses the EU's approach to AI liability, referencing the AI Act, the Revised Product Liability Directive, and the Artificial Intelligence Directive (AILD). The analysis considers how these regulations attempt to address the unique risks associated with AI. Ultimately, the document provides a preliminary framework for understanding and navigating the evolving legal landscape of AI liability, acknowledging the need for ongoing discussion and adaptation as AI technology continues to advance. The report concludes with a summary of key findings and points to further research and development in this critical area.
