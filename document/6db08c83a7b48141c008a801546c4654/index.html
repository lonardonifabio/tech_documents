Regulating Machine Learning: where do we stand?

Authors: AXA

This document, produced by AXA, explores the evolving landscape of regulating machine learning (ML) technologies. It highlights the increasing adoption of AI and ML across industries and acknowledges the significant opportunities these technologies present. However, the emergence of ML also poses challenges to existing regulatory frameworks. The core of the document focuses on the EU's approach, specifically referencing the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). The GDPR, enacted in May 2018, indirectly regulates ML by emphasizing data protection and privacy, particularly concerning the vast amounts of data utilized by ML algorithms. AXA, as a data controller, is obligated to implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to comply with GDPR's data protection principles. The document's primary objective is to raise awareness among key stakeholders – including Chief Data Officers (CDOs), Data Protection Officers (DPOs), data scientists, actuaries, and others – regarding the inherent risks associated with large-scale ML deployments. These risks are categorized as fairness, reliability, and explainability. The document details how current regulations, primarily the GDPR, are attempting to address these challenges. It aims to foster proactive thinking and anticipation of future regulatory changes within the field of machine learning, emphasizing the unique differences between ML algorithms and traditional algorithms, particularly their relationship to data. The document intends to provide a foundational understanding of the risks and regulatory considerations surrounding the use of ML.
