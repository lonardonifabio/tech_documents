Mathematics for Machine Learning

Authors: Marc Peter Deisenroth, A. Aldo Faisal, Cheng Soon On

This textbook, "Mathematics for Machine Learning" by Deisenroth et al., addresses the significant challenge of integrating the mathematical foundations required for understanding and applying machine learning. It recognizes that the necessary mathematical tools – including linear algebra, analytic geometry, matrix decompositions, vector calculus, optimization, and probability & statistics – are often scattered across various courses, making it difficult for students and professionals in data science or computer science to gain a cohesive understanding. The book aims to bridge this gap by providing a self-contained resource that introduces these concepts with minimal prerequisites. It focuses on deriving four core machine learning methods: linear regression, principal component analysis, Gaussian mixture models, and support vector machines. The book is designed for both those with a pre-existing mathematical background, offering a starting point for machine learning texts, and for those new to the mathematics, providing practical experience and intuition. Each chapter is supplemented with worked examples and exercises to reinforce learning. Furthermore, programming tutorials are available on the book’s website to support practical application. Marc Peter Deisenroth, the Senior Lecturer in Statistical Machine Learning at Imperial College London, and A. Aldo Faisal, who leads the Brain & Behaviour Lab at Imperial College London, are the key contributors to this resource. The book’s approach emphasizes a practical, hands-on learning experience, making it a valuable tool for anyone seeking to understand the mathematical underpinnings of machine learning.
