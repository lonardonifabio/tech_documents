Overview of artificial intelligence regulation

Authors: DETEC (OFCOM), FDFA (State Secretariat, Europe Division)

This document, dated February 12, 2025, presents an overview of artificial intelligence (AI) regulation commissioned by the Federal Council of Switzerland. It highlights the rapid development of AI, particularly generative AI applications like ChatGPT, and acknowledges the significant opportunities AI presents for Switzerland as a business and innovation hub. However, it also recognizes the emerging legal challenges, specifically concerning the transparency and traceability of AI-based decisions. The report emphasizes the growing international regulatory landscape, referencing initiatives such as the Council of Europe's AI Convention and the EU's AI Act. Currently, Switzerland lacks specific AI legislation, prompting a critical assessment of the existing legal framework. The document underscores AI's prioritization within the Federal Council's 2023-2027 legislative plan, necessitating a fundamental decision regarding Switzerland's regulatory approach. DETEC (OFCOM) and the FDFA (State Secretariat, Europe Division) were tasked with producing this overview, intending to provide a foundation for the Federal Council's forthcoming decision. The report essentially serves as an initial assessment and recommendation for future regulatory action, acknowledging the urgency and complexity of the situation.
