Reimagining AI2025: For Environmental Justice and Creativity

Authors: Jess Reia, MC Forelle, Yingchong Wang

This collection of essays, titled "Reimagining AI2025: For Environmental Justice and Creativity," is edited by Jess Reia, MC Forelle, and Yingchong Wang. It brings together a diverse group of contributors exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence, environmental justice, and creative practices. The book delves into critical questions surrounding the development and deployment of AI, specifically focusing on its potential impact on marginalized communities and the creative industries. Contributors represent a range of disciplines, including computer science, environmental studies, sociology, and art. The project is spearheaded by the Digital Technology for Democracy Lab at the University of Virginia. The work is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 International license, promoting open access and collaborative engagement. The collection aims to foster a more equitable and imaginative approach to AI development, recognizing the urgent need to address environmental injustices and harness AI's creative potential for positive social change. It’s a significant effort to consider the ethical and societal implications of AI, particularly within the context of environmental concerns and creative endeavors. The contributors offer diverse perspectives and methodologies, reflecting the complexity of the issues at hand. The book’s focus on ‘Reimagining AI’ suggests a deliberate effort to move beyond conventional approaches and explore innovative solutions. The inclusion of a research team and revision process indicates a rigorous and collaborative approach to the project’s development. The design and layout are overseen by Nela Díaz, further highlighting the attention to detail and presentation of this important work. The project's commitment to open licensing underscores its dedication to accessibility and widespread dissemination of knowledge.
