Artificial Intelligence and Digital Transformation Competencies for Civil Servants

Authors: Working Group Report on AI Capacity Building

This report, titled "Artificial Intelligence and Digital Transformation Competencies for Civil Servants," outlines the essential skills and knowledge required for government professionals to effectively navigate and leverage the opportunities presented by artificial intelligence and digital transformation. The document focuses on building AI capacity within the public sector, providing a framework for understanding and developing digital competencies. It is structured around three key competency domains: Digital Competencies in Government, Data Use and Governance, and Digital Management and Execution. The report begins with a Table of Contents and Acknowledgements, followed by a Foreword and an Executive Summary. The core of the document details a comprehensive Digital Competency Framework, broken down into specific areas such as systems thinking, strategic foresight, agile strategy, digital literacy, data-driven decision making, open data, privacy, security, and ethical frameworks. Furthermore, it emphasizes the importance of people-centricity, iteration, agile execution, and digital leadership. A significant portion of the report is dedicated to exploring the challenges and opportunities of digital transformation within government, specifically highlighting AI's potential as a tool to address public sector challenges. The document advocates for a holistic approach to digital transformation, recognizing the need for both technical skills and broader competencies like creativity, adaptability, and curiosity. It underscores the importance of aligning digital strategies with ethical considerations and legal frameworks. The report's ultimate goal is to equip civil servants with the necessary tools and understanding to drive innovation and improve public services through the strategic application of AI and digital technologies.
