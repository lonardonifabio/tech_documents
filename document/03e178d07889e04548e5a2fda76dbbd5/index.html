Where’s the Value in AI?

Authors: Nicolas de Bellefonds, Tauseef Charanya, Marc Roman Franke, Jessica Apotheker, Patrick Forth, Michael Grebe, Amanda Luther, Romain de Laubier, Vladimir Lukic, Mary Martin, Clemens Nopp, Joe Sassine

This report, published in October 2024 by the Boston Consulting Group, addresses the persistent question of where the tangible value lies within the rapidly evolving field of Artificial Intelligence (AI). Despite significant investments from CEOs, including funding, talent acquisition, and pilot programs, only a minority of companies (26%) have successfully progressed beyond the initial proof-of-concept stage and are demonstrably generating value from AI initiatives. The report delves into the key factors driving success for AI leaders, highlighting the distinct behaviors and strategies employed by those who are achieving results. It identifies surprising sources of value within AI, exploring applications both within core business functions and across diverse sectors. The analysis reveals that value generation is not uniform across industries, with sector-specific considerations playing a crucial role. The report provides a ‘playbook’ for companies seeking to win with AI, outlining the capabilities required for success and offering practical guidance on how to overcome common challenges. It includes case studies of two companies that have effectively implemented this playbook, showcasing real-world applications and outcomes. The document specifically addresses the impact of generative AI (GenAI) and emphasizes the need for a strategic approach to maximize value creation. Ultimately, the report aims to provide actionable insights for organizations looking to navigate the complexities of AI and unlock its full potential, moving beyond the hype to focus on demonstrable results. The analysis underscores the importance of understanding both the technological advancements and the strategic considerations necessary for successful AI adoption.
