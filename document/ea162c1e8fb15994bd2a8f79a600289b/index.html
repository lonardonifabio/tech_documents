Building Blocks of Machine Learning Methods

Authors: Unknown

This book provides a foundational understanding of common machine learning methods, presented as a practical 'toolbox' for machine learning engineers. It focuses on delivering the core concepts and derivations of these methods, aiming to make readers comfortable with utilizing them effectively. The book is structured around individual machine learning methods or groups of methods, with each chapter corresponding to a single tool within the ML toolbox. A key element of the book's approach is to derive the methods from first principles, both theoretically and through code. Each chapter is meticulously broken down into three sections: a 'Concepts' section introducing the methods conceptually and deriving their results mathematically; a 'Constructions' section demonstrating how to build the methods; and a practical approach to understanding the underlying mechanics. The book's goal is to equip readers with the ability to not just use these methods, but to understand their origins and how they are constructed, fostering a deeper level of competence and control within the field of machine learning. The book is designed to be a valuable resource for those entering the field or seeking a solid grounding in the fundamental techniques.
