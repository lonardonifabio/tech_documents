Navigating the impact of AI systems in the workplace: strengths and loopholes of the EU AI Act from a labour perspective

Authors: Chiara Cristofolini

This research paper, authored by Chiara Cristofolini, critically examines the potential impacts of artificial intelligence (AI) systems within the workplace, specifically focusing on the EU AI Act and its implications from a labor law perspective. The document begins with an introduction recognizing AI as both an opportunity and a risk for employment. It then delves into the European Union's regulatory approach to AI, outlining the objectives and legal foundations of the EU AI Act. A key focus is on the ‘risk-based’ approach and its consequences for the deployment of employment-related AI systems. The paper assesses the obligations placed on providers of high-risk AI systems, alongside analyzing the rights and responsibilities of employers who utilize these systems. Furthermore, it investigates the transparency and information requirements associated with AI deployment, highlighting both the strengths and weaknesses of the current framework. The research provides a critical evaluation of the EU AI Act’s potential to safeguard labor rights and mitigate the risks associated with AI adoption in the workplace. The paper’s analysis is grounded in a labor law perspective, considering the impact on employment, worker rights, and the overall regulatory landscape. The document’s structure includes an introduction, a detailed exploration of the EU’s regulatory approach, an assessment of the risk-based approach, an examination of provider obligations, an analysis of employer responsibilities, and concluding remarks. It’s a double-blind peer-reviewed article submitted to the Italian Labour Law e-Journal. The research contributes to the ongoing debate surrounding the ethical and legal considerations of AI implementation in the workplace, advocating for a balanced approach that prioritizes worker protection and responsible innovation.
