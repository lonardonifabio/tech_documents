AI for Teachers: an Open Textbook

Authors: Colin Delahiguera, Jotsna Iyer

This open textbook, "AI for Teachers," explores the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) into education. It's divided into four parts, beginning with foundational concepts like why teachers should learn about AI, followed by an examination of AI's prevalence and existing applications in education. The second part focuses on information retrieval and search engine technology, including machine learning and indexing. The core of the book then delves into managing learning with smart systems and learning analytics, incorporating data mining and data-based systems. A significant portion is dedicated to personalized learning, specifically adaptive learning systems and how platforms like YouTube utilize AI to learn user behavior. The text addresses critical considerations such as data privacy, bias, and fairness in AI-driven educational systems. The book provides a practical guide for educators seeking to understand and leverage AI's potential to enhance teaching and learning experiences. It covers a wide range of topics, from search engine ranking to data-based systems, offering insights into both the technical aspects and the ethical implications of AI in education. The open textbook format promotes accessibility and encourages educators to explore and experiment with AI tools and techniques. The content is structured to build understanding progressively, starting with basic concepts and moving towards more advanced topics. Ultimately, the book aims to equip teachers with the knowledge and skills necessary to navigate the evolving landscape of AI in education.
