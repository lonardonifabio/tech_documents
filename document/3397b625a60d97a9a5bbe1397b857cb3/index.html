The International Finance Forum (IFF) is an international, independent, non-profit, non-governmental organization established in October 2003. It was founded by G20 countries and prominent international organizations including the United Nations, the World Bank, and the International Monetary Fund. The IFF, also known as ‘F20 (Finance 20)’, serves as a permanent institution dedicated to fostering strategic dialogue, exchange, and cooperation across fields like global finance and public policy. Its core mission is to establish a platform for practical innovation, academic research, and talent cultivation, aligning with the overarching principle of ‘Comprehensive and Sustainable Development – New Capital, New Value, New World’. The organization operates with an emphasis on independence, objectivity, foresight, and inclusiveness, facilitating global financial cooperation and exchanges. The IFF’s activities encompass in-depth research on global economic and financial trends, aiming to provide strategic insights and contribute to sustainable development. The organization’s structure and operations are designed to ensure transparency and fairness, promoting a collaborative environment for international stakeholders. Essentially, the IFF acts as a multilateral dialogue platform and academic think-tank, focused on shaping the future of global finance and related policy areas. The organization’s long-term goal is to build itself into a world-class academic think-tank and multilateral dialogue platform with strategic insight.
