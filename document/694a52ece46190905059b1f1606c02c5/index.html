QuantumBlack, AI by McKinsey: Europe’s AI Opportunity

Authors: Alexander Sukharevsky, Eric Hazan, Sven Smit, Marc-Antoine de la Chevasnerie, Marc de Jong, Solveigh Hieronimus, Jan Mischke, Guillaume Dagorret

This report, produced by QuantumBlack in collaboration with McKinsey, focuses on Europe’s potential within the burgeoning generative AI (gen AI) economy. The document advocates for a comprehensive, three-lens approach – assessing adoption, creation, and energy – to fully capitalize on gen AI’s economic opportunities. It highlights a significant gap in gen AI adoption across European organizations, with lags of 45 to 70 percent compared to their US counterparts. Despite this, the report argues that the window of opportunity remains open due to the nascent stage of the technology and the untapped productivity gains. Specifically, the analysis identifies that while Europe holds a leading position in AI semiconductor equipment, it lags significantly in other key segments of the gen AI value chain, including foundation models, AI applications, and AI services, holding below 5% market share. The report emphasizes the need for strategic investment and policy interventions to address these shortcomings and unlock Europe’s potential within the rapidly evolving gen AI landscape. The document suggests that a failure to address these gaps could hinder Europe’s competitiveness and limit the potential productivity gains associated with gen AI. The analysis underscores the importance of a coordinated effort across various sectors to drive innovation and establish a stronger presence within the global gen AI market. The report’s findings are intended to inform European policymakers and business leaders in their strategic decisions regarding AI investment and development.
