Governing AI Agents Under the EU AI Act

Authors: Amin Oueslati, Robin Staes-Polet

This document, published by The Future Society in June 2025, analyzes the implications of the EU AI Act for governing AI agents. It delves into the evolving landscape of AI agents, focusing on trends, characterization, and systemic risks associated with GPAI models and high-risk systems. The report systematically examines the applicability of the EU AI Act to AI agents, categorizing them based on their architecture and potential risks. Key areas of investigation include risk mechanisms, the impact of Chapter V and III of the AI Act, and the role of enforcement authorities. A significant portion of the document is dedicated to establishing a governance framework along the value chain, proposing risk assessments and outlining illustrative examples of risk identification. The authors address open questions and working assumptions related to the Act's implementation, particularly concerning the allocation of obligations across the AI agent development and deployment lifecycle. The analysis is crucial for understanding how the EU AI Act will shape the development and operation of increasingly sophisticated AI agents, highlighting the need for proactive risk management and a robust governance structure. The document provides a detailed exploration of the challenges and opportunities presented by AI agents within the context of the EU's regulatory framework, aiming to inform stakeholders and contribute to a responsible and ethical approach to AI development.
