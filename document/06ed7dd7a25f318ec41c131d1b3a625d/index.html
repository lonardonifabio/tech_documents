Risk Practice: Building the internal-audit function of the future

Authors: Ida Kristensen, Merlina Manocaran, Edmond Sannini, Haris Usman

This document, published in February 2021, addresses the evolving role of internal audit (IA) functions within organizations, particularly in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic and its associated disruptions. It highlights that while organizations initially focused on immediate priorities, a recalibration is now necessary to address long-term uncertainty and complexity. The primary function of IA remains safeguarding organizational assets and reputations, and supporting operational sustainability, but these have been significantly challenged by the pandemic. The rise of home-based working has increased asset risks, while the disrupted business environment has amplified uncertainty regarding reputation and sustainability. IA functions are now tasked with maintaining secure oversight while adapting to this dynamic risk landscape. Traditionally, IA focuses on assurance around business processes and controls, but the increased complexity and volume of risks, particularly in areas like governance, risk appetite, and control culture (as seen at financial institutions), require IA to deliver a broader range of services quickly. The document emphasizes the need for internal audit to proactively address these amplified risks and uncertainties, demonstrating a shift from reactive to a more strategic and adaptable approach. The core challenge is to ensure continued effectiveness of the IA function amidst a rapidly changing and increasingly complex risk environment.
