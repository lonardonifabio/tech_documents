A Comprehensive Guide to Machine Learning

Authors: Soroush Nasiriany, Garrett Thomas, William Wang, Alex Yang

This document serves as a comprehensive guide to Machine Learning, specifically designed for the CS 189 course at UC Berkeley. Created by Soroush Nasiriany, Garrett Thomas, William Wang, and Alex Yang, the guide was initially developed in Fall 2017 and has evolved through contributions from Professors Anant Sahai, Stella Yu, and Jennifer Listgarten, alongside the inspiration drawn from Jonathan Shewchuk's machine learning notes. The guide aims to share knowledge with UC Berkeley students and the broader public, potentially attracting interest from other universities. It provides definitions and notation related to machine learning, including sets (like R and Rn), vector spaces, Kronecker delta, and function gradients and Hessians. The document includes information on probability distributions and probability densities. The authors encourage reporting of errors and offer contact information for redistribution purposes. The guide's availability is linked at http://www.eecs189.org/ and http://snasiriany.me/cs189/. It's a foundational resource for understanding core concepts within the machine learning curriculum.
