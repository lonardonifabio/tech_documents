Privacy, Data Protection and Data-driven Technologies

Authors: Martin Ebers

This book explores the complex intersection of privacy, data protection, and emerging data-driven technologies. It features contributions from leading experts in law and technology, focusing on the significant challenges posed by advancements like artificial intelligence (AI), blockchain, machine-to-machine (M2M) communication, and technologies such as facial recognition and personal care robots. The core argument centers on evaluating the adequacy of existing European Union (EU) data protection laws in light of these new technologies, specifically addressing the balance between fostering innovation and safeguarding individual privacy. The book delves into a wide range of privacy concerns, including targeted advertising and the governance of algorithms. Martin Ebers, the author, is a prominent figure in the field, holding positions as President of the Robotics & AI Law Society (RAILS) and Associate Professor of IT Law at the University of Tartu (Estonia). His extensive background includes over 100 international conference presentations, membership in numerous research networks, and a prolific publication record of 16 books and over 120 articles primarily focused on AI, law, and related areas.
