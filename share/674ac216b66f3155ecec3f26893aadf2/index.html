Conformity Assessments under the EU AI Act: A step-by step guide

Authors: Andreea Serban, Vasileios Rovilos, Katerina Demetzou

This document provides a comprehensive, step-by-step guide to conducting conformity assessments under the EU AI Act. Updated with the final text of the AI Act, it offers practical guidance for organizations seeking to ensure their AI systems comply with the regulations. The guide is designed to assist businesses and legal professionals in navigating the complexities of the new legislation. It details the key requirements of the AI Act, including risk classification, data governance, transparency, and accountability. The guide emphasizes the importance of a systematic approach to risk assessment and mitigation, outlining the necessary steps for demonstrating compliance. Furthermore, it addresses specific areas of concern, such as bias detection and mitigation, data security, and human oversight. The document is regularly updated to reflect the latest developments and clarifications surrounding the AI Act, ensuring that users have access to the most current information. The authors, Andreea Serban, Vasileios Rovilos, and Katerina Demetzou, developed this guide with input from Chuma Akana, and initially created it during their time at the Future of Privacy Forum. The guide is a valuable resource for anyone involved in the development, deployment, or use of AI systems within the European Union, and it is intended to promote responsible innovation and minimize legal risks. It is crucial to note that this document is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Readers are advised to consult with legal counsel to ensure compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. The guide's content is subject to revision due to ongoing advancements in AI methodologies and regulatory changes. The final text of the AI Act is incorporated into the guide, making it a critical resource for understanding and implementing the new standards.
