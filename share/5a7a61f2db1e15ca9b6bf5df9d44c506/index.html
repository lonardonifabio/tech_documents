The State of AI March 2025: How Organizations are Rewiring to Capture Value

Authors: Alex Singla, Alexander Sukharevsky, Lareina Yee, Michael Chui, Bryce Hall

This document, titled "The State of AI March 2025: How Organizations are Rewiring to Capture Value," analyzes the evolving strategies organizations are adopting to leverage generative AI (gen AI). Based on the latest McKinsey Global Survey, the report highlights that companies are proactively redesigning workflows, establishing robust governance frameworks, and addressing emerging risks associated with gen AI deployment. Key findings indicate a significant shift in organizational structure, with senior leaders taking on crucial roles in overseeing AI governance. The survey reveals that organizations are actively hiring for new AI-related positions while simultaneously retraining existing employees to facilitate AI deployment. Notably, larger companies with annual revenues exceeding $500 million are demonstrating a faster pace of change compared to smaller organizations. The document emphasizes the growing momentum in AI adoption, with over three-quarters of respondents reporting the use of AI across various business functions, with gen AI experiencing particularly rapid growth. The report underscores the importance of strategic organizational changes and proactive risk mitigation as organizations seek to maximize the value derived from gen AI investments. It suggests that the successful integration of AI requires a combination of technological advancements, skilled personnel, and a well-defined governance structure. The survey data provides valuable insights for businesses looking to navigate the complexities of the rapidly evolving AI landscape and capitalize on its potential.

Key Concepts:

  • Organizations are rewiring to capture value from gen AI.
  • Companies are redesigning workflows and elevating governance to mitigate risks and drive bottom-line impact.
  • Large companies are leading the way in AI adoption and deployment.
  • Organizations are mitigating gen-AI related risks.
  • Large organizations are more likely to manage cybersecurity and privacy risks.
  • Transformative change in business models due to AI
  • Employees spending time on new activities due to automation.
  • Organizations reducing headcount due to time saved from automation.
  • Expectations for AI-related reskilling in the next three years.
  • Industry-specific variations in workforce reduction expectations (e.g., financial services).
