Scalable AI Incident Classification

Authors: Simon Mylius, Winter Fellow at the Centre for the Governance of AI (GovAI)

This document details a proof-of-concept project focused on automating the classification of AI incidents using Large Language Models (LLMs). The work, spearheaded by Simon Mylius in collaboration with the Centre for the Governance of AI (GovAI), aims to improve the efficiency and scalability of analyzing the AI Incident Database. The core of the project involves leveraging an LLM to classify incident reports based on risk taxonomies, specifically utilizing the MIT AI Risk Taxonomies, and assessing harm severities according to the CSET Taxonomy of AI Harm. This initiative represents a critical step towards streamlining the management and understanding of AI-related incidents, particularly as the volume of reported incidents continues to grow. The project's success hinges on the LLM's ability to accurately interpret the nuances of incident reports and consistently apply the defined taxonomies and severity scales. Further investigation into the tool's performance and the factors influencing its confidence ratings is crucial for refining the process and ensuring the reliability of the automated analysis. The document serves as a foundational exploration of automated incident classification within the context of the AI Incident Database, highlighting the potential for LLMs to transform this critical data source.

Key Concepts:

  • Scalable AI Incident Classification: A system utilizing LLMs to classify AI incidents based on risk taxonomies and visualized through interactive dashboards.
  • Automated Incident Classification: The programmatic processing of raw incident reports to classify risks and assign severity scores, alongside a confidence rating.
  • Confidence Rating: The tool's assessment of the reliability of the classification process, providing a confidence score for each incident analysis.
