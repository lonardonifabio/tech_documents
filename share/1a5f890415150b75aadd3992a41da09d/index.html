Code of Practice for General -Purpose AI Models Transparency

Authors: Nuria Oliver, Rishi Bommasani

This document excerpt outlines the 'Code of Practice for General -Purpose AI Models Transparency,' a framework designed to assist organizations in complying with the requirements of the EU AI Act. Specifically, it details the Transparency Chapter, focusing on three measures signatories are expected to implement. The core of the document centers around the Model Documentation Form, a tool created to streamline the process of compiling the necessary information for downstream providers, the AI Office, and national competent authorities. The form is structured to categorize information based on its intended recipient, ensuring appropriate data sharing practices. Crucially, information destined for the AI Office or national competent authorities is subject to a request-based access system. These requests must be justified with a clear legal basis and purpose, and the scope of the request is limited to the strictly necessary items required by the AI Act at the time of the request. This layered approach aims to balance transparency with the AI Office's and national competent authorities' oversight responsibilities, ensuring compliance with the AI Act's provisions while safeguarding data privacy and operational efficiency. The document's purpose is to provide a practical guide for organizations navigating the complexities of the AI Act's transparency obligations, fostering trust and accountability in the development and deployment of AI models.

Key Concepts:

  • Signatories' transparency obligations under the AI Act
  • Implementation of Measures to comply with the AI Act
  • User-friendly Model Documentation Form for compiling information
  • Data sharing with the AI Office and national competent authorities upon request
  • Proactive information sharing for DPs
  • Request-based information access for AIO and NCAs
  • Scope of requests limited to necessary items under the AI Act
  • Compliance assessment of high-risk AI systems
  • Data rights from third parties
  • Training, testing, and validation data
