Transforming Animal Farming through Artificial Intelligence

Authors: Claudia Vinci, Members' Research Service

This briefing paper, produced by the European Parliamentary Research Service, examines the potential of artificial intelligence (AI) and the Internet of Things (IoT) to revolutionize the livestock sector. The document highlights a projected 3% increase in global meat protein consumption by 2033, which is anticipated to lead to elevated greenhouse gas emissions. AI-powered systems are presented as a key solution, enabling real-time monitoring of animal health, behavior, and welfare. These systems facilitate the early detection of disease and stress, allowing for personalized care and proactive interventions. The concept of precision livestock farming is central to the analysis, utilizing sensors, cameras, and machine learning algorithms to collect and analyze data, driving data-driven decision-making and optimized production methods. This approach aims to simultaneously increase productivity and reduce emissions, while significantly improving animal welfare. Specifically, AI can predict disease outbreaks, identify potential host reservoirs, and detect emerging disease threats, leading to timely interventions. Furthermore, AI's ability to recognize discomfort, stress, or pain in animals directly addresses welfare concerns. The briefing underscores the potential of AI to contribute to a more sustainable and efficient farming and food system. The document emphasizes the importance of integrating these technologies to mitigate the environmental impact of meat production and enhance animal well-being. The core argument is that AI offers a pathway towards a more responsible and productive livestock sector, aligning with broader sustainability goals.

Key Concepts:

  • Transforming animal farming through artificial intelligence to increase productivity and reduce environmental impact.
  • Precision livestock farming utilizes sensors and machine learning to optimize production and improve animal welfare.
  • AI's role in predicting disease outbreaks and enabling prompt intervention for improved animal health and welfare.
  • Manual observation of animal behaviour is time-consuming and subjective.
  • Changes in animal behaviour (lethargy, decreased appetite, increased vocalisation, withdrawal, unusual eating habits, heightened activity, increased breathing rate) indicate distress.
  • AI-driven monitoring systems optimize environmental factors (temperature, humidity, air quality) to improve animal welfare.
