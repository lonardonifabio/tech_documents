Principles of Data Wrangling

Authors: Tye Rattenbury, Joe Hellerstein, Jeffrey Heer, Sean Kandel, Connor Carreras

Principles of Data Wrangling is a practical guide focused on the techniques involved in data preparation, a crucial step in any data analysis or machine learning project. The book emphasizes a hands-on approach, providing readers with the knowledge and skills to effectively transform raw data into a usable format. It covers a wide range of data wrangling techniques, including data cleaning, transformation, and enrichment. The book aims to equip readers with the ability to handle messy, real-world data, addressing common challenges such as missing values, inconsistent formats, and outliers. It's designed for data analysts, data scientists, and anyone involved in the data preparation process. The content is structured to build a solid foundation in data wrangling principles and provides practical examples and exercises to reinforce learning. The book's emphasis on 'practical techniques' suggests a focus on actionable steps and real-world scenarios. It's a comprehensive resource for mastering the art of data wrangling, a skill that is increasingly vital in today's data-driven world.
