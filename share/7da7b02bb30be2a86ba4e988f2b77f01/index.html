Governing AI for Humanity

Authors: High-level Advisory Body on Artificial Intelligence

This report, released in September 2024, details the findings and recommendations of the High-level Advisory Body on Artificial Intelligence. Established in October 2023 by the United Nations Secretary-General as part of the Roadmap for Digital Cooperation, the body’s purpose is to analyze and advance recommendations for the international governance of artificial intelligence. The Advisory Body comprises members who have contributed in their individual capacities, not as representatives of their organizations, reflecting a majority consensus. The report identifies the critical need for global governance frameworks surrounding AI, highlighting existing gaps in international cooperation. It emphasizes the importance of establishing a ‘common understanding’ and ‘common ground’ amongst nations. Key enablers for harnessing AI for humanity are explored, including coherent efforts and reflections on institutional models. The report outlines significant risks and challenges associated with AI development and deployment, demanding proactive measures to mitigate potential negative consequences. Furthermore, it stresses the necessity of a coordinated global approach to ensure AI benefits all of humanity. The document serves as a call to action, urging stakeholders to address these challenges and work towards a future where AI is developed and utilized responsibly and ethically. The report’s findings are intended to inform policy discussions and guide the development of robust governance mechanisms for artificial intelligence on a global scale. The document’s creation represents a significant step towards establishing a framework for responsible AI development and deployment, acknowledging the complexities and potential pitfalls inherent in this rapidly evolving technology.

Key Concepts:

  • Need for global governance of artificial intelligence
  • Identified gaps in global AI governance
  • Promoting common understanding and cooperation in AI governance
  • A global approach to governing AI starts with a common understanding of its capabilities, opportunities, risks and uncertainties.
  • Pooling scientific knowledge is most efficient at the global level, enabling joint investment in a global public good.
  • Learning from precedents such as the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) and the United Nations Scientific Committee on the Effects of Atomic Radiation, an international, multidisciplinary scientific panel on AI could collate and catalyse leading-edge research.
  • Need for shared resources (open models) to enable inclusive AI participation.
  • Importance of global approaches in supporting AI ecosystems.
  • Role of networks in facilitating capacity development and matching partnerships.
  • Partnership with external organizations
