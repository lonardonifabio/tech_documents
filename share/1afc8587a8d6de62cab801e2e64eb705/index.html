VIP Cheatsheet: Convolutional Neural Networks

Authors: Afshine Amidi, Shervine Amidi

This document provides a concise cheatsheet outlining the fundamental concepts of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), a crucial component of deep learning. It begins by introducing CNNs as a specific type of neural network primarily composed of convolution and pooling layers. The core of the document focuses on the Convolutional layer (CONV), explaining its operation using filters and hyperparameters like filter size (F) and stride (S), resulting in feature maps or activation maps. It highlights the generalization of the convolution step to 1D and 3D cases. Furthermore, it details the Pooling layer (POOL), describing it as a downsampling operation that promotes spatial invariance, utilizing max and average pooling techniques. The document clarifies the purpose of each pooling operation ‚Äì max pooling selects the maximum value, while average pooling averages the values. It also mentions the common use of pooling layers, particularly in the LeNet architecture. Finally, the document briefly touches upon Fully Connected (FC) layers, indicating they operate on flattened inputs. This cheatsheet serves as a quick reference for understanding the key elements of CNNs, providing a foundational understanding for those new to the field or seeking a refresher on the core components.

Key Concepts:

  • Convolution operation using filters to extract features from input data.
  • Downsampling of feature maps through max or average pooling to achieve spatial invariance.
  • Flattened input for fully connected layers to process extracted features.
