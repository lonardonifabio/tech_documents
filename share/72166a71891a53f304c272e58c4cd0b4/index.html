Framing the Future of Web 3.0

Authors: Michael Ng, CFA, Alexandra Steiger, Lane Czura

This document, produced by Goldman Sachs, analyzes the potential shifts in industry structure anticipated with the emergence of Web 3.0. It posits that the global internet is transitioning from the innovation phase of Web 2.0, with established leaders. The report anticipates dramatic changes in industry structure, moving towards decentralized, more localized, and niche-focused approaches. These shifts could significantly impact investor perceptions of platform strength, industry input costs, and monetization strategies due to increased personalization and evolving media & commerce trends. The analysis highlights the potential for decentralized networks to reshape the landscape. Crucially, the document explicitly states a potential conflict of interest due to Goldman Sachs' business relationships with companies covered in its research reports, advising investors to consider this report as only one factor in their investment decisions. Furthermore, it directs readers to the Disclosure Appendix at www.gs.com/research/hedge.html for regulatory information and clarifies that analysts employed by non-US affiliates are not registered/qualified as research analysts with FINRA in the U.S. The report emphasizes the need for investors to be aware of this conflict of interest and to conduct thorough due diligence.
