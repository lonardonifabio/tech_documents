Artificial Intelligence and Transitional Justice: Framing the Connections

Authors: Amritha R Warrier, Dr Sue Anne Teo, Michelle Bouchebel, Thomas Unger

This publication, released in January 2025, explores the complex and increasingly relevant connections between Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Transitional Justice. The document frames AI's role in processing information and making decisions, particularly concerning biased online data, within the context of justice systems following periods of conflict or human rights violations. It highlights the potential for AI to both exacerbate existing inequalities and offer new tools for accountability and redress. Dr. Sue Anne Teo, the primary author, is a researcher specializing in AI and human rights at the Raoul Wallenberg Institute of Human Rights and Humanitarian Law in Sweden. Her doctoral research, conducted with support from the Europaeum Scholar program, focused on the intersection of AI and human rights law. She brings a multidisciplinary perspective, drawing on her legal expertise from the University of Copenhagen, Cambridge, and LSE. The publication is a collaborative effort, co-funded by the European Union and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Netherlands, and features contributions from the Policy and Innovation Team at Impunity Watch. The visual element, a tic-tac-toe board with human faces, serves as a symbolic representation of AI's operation on pre-existing, potentially biased data. The document’s aim is to stimulate critical discussion and research into the ethical and legal challenges posed by AI’s application within transitional justice contexts, emphasizing the need for careful consideration of bias, transparency, and accountability. It underscores the importance of integrating human rights principles into the development and deployment of AI technologies to ensure fairness and justice for all. The publication’s co-funding reflects a growing international interest in this area, recognizing the significant implications of AI for human rights and the rule of law.
