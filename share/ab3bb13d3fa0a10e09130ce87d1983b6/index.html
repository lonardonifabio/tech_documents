A Playbook for Product Managers & Business Leaders: Responsible Use of Generative AI

Authors: Genevieve Smith, Natalia Luka, Jessica Newman, Merrick Osborne, Brandie Nonneck

This playbook, developed by the BAIR Responsible AI Initiative at UC Berkeley, provides a practical guide for product managers and business leaders navigating the responsible implementation of generative AI (genAI). It addresses the critical need to proactively mitigate potential risks and harms associated with genAI, fostering trust and accountability. The document outlines 10 ‘plays’ designed to guide users through the complexities of incorporating genAI into their workflows and new products. The playbook begins with a foundational overview of the current state of genAI adoption, highlighting the challenges related to responsible use and defining what ‘responsibility’ entails within this context. It then delves into the specifics of each ‘play,’ detailing the key stakeholders involved, implementation strategies, relevant case studies, and available tools and resources. The core objective is to equip decision-makers with the knowledge and frameworks necessary to harness the transformative potential of genAI while upholding ethical standards and minimizing potential negative consequences. The document emphasizes a proactive approach, recognizing that responsible genAI adoption is not merely a compliance issue, but a strategic imperative for building sustainable and trustworthy AI solutions. Ultimately, the playbook serves as a valuable resource for organizations seeking to integrate genAI effectively and ethically, ensuring alignment with the principles of responsible AI development and deployment. The BAIR Responsible AI Initiative’s involvement underscores the importance of a research-backed and academically informed approach to this rapidly evolving field.
