OWASP Top 10 for LLM Apps & Gen AI Agentic Security Initiative

Authors: OWASP

This document, Version 1.0 released in February 2025, presents the OWASP Top 10 for Large Language Model (LLM) Applications and Gen AI Agentic Security Initiative. It focuses on identifying and mitigating security threats specifically targeting applications built around Generative AI and Agentic AI systems. The document serves as a foundational resource for developers, security professionals, and anyone involved in the design, development, and deployment of Gen AI applications. It outlines key vulnerabilities and provides guidance on how to address them. The core of the document is based on the established OWASP Top 10 framework, adapted to the unique risks associated with LLMs and Agentic AI. Specifically, it addresses threats related to prompt injection, data poisoning, model theft, and other vulnerabilities inherent in these rapidly evolving technologies. The document includes a Red Teaming Guide, further emphasizing the importance of proactive security testing.   • Reflective Agent: Agents that iteratively evaluate and critique their own outputs to enhance performance.
  • Task-Oriented Agent: Agents designed to handle specific tasks with clear objectives.
  • Hierarchical Agent: Agents are organized in a hierarchy, managing multi-step workflows or distributed control systems.
  • AI agent hijacking: where an AI agent ingests adversarial manipulated data and subsequently executes unintended actions.
  • Tool Misuse: attackers manipulating AI agents to abuse their integrated tools.
  • Privilege Compromise: attackers exploit weaknesses in permission management to perform unauthorized actions.
