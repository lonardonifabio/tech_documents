Trust, attitudes and use of artificial intelligence: A global study 2025

Authors: Nicole Gillespie, Steve Lockey, Alexandria Macdade, Tabi Ward, Gerard Hassed

This document presents the findings of a global study titled "Trust, attitudes and use of artificial intelligence: A global study 2025," conducted by the University of Melbourne in collaboration with KPMG International. The research investigates public trust, attitudes, and the utilization of artificial intelligence across various regions. The study was led by Professor Nicole Gillespie and Dr Steve Lockey from the University of Melbourne, with contributions from Alexandria Macdade, Tabi Ward, and Gerard Hassed. A multidisciplinary advisory board, comprising academics and industry experts, provided feedback and input throughout the project's development. The research methodology involved data collection, analysis, and reporting, utilizing a comprehensive approach to understand the complex relationship between trust, attitudes, and the adoption of AI. The study's findings are released under a Creative Commons Attribution, Non-Commercial, Share Alike 4.0 International license, allowing for non-commercial use with proper attribution. The license explicitly restricts commercial use and mandates clear indication of any modifications made to the original material. The document serves as a valuable resource for researchers, policymakers, and organizations seeking insights into the evolving landscape of AI adoption and the critical role of public trust in its successful implementation. The collaborative effort between the University of Melbourne and KPMG highlights the importance of interdisciplinary research in addressing the challenges and opportunities presented by artificial intelligence. The study's findings are intended to inform strategies for fostering trust and promoting responsible AI development and deployment.
