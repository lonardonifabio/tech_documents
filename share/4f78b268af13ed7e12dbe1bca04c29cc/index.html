AI Incident Database Update - June 2025

Authors: Unknown

This document details a major update to the AI Incident Database, released on June 23, 2025. The update incorporates the latest incidents from the AIID (AI Incident Database) as of that date, specifically up to incident ID #1116. Key improvements include a revised LLM analysis process aimed at enhancing the validity of incident reports, a complete reclassification of the entire dataset to ensure consistency across all incidents, and a simplified harm severity analysis using a 1-5 point scale instead of the previous 0-10 scale. This simplification was intended to remove ambiguities and promote more objective grading. Furthermore, a new Impact Profile visualization has been introduced, presenting harm data as a spider chart for easy comparison and identification of specific incident profiles. Finally, the National Security Impact Assessment has been updated, assessing the impact of each incident across five categories: Physical Security & Critical Infrastructure. The update represents a significant effort to maintain the accuracy, consistency, and usability of the AI Incident Database, providing a more robust tool for analyzing and understanding AI-related incidents.

Key Concepts:

  • Latest incidents data update
  • Reclassification of incidents for consistency
  • New harm severity scale (1-5 points)
  • Impact Profile visualization (spider-chart)
  • National Security Impact Assessment (5 categories)
