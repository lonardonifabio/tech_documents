Artificial Intelligence: Foundations, Theory, and Algorithms

Authors: Gerhard Paaß, Sven Giesselbach

This book, "Artificial Intelligence: Foundations, Theory, and Algorithms," serves as a comprehensive resource for understanding the core concepts and advancements within the field of Artificial Intelligence. It’s designed to facilitate the dissemination of knowledge, technologies, and methodologies related to AI and its diverse applications. The book’s primary focus is on establishing a strong foundation in AI, covering both theoretical aspects and practical algorithms. It caters to a broad audience, including research students and researchers across various disciplines such as computer science, computer engineering, electrical engineering, and data science. The content systematically explores fundamental AI topics, beginning with the theoretical underpinnings and progressing to algorithmic implementations. Key areas of investigation include knowledge representation, automated reasoning, inference, reasoning under uncertainty, planning, scheduling, problem-solving, cognition, search, diagnosis, constraint processing, multi-agent systems, and game theory within the context of AI. Furthermore, the book delves into more contemporary techniques like machine learning, deep learning, and reinforcement learning. The series itself, "Artificial Intelligence: Foundations, Theory, and Algorithms," aims to provide a platform for the publication and communication of all AI-related topics, ensuring that researchers and practitioners have access to the latest developments and innovations. The book’s structure is designed to be accessible to those new to the field, while still providing sufficient depth for experienced researchers. It’s a valuable resource for anyone seeking a solid understanding of the principles and techniques that drive the rapidly evolving field of artificial intelligence. The series editors, Barry O’Sullivan and Michael Wooldridge, represent leading figures in the field, further solidifying the book’s credibility and relevance.
