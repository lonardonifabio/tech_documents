Building Applications with Foundation Models

Authors: Chip Huyen

This book, authored by Chip Huyen, provides a comprehensive and accessible guide to AI engineering, specifically focusing on the utilization of readily available foundation models. It addresses the transformative impact of foundation models on the AI landscape, shifting it from a specialized field to a broadly applicable development tool. The book is targeted towards AI application developers who may lack prior AI experience, offering a structured approach to building AI products. It delves into the core concepts of AI engineering, explaining its differences from traditional machine learning engineering. Key topics covered include the development process for AI applications, identifying and addressing challenges at each stage, and exploring various model adaptation techniques such as prompt engineering, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), fine-tuning, agents, and dataset engineering. The book also tackles critical considerations like latency and cost optimization when deploying foundation models, providing strategies for efficient serving. Furthermore, it emphasizes the importance of selecting appropriate models, metrics, data, and developmental patterns based on specific needs.
