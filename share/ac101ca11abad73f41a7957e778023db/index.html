AI 2027: Predicting the Impact of Superintelligence

Authors: Daniel Kokotajlo, Scott Alexander, Thomas Larsen, Eli Lifland, Romeo Dean

This document, "AI 2027," is a projection of the potential impact of superhuman AI over the next decade, arguing that its effects will be transformative, potentially rivaling the Industrial Revolution. Authored by a collective of thinkers, the piece builds upon predictions from prominent figures like Sam Altman (OpenAI) who believe AGI could arrive within five years. The authors acknowledge the potential for skepticism but firmly believe the arrival of superintelligence is a plausible scenario, highlighting the lack of serious discussion and planning around this topic. They outline a methodology of repeatedly asking "what would happen next," starting from the present day and projecting forward in time. The core argument is that society is unprepared for the arrival of superintelligence and that more detailed, concrete analysis is urgently needed. The document aims to stimulate a broader conversation about the trajectory of AI development and how to proactively shape a positive future. It emphasizes the need for dissenting voices and rigorous thought to guide the development of AI. The authors' goal is to fill a critical gap in understanding and discussion surrounding this potentially monumental shift.
