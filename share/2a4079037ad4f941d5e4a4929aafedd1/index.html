The core discussion revolves around two primary approaches to modeling appearance: local and global models. Local models, exemplified by Gouraud and Phong shaders, are based on the Phong lighting model and do not account for object-object reflections, offering efficiency for real-time rendering. Global models, such as ray-tracing and radiosity, are employed to simulate realistic reflections and interactions between objects, though these are computationally more intensive. The document highlights the trade-offs between these approaches, acknowledging that current Realtime 3D systems predominantly utilize local models, often extended to incorporate global attributes.
