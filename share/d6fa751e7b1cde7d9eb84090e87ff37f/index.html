AI Applications in SMEs: Why RAG and Agentic AI do not reach production

Authors: PandasAI Open Research Report, Sinaptik GmbH

This report, based on insights from approximately 100 AI and data professionals, investigates the challenges faced by Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs) that utilize AI applications without owning their own AI infrastructure. The research focuses on the prevalent reliance on cloud-based AI services and consultancy-driven implementation, revealing a fragile ecosystem prone to issues. A key finding is that Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and Agentic AI approaches, despite significant hype, are struggling to achieve production-level success within this context. The report identifies several critical problems, including the inherent instability of RAG systems, the ineffectiveness and high cost of Agentic AI, and difficulties in managing memory and knowledge layers. Furthermore, it highlights the fragility of freshness layers and the challenges associated with implementing AI guardrails. The analysis extends to concerns surrounding the accuracy of open models, the dependency on LLM APIs and their associated costs, and the reluctance to engage in training and fine-tuning. The study also addresses the difficulties in establishing reliable and programmable AI solutions. The report underscores the need for a shift in approach, moving beyond simply deploying AI tools to building robust, sustainable, and truly valuable AI applications within the SME landscape. The research suggests a critical need for SMEs to move beyond a purely consumption-based model and towards a more strategic and integrated approach to AI adoption, focusing on building internal capabilities and establishing clear governance frameworks. The findings point to a significant disconnect between the expectations surrounding AI and the practical realities of implementation, particularly for organizations lacking dedicated AI infrastructure and expertise.

Key Concepts:

  • Fragile ecosystem of AI implementation in SMEs relying on external services.
  • Challenges with RAG and Agentic AI reaching production.
  • Dependency on LLM APIs and associated cost frustrations.
  • Importance of evaluation in achieving AI success.
  • The distinction between RAG-based and agentic applications is becoming increasingly blurred.
  • Successful implementations incorporate blended functionalities.
  • RAG systems can be enhanced with agentic capabilities to provide more comprehensive and interactive user experiences.
  • Startups face fundamental business model challenges and dependency on large model providers.
  • Fragility of AI systems due to lack of freshness.
  • The need for robust change detection and content lifecycle management for maintaining accurate knowledge layers.
