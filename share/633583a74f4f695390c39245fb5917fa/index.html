Superintelligence Strategy: Expert Version

Authors: Dan Hendrycks, Eric Schmidt, Alexandr Wang

This document outlines the urgent need for a strategic approach to navigating the potential risks and opportunities presented by rapidly advancing artificial intelligence, particularly the emergence of superintelligence. It argues that the current trajectory of AI development, characterized by competitive advancements and the potential for destabilizing proliferation, mirrors the dynamics of nuclear deterrence. The core concept introduced is Mutual Assured AI Malfunction (MAIM), a strategy analogous to Mutual Assured Destruction (MAD) designed to deter unilateral AI dominance. MAIM proposes that any state attempting to achieve AI supremacy will face preemptive sabotage from rival nations, utilizing methods ranging from cyberattacks to physical destruction of data centers. The document emphasizes the importance of states bolstering their economies and militaries through AI investment, alongside proactive nonproliferation efforts to prevent weaponizable AI capabilities from falling into the hands of rogue actors. It posits that the strategic landscape for AI superpowers is already defined by this inherent risk of sabotage, creating a new era of competition and potential conflict. The authors highlight the need for a proactive, deterrent strategy to manage the risks associated with superintelligence, recognizing the potential for AI to fundamentally reshape national security and international relations. The document’s central argument is that a robust MAIM strategy is crucial to mitigating the dangers of uncontrolled AI development and ensuring a more stable global order. The document’s focus on proactive measures and deterrence reflects a serious assessment of the potential consequences of unchecked AI advancement.

Key Concepts:

  • The anticipation of superintelligence by AI researchers.
  • MAIM as a deterrence regime analogous to nuclear MAD.
  • The potential for AI to destabilize global power dynamics and increase conflict risk.
  • Rapid advancements in AI are transforming society.
  • AI presents opportunities and risks for policymakers and strategists.
  • The dual-use nature of AI (civilian and military) poses significant challenges.
  • AI systems introduce new avenues for pursuing weapons that could confer a decisive advantage.
  • Advanced AI systems may drive technological breakthroughs that alter the strategic balance, similar to the introduction of nuclear weapons.
  • AI-enabled cyberweapons could suddenly and comprehensively destroy a state’s critical infrastructure.
  • The concept of ‘superweapons’ aims to undermine deterrence and reorder global power structures.
