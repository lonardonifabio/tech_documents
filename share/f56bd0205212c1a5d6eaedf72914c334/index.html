Key Concepts:

  • A foundation model is a large artificial intelligence model trained on a vast quantity of unlabeled data at scale resulting in a model that can be adapted to a wide range of downstream tasks.
  • GPT is a family of Transformer-based large language models developed by OpenAI.
  • Embedding vectors are models or model components that have been already been trained and can be fed into a neural network or trained themselves.
  • Vector databases (Weaviate, Redis, Qdrant, Milvus, Chroma)
  • Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)
  • Language Models (LaMDA)
  • Chain-of-thought prompting is a technique to improve reasoning in large language models by generating intermediate steps.
  • Few-shot learning allows a model to process examples before attempting a task.
  • Few-shot learning reduces the need for task-specific data and minimizes overfitting compared to fine-tuning.
