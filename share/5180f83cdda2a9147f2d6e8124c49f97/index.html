Top 10 operational impacts of the EU AI Act

Authors: IAPP

This document excerpt details a ten-part series launched by the International Association of Privacy Professionals (IAPP) focusing on the operational impacts of the EU AI Act. The series, authored by leading European legal experts, aims to translate the complex provisions of the Act into actionable terms for stakeholders. The EU AI Act, finalized on July 12, 2024, following extensive negotiations concluding in December 2023, represents a world-first in AI regulation. It adopts a risk-based approach, combining a human-centric and product-safety framework to establish a harmonized regulatory environment across the European Union. The series addresses key aspects of the Act, including its scope, subject matter, definitions, and the identification and assessment of risk associated with high-risk AI systems. Furthermore, it delves into the specific requirements for general-purpose AI models, emphasizing the importance of AI assurance, testing, evaluation, and oversight mechanisms. The document highlights the Act's significance in shaping the governance of AI globally and the IAPP's role in providing practical guidance for navigating its complexities. The series is designed to assist organizations in understanding and complying with the new regulations, ensuring responsible AI development and deployment within the EU and beyond. The publication of the final text in the Official Journal of the European Union marked a crucial step, initiating a phased implementation process that will unfold over the coming months, solidifying the EU's position as a leader in AI governance.

Key Concepts:

  • Harmonized framework for AI regulation
  • Human-centric approach to AI regulation
  • Product-safety approach to AI regulation
  • Risk assessment and mitigation for AI systems
  • Post-market monitoring obligations under the AI Act
  • Information obligations for serious incidents
  • Fragmented surveillance landscape
  • Remedies for affected persons
  • Fines
  • The AI Act applies to operators across the AI value chain.
