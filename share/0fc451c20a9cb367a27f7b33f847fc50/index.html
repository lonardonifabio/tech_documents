European Commission Frequently Asked Questions Data Act

Authors: European Commission

This document, a set of Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) regarding the European Commission's Data Act (Regulation (EU) 2023/2584), was published approximately a year before the regulation's entry into application. It aims to assist stakeholders in understanding and implementing the legal provisions of the Data Act, which establishes harmonized rules for fair value allocation from data generated by investment in data-generation technologies. The FAQs are the result of extensive stakeholder interactions and are intended to be a 'living document,' subject to updates as needed. Crucially, this document does not represent the official position of the European Commission and its responses do not expand upon or introduce new obligations under the legislation. The information provided is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. The European Commission encourages users to contact them with any questions not addressed within the document, and they will endeavor to respond promptly. The document's purpose is to facilitate understanding and implementation of the Data Act, a key piece of legislation designed to promote fairness in data value allocation.

Key Concepts:

  • Harmonised Rules for Data Allocation
  • Fairness in Data Value Allocation
  • Data Generation Technology Investment Protection
  • Implementation of Legal Provisions
  • Living Document - Dynamic Updates
  • Protection of trade secrets through a new mechanism (5(9), Recital 31)
  • Level of enrichment of data as a key factor in data value allocation
  • User rights to receive, use, and port generated data (raw and pre-processed)
  • Distinction between raw/pre-processed and derived/inferred data based on modification and investment
  • Pseudonymisation and encryption are key components of the Data Act.
