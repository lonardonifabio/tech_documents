This document, released by the European Commission on June 28, 2023, provides questions and answers regarding the Data Act. It outlines the rationale behind the Data Act, which addresses significant inefficiencies within the European economy due to the underutilization of data generated by connected products. The core issues highlighted include a lack of clarity concerning data access rights, the disadvantage faced by Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) in negotiating data-sharing agreements, and obstacles in switching between cloud and edge services within the EU. These factors contribute to a substantial loss of economic value at the EU level, negatively impacting consumer choice, innovation, and public service delivery. The Data Act aims to rectify these problems by removing barriers to data access for both public and private entities, while simultaneously safeguarding the incentives for data generation. Specifically, it establishes a balanced control over data for its creators, ensuring fairness in the allocation of data value among stakeholders involved in data contracts, and respecting the rights of companies and individuals who invest in data products and services. The document emphasizes the importance of unlocking the value of data generated by connected objects, recognizing this as a critical area for innovation. The Data Act seeks to create a more equitable and efficient data economy within the European Union, fostering greater collaboration and value creation from data assets. The overarching goal is to stimulate innovation and improve the effectiveness of data-driven solutions across various sectors.
