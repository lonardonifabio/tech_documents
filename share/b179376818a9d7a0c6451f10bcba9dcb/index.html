General Purpose AI Code of Practice

Authors: GPAI

The "General Purpose AI Code of Practice" (Codice di condotta sull'IA di uso generale) is a document designed to guide providers of General Purpose AI (GPAI) models, particularly those with systemic risk, in meeting the requirements of the EU AI Act. It serves as a framework for demonstrating compliance, allowing the AI Office to assess it, and fostering a secure and reliable AI ecosystem. This document represents the second draft, released in January 2025, with a target completion date of May 2, 2025. Key aspects of the code include a strong emphasis on transparency, requiring detailed documentation of models including training data, testing procedures, and identified potential risks. This transparency is intended to empower users to understand the capabilities and limitations of the AI models. Furthermore, the code mandates that GPAI model providers implement measures to respect copyright regulations, specifically concerning the use of copyrighted data in model training. A critical component is the consideration of systemic risk, demanding that high-risk GPAI providers actively evaluate and mitigate the potential systemic risks associated with their models. The document's purpose is to establish a robust set of guidelines for responsible AI development and deployment, aligning with the EU AI Act's objectives. The ongoing development and refinement of this code are crucial for ensuring a trustworthy and accountable AI landscape. The document's creation is a collaborative effort, aiming to create a practical and enforceable standard for GPAI providers.

Key Concepts:

  • The General Purpose AI Code of Practice provides guidance to GPAI model providers on complying with the AI Act.
  • Providers must demonstrate compliance and allow the AI Office to evaluate it.
  • Key aspects include transparency regarding model training and testing, copyright protection for training data, and risk mitigation for high-risk models.
