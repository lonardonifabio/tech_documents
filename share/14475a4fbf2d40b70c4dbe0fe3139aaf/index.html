Approaches to Responsible Governance of GenAI in Organizations

Authors: Himanshu Joshi, Shabnam Hassani, Dhari Gandhi, Lucas Hartman

This white paper addresses the critical need for responsible governance frameworks surrounding the rapidly expanding use of Generative AI (GenAI) across various industries. Recognizing the significant opportunities GenAI presents for innovation, the document simultaneously acknowledges the complex ethical, accountability, and societal implications that accompany its integration. The research is based on a comprehensive approach, combining a thorough literature review with established governance frameworks, and crucially, insights derived from weekly industry roundtable discussions involving a diverse group of experts from different organizations and professional backgrounds. These discussions provided invaluable practical knowledge, ensuring the recommendations within the paper are firmly rooted in real-world challenges and experiences. The core objective of this work is to furnish organizations with actionable guidance for implementing a balanced governance strategy. This strategy emphasizes a risk-based approach, enabling organizations to foster innovation while maintaining appropriate oversight and control. The paper identifies key principles for integrating responsible GenAI governance, considering the varied structures of different organizations. The research aims to provide a practical roadmap for navigating the complexities of GenAI adoption, promoting responsible innovation and mitigating potential risks. The document’s strength lies in its combination of theoretical foundations with concrete, expert-validated recommendations, making it a valuable resource for organizations seeking to strategically manage the deployment of GenAI.

Key Concepts:

  • Responsible GenAI governance is crucial for organizations.
  • A balanced, risk-based governance approach is recommended to enable innovation and oversight.
  • The paper utilizes a combination of literature review, governance frameworks, and expert insights to inform its recommendations.
  • Lack of clear accountability structures hinder AI governance.
  • Risk-based approaches are central to AI governance, exemplified by frameworks like NIST RMF and the EU AI Act.
  • Sector-specific risk considerations drive the need for adaptable governance strategies.
  • A successful responsible AI governance framework must be embedded at all levels of an organization.
  • Effective governance frameworks should address GenAI concerns from a strategic level down to tactical implementation.
  • The governance structure must incorporate roles at each level to ensure accountability and alignment across the organization.
  • Embedding ethical commitments within GenAI projects.
