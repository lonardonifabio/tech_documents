Building the Control Layer for Agentic AI with an AI Gateway

Authors: Abhishek Choudhary (CTO, True Foundry), Greg Coquill (AI Product Lead, Linked In ToP Voice)

This ebook addresses the critical operational challenges hindering the widespread adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) within enterprises. The rapid integration of LLMs is accompanied by significant hurdles, including API fragmentation, inconsistent latency, unpredictable throttling, opaque cost structures, and a lack of effective governance. These issues represent existential bottlenecks, preventing AI from transitioning from experimentation to robust, production-level infrastructure. To overcome these challenges, the document introduces the concept of an AI Gateway ‚Äì a novel architectural layer designed to unify, govern, and optimize AI workloads. Unlike traditional API Gateways, AI Gateways are fundamentally semantic-aware and context-sensitive, capable of mediating between human language and machine execution. The AI Gateway's core functions include abstracting evolving model APIs, enforcing semantic guardrails, orchestrating multi-model fallbacks, and capturing detailed observability metrics, such as token usage and model costs. Essentially, it acts as a policy-enforced access layer, providing a centralized and controlled environment for managing and utilizing LLMs effectively. The document highlights the need for a more sophisticated approach to AI infrastructure management, moving beyond simple traffic routing to encompass the complexities of LLM deployments. The goal is to enable organizations to confidently scale their AI initiatives while maintaining control, predictability, and cost-efficiency.
