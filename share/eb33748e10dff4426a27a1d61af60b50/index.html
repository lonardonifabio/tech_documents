Statistics Interview Questions

Authors: Gouthami Krishnamurthy

This document provides a concise overview of fundamental statistics concepts frequently encountered in interview settings. It begins by outlining key areas within statistics, including measures of central tendency and dispersion, covariance and correlation, probability distributions, standardization, and the central limit theorem. The document then delves into Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), explaining its process of visually and statistically analyzing data to uncover patterns, distributions, and relationships. A crucial distinction is made between quantitative and qualitative data, defining quantitative data as numeric and measurable, and qualitative data as categorical and language-based. Within quantitative data, the document further clarifies discrete and continuous data types. For qualitative data, it distinguishes between nominal and ordinal data types, providing examples such as age, height, weight, income, gender, marital status, and native language. Finally, the document defines a Key Performance Indicator (KPI) as a metric used to track and evaluate progress towards a specific goal. The overall purpose of this document is to serve as a quick reference guide for individuals preparing for statistics-related interviews, covering essential terminology and concepts. It's a practical resource for understanding the core principles of statistical analysis and data types.

  • Measure of dispersion
  • Covariance and correlation
  • Probability distribution function
  • Standardization and normalization
  • Central limit theorem
  • Population and sample
  • Hypothesis testing
  • Exploratory Data Analysis
  • Key Performance Indicator
