Social Licensing for Data Reuse

Authors: Unknown

This report addresses the critical shortcomings in existing data governance frameworks by introducing the concept of 'social licensing for data reuse.' Social licensing establishes a governance framework where communities collectively define the terms, purposes, and conditions under which data pertaining to them is utilized. It moves beyond traditional consent models, which primarily focus on individual agreement at the point of data collection, and instead creates a continuous process for communities to shape and oversee data reuse. The report emphasizes that social licensing complements consent, recognizing the importance of embedding social, ethical, and cultural considerations alongside legal requirements. This is particularly vital when dealing with data that represents collective interests, such as environmental monitoring data, community health data, and cultural knowledge. The core of the report is the introduction of a 'Social Licensing Toolkit,' designed to assist development agencies, governments, and practitioners in implementing this framework. The toolkit utilizes a three-stage process: first, it facilitates participatory engagement to document community-defined terms and conditions; second, it translates these preferences into enforceable data sharing and use agreements; and third, it ensures that community priorities are embedded within the agreements. The report argues for a layered governance system that balances individual rights with community-level oversight, acknowledging the diverse and evolving needs of communities impacted by data collection and reuse. The ultimate goal is to foster responsible data governance practices that respect community values and promote equitable access to data benefits. The document highlights the need for a proactive approach to data governance, moving beyond reactive responses to ethical concerns and towards a system that genuinely incorporates community perspectives.
