Probability Cheatsheet v2.0

Authors: William Chen, Joe Blitzstein, Sebastian Chiu, Yuan Jiang, Yuqi Hou, Jessy Hwang

This document is a comprehensive probability cheatsheet compiled by William Chen and Joe Blitzstein, drawing heavily on Joe Blitzstein's (stat110) lectures and the Blitzstein/Hwang's Introduction to Probability textbook. It provides a concise overview of fundamental probability concepts, including the multiplication rule for compound experiments, sampling tables, the distinction between ordered and unordered sampling, and the concept of independence. Key topics covered include the naive definition of probability, calculating probabilities based on favorable outcomes, and understanding conditional independence. The cheatsheet also includes information on sampling techniques, such as sampling with and without replacement, and the use of sampling tables to determine the number of possible samples of a given size from a population. The document emphasizes the importance of understanding the underlying principles of probability and provides a practical guide for quickly referencing key formulas and concepts.
