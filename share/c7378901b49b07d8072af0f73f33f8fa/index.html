Ipsos AI Monitor 2025: Wonder and the Worry of AI

Authors: Ipsos

The Ipsos AI Monitor 2025, based on a 30-country Ipsos Global Advisor Survey, explores the complex and often contradictory attitudes towards Artificial Intelligence. The report highlights a significant 'Wonder and the Worry' tension, where a substantial portion of the global population expresses both excitement about AI's potential to solve global problems and apprehension regarding the disruptive changes it may bring. Approximately half of respondents globally report being excited about AI, while the other half express nervousness. A key finding is the pronounced regional variation in these sentiments. The Anglosphere (U.S., Great Britain, Canada, Ireland, and Australia) demonstrates a notably higher level of nervousness compared to excitement. European markets exhibit a more balanced perspective, with a mid-range of excitement, while certain markets in Southeast Asia display a more positive outlook. Interestingly, Japan presents an outlier, showing neither excitement nor nervousness. The report indicates that nervousness regarding AI has increased significantly since 2022, although it remains relatively stable year-over-year. This data suggests a persistent and evolving public perception of AI, characterized by a blend of optimism and concern. The survey's findings provide valuable insights into the factors driving public opinion on AI and the need for careful consideration of its societal implications.
