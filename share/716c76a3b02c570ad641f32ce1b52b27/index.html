Deep Learning Roadmap 2021

Authors: datadrivenscience

This document presents a roadmap for learning Deep Learning, specifically focusing on a series of modules designed to build a strong foundation in the field. The roadmap is structured into three parts, each progressively deepening the understanding of deep learning concepts and techniques. Part 01 covers foundational mathematics essential for deep learning, including Applied Mathematics, Linear Algebra, Probability and Information Theory, and Numerical Computation for Machine Learning, alongside introductory Machine Learning Basics. Part 02 delves into core deep learning practices, exploring Deep Feedforward Networks, Regularization for Deep Learning, Optimization for Training Deep Models, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), and the overall Deep Learning Methodology. Finally, Part 03 expands the scope to include more advanced topics such as Linear Factor Models, AutoEncoders, Representation Learning, Structured Probabilistic Models For Deep Learning, Monte Carlo Methods, The Partition Function, Approximate Inference, and Deep Generative Models. The document is associated with DataDrivenScience (DDS), emphasizing their commitment to helping individuals build careers in Data Science. It encourages engagement through hashtags (#LearnWithDDS) and a call to action to provide feedback in the comments. The document is presented as a resource for learning and development within the data science domain, highlighting DDS's role in facilitating this journey.
