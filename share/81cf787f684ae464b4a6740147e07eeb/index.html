Methodological Brief s Impact Evaluation No. 10 Overview: Data Collection and Analysis Methods in Impact Evaluation

Authors: Greet Peersman

This methodological brief, titled 'Impact Evaluation No. 10 Overview: Data Collection and Analysis Methods in Impact Evaluation,' is produced by the UNICEF Office of Research. The Office of Research serves as UNICEF’s dedicated research arm, with the core mission of enhancing international comprehension of children’s rights and facilitating the global implementation of the Convention on the Rights of the Child. The brief focuses on sharing contemporary research practice, methods, designs, and recommendations from leading researchers and evaluators. It’s specifically targeted at UNICEF staff involved in conducting, commissioning, or interpreting research and evaluation findings to inform decisions related to programming, policy, and advocacy. The document highlights the importance of evidence-based policies within UNICEF’s global programs. It emphasizes the role of research in stimulating dialogue around impact evaluation methods and contributing to a broader global conversation concerning children and child rights. The brief’s creation is driven by the need to ensure UNICEF’s strategies are grounded in robust research and evaluation practices. The document’s internal peer review process underscores UNICEF’s commitment to quality and rigor in its research endeavors. It’s crucial to note that the views expressed are those of the authors and/or editors and do not necessarily represent the official policies or views of UNICEF. The document’s purpose is to foster further discussion and refine impact evaluation methodologies within the organization and beyond. The brief’s content is designed to be a valuable resource for UNICEF staff seeking to strengthen their understanding and application of research methods in the context of children’s rights programming and policy development.
