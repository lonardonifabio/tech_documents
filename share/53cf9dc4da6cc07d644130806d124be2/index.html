The Data Science Handbook

Authors: Max Song, Henry Wang, William Chen, Carl Shanfore

The Data Science Handbook is a collection of insights and advice gathered from 25 prominent data scientists, including figures like DJ Patil, Hilary Mason, Pete Skomoroch, Riley Newman, Drew Conway, and many others. The book aims to provide a comprehensive overview of the field, covering various aspects from taking risks and giving back (DJ Patil) to becoming a successful data scientist (Hilary Mason) and the role of data in journalism (Mike Dewar) and customer understanding (Riley Newman). It also addresses practical considerations such as creating a data science curriculum (Clare Corthell) and highlights the importance of understanding human problems rather than relying solely on technical metrics (Drew Conway). The book's content is structured around individual perspectives from leading data scientists, offering a diverse range of viewpoints and experiences within the rapidly evolving field.
