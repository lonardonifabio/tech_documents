Navigating the impact of AI systems in the workplace: strengths and loopholes of the EU AI Act from a labour perspective

Authors: Chiara Cristofolini

This research paper, authored by Chiara Cristofolini, critically examines the potential impacts of artificial intelligence (AI) systems within the workplace, specifically focusing on the EU AI Act and its implications from a labor law perspective. The document analyzes both the opportunities and risks presented by AI's rapid advancements, arguing that the technology's deployment necessitates careful consideration of labor-related concerns. The paper begins with an introductory overview of AI's transformative potential and subsequent risks. It then delves into the European Union's regulatory strategy for AI, highlighting the risk-based approach and its effects on the implementation of employment-related AI systems. A key focus is placed on the obligations of AI system providers, particularly those developing high-risk systems, and the corresponding responsibilities of employers who deploy these technologies. The research assesses the strengths and weaknesses of transparency and information requirements associated with AI systems. The paper ultimately provides a critical assessment of the EU AI Act, aiming to inform policy and practice regarding the ethical and legal considerations surrounding AI's integration into the workforce. The document is submitted to a double-blind peer review and authored by an Assistant Professor in Labour Law, University of Trento. It addresses the need for a balanced approach, acknowledging the potential benefits of AI while simultaneously safeguarding worker rights and addressing potential vulnerabilities.
