Robotic Process Automation: Eight Guidelines for Effective Results

Authors: Cathy Tornbohm

This document, published in October 2016, provides eight guidelines for ensuring the successful implementation of Robotic Process Automation (RPA) projects. The core challenge highlighted is the tendency for RPA projects to fail due to a lack of strategic alignment and a misunderstanding of the technology's capabilities. The document emphasizes that RPA projects shouldn't solely focus on cost reduction through headcount reduction; instead, they should prioritize revenue-generating activities and be integrated into a broader digital transformation strategy. A key concern raised is the increasing complexity of automation tools, including AI, which creates confusion around the term 'RPA' and its potential. The guidelines recommend a strategic approach, urging leaders to identify and quantify opportunities for RPA, carefully set expectations regarding the tools' capabilities, and treat RPA as part of a comprehensive automation strategy. The document stresses the importance of understanding the learning curve associated with various automation tools and avoiding the trap of assuming a single tool can address all unautomated processes. It advocates for a holistic view, recognizing that RPA is a component of a larger automation ecosystem. The document's overall message is that successful RPA implementation requires careful planning, strategic alignment, and a clear understanding of the technology's role within an organization's digital transformation efforts. It cautions against a purely tactical approach and promotes a forward-thinking strategy.

Key Concepts:

  • RPA projects should be strategically aligned to achieve specific goals.
  • RPA implementation requires careful consideration of employee roles and potential shifts in responsibilities.
  • RPA is most effective when integrated into a broader automation strategy.
  • Organizations should identify opportunities beyond simple cost reduction when implementing RPA.
  • Faster time to solution
  • Balancing external guidance and cost savings
  • Leveraging external resources for capability building
  • Risk associated with non-standard processes
  • Navigating company politics and political obstacles
  • Leveraging automation through RPA tools.
