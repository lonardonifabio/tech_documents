Three lines of defense against risks from AI

Authors: Jonas Schuett

This article explores the critical need for organizations developing and deploying artificial intelligence (AI) systems to proactively manage the inherent risks associated with this technology. The document highlights the ambiguity surrounding responsibility for AI risk management and proposes a solution: the ‘Three Lines of Defense’ (3LoD) model. This model, widely recognized as best practice across numerous industries, provides a structured framework for organizations to clearly assign and coordinate risk management roles and responsibilities. The core argument centers on how the 3LoD model can significantly improve AI risk management practices. Specifically, it addresses the challenge of identifying and closing gaps in risk coverage, thereby bolstering the overall effectiveness of risk management efforts. Furthermore, the article emphasizes the model's ability to empower the board of directors by providing enhanced oversight of management activities related to AI. The research is targeted towards decision-makers within leading AI companies, regulatory bodies, and organizations involved in establishing industry standards. The document delves into the practical implementation of the 3LoD model, offering guidance for AI companies seeking to strengthen their risk management strategies. It also acknowledges related concepts like the ‘Four Lines of Defense’ (4LoD) and ‘Five Lines of Assurance’ (5LoA) to provide a broader context. The article’s relevance extends to regulatory bodies tasked with overseeing AI development and deployment, ensuring responsible innovation and mitigating potential harms. The inclusion of abbreviations like 3LoD, 4LoD, and 5LoA demonstrates a sophisticated understanding of the risk management landscape surrounding AI. The document’s ultimate goal is to promote a more robust and accountable approach to AI risk management, contributing to safer and more ethical AI development and deployment.
