AIME is a resource designed to assist organizations in establishing a baseline of good practice for managing artificial intelligence (AI) systems. The consultation addresses the increasing complexity of navigating the landscape of AI frameworks and tools, particularly for smaller organizations that may lack the expertise or resources to effectively engage with them. The core purpose of AIME is to distill key principles from existing AI regulations, standards, and frameworks into an accessible tool for organizations to assess and improve their AI management systems. The consultation aims to gather feedback and insights on the development and implementation of AIME, recognizing the importance of responsible AI development and deployment while mitigating potential risks and harms associated with AI technologies. The document highlights the need for a practical and accessible resource to guide organizations through the complexities of AI management, ultimately supporting innovation and responsible technology adoption. The consultation closes on January 29th, 2025, and was issued on November 6th, 2024.

Key Concepts:

  • Establishing a baseline of good practice for managing AI systems.
  • Mitigating risks and potential harms associated with AI systems.
  • Accessible starting point for organizations to assess and improve their AI management systems.
  • Maintaining an AI system record
  • Completing a self-assessment through multiple-choice questions
  • Understanding organizational governance structures related to AI
  • Development of AI systems
  • Data preparation processes (data quality, completeness, representativeness, encoding, scaling, normalization)
  • Bias in AI systems (disproportionate weighting, unfair outcomes, perpetuating negative impacts)
