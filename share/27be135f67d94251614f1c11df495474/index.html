First Draft General-Purpose AI Code of Practice

Authors: Chairs and Vice-Chairs of the four Working Groups

This document presents the initial draft of a General-Purpose AI Code of Practice, developed under the AI Act. The draft is the result of work conducted by the four Working Groups ‚Äì Transparency and copyright-related rules, Risk identification and assessment for systemic risk, Technical risk mitigation for systemic risk, and Governance risk mitigation for systemic risk. The purpose of this draft is to guide providers of general-purpose AI models, particularly those with systemic risk, through a framework of considerations. The document emphasizes an iterative approach, anticipating ongoing refinement based on feedback. Stakeholders are encouraged to provide feedback by November 28th, 12:00 CET via a dedicated platform (Futurium). The draft outlines guiding principles and objectives, and anticipates further modifications based on internal discussions and external input. The final Code will be crucial in shaping the future of general-purpose AI model development and deployment, aiming to address key risks and ensure responsible innovation. The document highlights the importance of collaboration and continuous improvement to ensure the Code remains relevant and effective as the field of AI evolves. The working groups are focused on transparency, risk assessment, technical mitigation, and governance, respectively, to provide a comprehensive approach to responsible AI development.
