How to boost innovation through effective AI Governance?

Authors: PwC

This document, published by PwC, focuses on strategies for boosting innovation while effectively navigating the complexities of AI governance, specifically in the context of the upcoming EU AI Act. Released in November 2024, it highlights the critical need for organizations to optimize their AI governance frameworks to ensure compliance with the Act, which is slated to take effect from 2025. The document emphasizes the significant penalties ‚Äì up to EUR 40 million or 7% of annual turnover ‚Äì for non-compliance. It details the phased implementation timeline of the EU AI Act, outlining key dates for the entry into force, the introduction of codes of practice, and the enforcement of sanctions. The timeline includes specific durations for prohibited AI systems (6 months), codes of practice (9 months), and sanctions (12 months), with longer durations for high-risk AI systems and those already on the market (up to 24 months). The document also addresses the territorial scope of the EU AI Act, encompassing AI systems within the Union, regardless of the provider's location. It stresses the importance of conformity assessment throughout the entire AI value chain, considering the AI system's risk potential and the organization's role. The document identifies key stakeholders including hardware providers, AI service providers, model providers, importers, retailers, and users. It underscores the need for a proactive approach to AI governance to mitigate risks and capitalize on the opportunities presented by AI innovation, particularly in light of the stringent regulatory environment established by the EU AI Act.
