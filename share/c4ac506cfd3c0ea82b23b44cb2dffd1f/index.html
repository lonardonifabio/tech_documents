Auditing AI-Systems: From Basics to Applications

This document excerpt details the outcomes of a one-day workshop held on October 6th, 2020, in Berlin, focused on the auditability of AI systems. The primary goal was to address key questions surrounding the current state of AI system auditability, identify open research questions, and pinpoint urgent future work and promising approaches. The workshop considered the entire lifecycle of AI systems, with a particular emphasis on deep neural networks (DNNs) trained by machine learning (ML). DNNs were examined through the lenses of IT security, safety, and robustness. Specific use cases were utilized as illustrative examples. The document outlines a generalized cAI (Composable AI) lifecycle (Fig. 2A) and then delves into the state-of-the-art of various aspects of cAI system auditability, including the training of AI systems using machine learning and data, attack and defense strategies, verification and validation techniques, interpretability methods, and standardization efforts. A critical element highlighted is the importance of sufficient data quality and quantity for achieving robust AI system performance. The document implicitly acknowledges that the workshop covered a broad range of topics, with some aspects receiving only brief summaries due to their potential impact on AI safety and security. The overall aim is to provide a foundational understanding of the challenges and opportunities associated with making AI systems auditable, contributing to the development of more secure and reliable AI technologies. The focus on DNNs and their training processes underscores the current relevance of this research area within the broader field of AI safety and security.
