Defence and Artificial Intelligence

Authors: Sebastian Clapp, Members' Research Service

This briefing paper, produced by the European Parliamentary Research Service, examines the rapidly evolving intersection of artificial intelligence and defence. The document highlights how the ongoing conflict in Ukraine has dramatically illustrated AI's crucial role across various facets of modern warfare, specifically in intelligence gathering, the deployment of autonomous systems, and sophisticated cyber operations. The analysis reveals a burgeoning global AI arms race, with China and the United States aggressively competing for technological dominance, and Russia significantly increasing its investment in AI capabilities to bolster its military strength. The European Union's Strategic Compass explicitly recognizes the strategic importance of defence innovation, emphasizing the need to bolster the EU's emerging military technologies, and notably, AI. Over the past decade, the EU and its Member States have increasingly recognized AI's vital significance for security and defence, resulting in substantial investments in AI-driven military technologies. Current progress is evident through numerous projects funded by the European Defence Fund and Permanent Structured Cooperation (PESCO), which are actively integrating AI into future military capabilities. Furthermore, there are concerted efforts to foster collaboration and synergy between the civilian, defence, and AI sectors, aiming to maximize the potential of this technology. The document also notes the EU's cooperation with NATO, reflecting a broader strategic alignment. However, the rapid advancement of AI in warfare introduces critical ethical considerations, including establishing clear lines of accountability, ensuring compliance with international humanitarian law, and mitigating the risk of unintended escalation and conflict. The briefing underscores the urgency for the EU to proactively address these challenges and shape the development and deployment of AI in defence to maintain strategic advantage and uphold ethical standards.

Key Concepts:

  • AI arms race between China, Russia, and the United States
  • Integration of AI into military capabilities
  • Ethical concerns regarding AI in warfare (accountability, international humanitarian law, conflict escalation)
  • Civilian AI Sector Innovation
  • AI Arms Race (Geopolitical)
  • AI Infrastructure Development
  • Commercial Application of AI
  • Transfer of AI Technology (Civilian to Defense)
  • Data sovereignty and security
  • Trust and interoperability of data
