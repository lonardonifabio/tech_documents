The EU Artificial Intelligence Act: Latest Developments and Key Takeaways

Authors: EYGM Limited

This document provides an overview of the recently adopted EU Artificial Intelligence Act, which entered into force on August 1, 2024, and will progressively implement tiered compliance obligations across the 27 EU Member States. Adopted at the end of May 2024, the Act represents a landmark achievement in global AI regulation, driven by the EU’s ambition to establish a comprehensive legislative framework for trustworthy and responsible AI systems. The Act’s phased implementation, with most requirements taking effect by mid-2027, reflects a deliberate approach to allow businesses time to adapt. It builds upon existing EU digital legislation, including the GDPR, the Digital Services Act, the Digital Markets Act, the Data Act, and the Cyber Resilience Act, demonstrating a coordinated strategy for governing emerging technologies. The document focuses on outlining the key elements of the AI Act and detailing the tiered compliance obligations associated with it. The Act’s unification of AI regulation across the single market is a significant development, aiming to create a consistent legal landscape for AI development and deployment. Furthermore, the AI Act possesses important extraterritorial implications, extending its reach to all AI systems that impact individuals within the EU, regardless of where the system is developed or operated. This broad scope underscores the EU’s commitment to safeguarding fundamental rights and promoting ethical AI practices. The document explicitly states that it does not constitute legal advice, emphasizing the need for professional consultation when navigating the complexities of the new regulations. The overall goal of the AI Act is to foster innovation while mitigating potential risks associated with AI technologies, ensuring a balance between technological advancement and societal well-being. The document serves as a valuable resource for understanding the key aspects of this transformative legislation and its potential impact on businesses and individuals operating within the European Union.

Key Concepts:

  • The EU AI Act was adopted at the end of May 2024 and will enter into force from 1 August 2024.
  • The Act establishes tiered compliance obligations that will progressively come into effect.
  • The EU aims to lead in promoting a comprehensive legislative approach to trustworthy and responsible AI systems.
  • The AI Act unifies AI regulation across the 27 EU Member States.
