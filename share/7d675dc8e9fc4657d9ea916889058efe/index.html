Basic Management Principles

Authors: Jack E. Fincham, PhD, RPh

This document, "Basic Management Principles," authored by Jack E. Fincham, provides an introduction to fundamental management concepts applicable across various organizational scales, from individuals to large organizations. The core objective is to equip readers with a foundational understanding of management functions and the characteristics of effective managers. The material emphasizes the importance of self-awareness in management and highlights the professional skills required for success. It delves into key areas such as strategic planning, project management, and risk management, specifically within the context of pharmacy management. A significant portion of the document focuses on continuous quality improvement (CQI) using the P-D-C-A framework, illustrating how this process can be leveraged to address problems and opportunities. Furthermore, the text addresses the impact of change on pharmacy operations and underscores motivation as a critical managerial skill. The document also explores problem-solving techniques and their application to pharmacy management scenarios, recognizing the diverse approaches needed to tackle challenges. The author stresses the need for managers to understand and address risk management issues, and to appreciate the importance of various problem-solving techniques. Ultimately, the document aims to provide a practical guide for individuals seeking to develop their management skills within the pharmaceutical industry.

Key Concepts:

  • Understanding basic management principles applied to different organizational sizes.
  • Grasping the core functions of management.
  • Recognizing desirable characteristics of effective managers.
  • Importance of self-awareness in management.
  • Identifying professional skills needed for managers.
  • Impact of unresolved issues on management effectiveness.
  • Utilizing self-development methods.
  • Strategic planning and its application.
  • Applying project management tenets to new product/service launches.
  • Implementing and evaluating projects.
