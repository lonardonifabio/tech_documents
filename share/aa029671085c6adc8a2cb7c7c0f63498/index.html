Physical AI: The Next Wave?

Authors: Zoe Huczok, Albert Meige, Rick Eagar

This Viewpoint article explores the emerging field of Physical AI, examining its potential as the next significant advancement in artificial intelligence. The core concept revolves around establishing direct interactions between AI systems and the physical world, moving beyond traditional AI models reliant on data sourced from the internet. Physical AI leverages data captured through sensors, IoT devices, and other real-world sources to drive actions within the physical realm. The article highlights Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang's endorsement of Physical AI at the 2025 Consumer Electronics Show, emphasizing its potential across diverse applications. Key areas of focus include robotics, automation, and industrial process optimization, alongside applications in healthcare and personal robotic assistants. The document contrasts Physical AI with classical machine learning and generative AI, which primarily operate on digitally-sourced data. It identifies challenges associated with this new approach, particularly concerning data capture and real-time interaction. The article suggests that Physical AI represents a shift in AI development, prioritizing tangible outcomes and direct engagement with the environment, rather than solely relying on digital data for training and execution. The discussion touches upon the use of IoT devices and sensors as crucial components in enabling this interaction, ultimately positioning Physical AI as a transformative technology with broad implications for various industries.

Key Concepts:

  • Physical AI focuses on interactions between AI and the physical world.
  • Physical AI utilizes sensors and IoT devices to capture real-world data.
  • Physical AI's outputs directly control physical devices and systems.
  • "more for less" approach mimicking natural neuron function
  • Longer development times for physical AI compared to GenAI
  • Importance of error-free physical AI devices before launch
  • Safety concerns with AI interacting directly with the physical environment
