Generative AI in Action: Opportunities & Risk Management in Financial Services

Authors: Sapan Dogra, Michael Erras, Callum Farrell-Morris, Peter Hairs, Carsten Maple, Walter McCahon, Tom Niven, Bella Thornely, Luma Zitani

This report, jointly authored by UK Finance and Accenture with sponsorship from the UK Finance AI Policy Committee, provides an analysis of the opportunities and risks associated with generative AI within the financial services industry. Published in January 2025, the document explores the current landscape of generative AI adoption, identifying near-term trends and key components of generative AI solutions. It delves into prevalent use cases across financial services, including those impacting areas like customer service, fraud detection, and risk management. A significant portion of the report focuses on the inherent risks associated with generative AI, acknowledging the potential challenges and outlining considerations for responsible implementation. The document’s structure includes an overview of the report, forewords from UK Finance and Accenture, an executive summary, and a detailed examination of the current generative AI landscape, its components, and associated risks. The collaborative effort between UK Finance and Accenture aims to offer a comprehensive perspective on how financial institutions can strategically leverage generative AI while effectively managing the associated risks. The report’s publication date, January 2025, indicates a forward-looking approach, reflecting the rapidly evolving nature of this technology within the financial sector. The inclusion of the Alan Turing Institute and UK Finance AI Policy Committee highlights the breadth of expertise contributing to the analysis. The report’s core objective is to guide financial institutions in navigating the complexities of generative AI, promoting both innovation and responsible governance.
