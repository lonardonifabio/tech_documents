Authors: Expert Group for Responsible Introduction and use of AI assistants

This practical guide, translated from Norwegian to English, focuses on assisting organizations in the responsible introduction and utilization of AI assistants within the workplace. The document addresses critical considerations for organizations contemplating adopting AI assistants, aiming to ensure a well-defined and managed implementation. It begins by establishing whether the purpose of introducing an AI assistant is clear, prompting organizations to assess key factors such as clarity of objectives, organizational readiness, and the presence of necessary technical infrastructure and legal frameworks. The guide systematically explores these aspects, including defining the scope of the AI assistant's application, identifying the target audience, and understanding the data utilized by the system. A significant portion is dedicated to assessing organizational readiness, emphasizing management responsibilities, employee involvement, and the importance of competence in responsible AI usage. Furthermore, the document delves into the legal landscape, specifically referencing the EU AI Act, highlighting the need for organizations to navigate regulatory requirements. The guide encourages a proactive approach to risk management, urging awareness of potential issues during the implementation process. The core of the document is structured around a series of questions designed to guide organizations through a thorough evaluation process, ensuring they are prepared for the challenges and opportunities presented by AI assistants. The guide's ultimate goal is to promote a responsible and effective integration of AI into the working environment, mitigating potential risks and maximizing benefits.

Key Concepts:

  • Determining the purpose of introducing an AI assistant.
  • Assessing organizational readiness for AI implementation.
  • Clarifying legal framework conditions related to AI usage.
  • Importance of defining goals when implementing AI assistants.
  • Need for risk assessments throughout the AI implementation process.
  • Data classification based on risk levels.
  • Understanding user needs as the foundation for AI assistant selection.
  • Risk of data storage and model training from AI assistants.
  • Importance of employee training and knowledge regarding AI assistant usage.
  • Establishing clear guidelines for data sharing with AI assistants.
