The ROI of AI Ethics: Profiting with Principles for the Future

Authors: Marisa Zalabak, Balaji Dhamodharan, Bill Lesieur, Olga Magnusson, Shannon Kennedy, Sundar Krishnan

This position paper, "The ROI of AI Ethics: Profiting with Principles for the Future," examines the critical intersection of return on investment (ROI) and ethical considerations within the rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence (AI). It argues that prioritizing ethical deployment of AI systems is not simply a matter of corporate social responsibility, but a fundamental strategic business decision. The document highlights the significant risks associated with unchecked AI implementation, including the potential for biases, data privacy violations, and compliance breaches. It cites several sources, including TrustCloud, Knutson 2024, Cogent Info 2024, Esperanca 2024, Trinkes, Hyperproof, Radiol 2023, and Leslie 2019, to support the claim that a lack of ethical guardrails can lead to detrimental societal and organizational outcomes. The paper’s core argument is that organizations can ‘profit with principles’ by proactively integrating ethical frameworks into their AI strategies. The document emphasizes the need for robust AI risk management frameworks to mitigate these risks and ensure responsible innovation. It suggests that by embracing ethical considerations, organizations can not only avoid negative consequences but also unlock long-term value and sustainable growth. The paper’s focus on ROI underscores the economic imperative of ethical AI, demonstrating that responsible innovation is not just the right thing to do, but a smart business move. The document’s title clearly indicates its central theme: leveraging ethical principles to achieve a positive return on investment in AI technologies.
