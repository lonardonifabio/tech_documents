Machine Learning based on Database Analysis. The Case of the Game of Chess

Authors: BENEDETTO TONI, PAOLO CIANCARINI, FRANCESCO ROMANI

This document excerpt details a thesis project conducted at the Università degli Studi di Pisa, focusing on applying Machine Learning techniques to the game of chess. The core of the work centers around utilizing database analysis to develop an intelligent chess-playing system. The thesis, authored by Benedetto Toni and supervised by Paolo Ciancarini with Francesco Romani as a co-supervisor, was completed during the summer session of the 1995-96 academic year. The work begins with an introduction to the intersection of games and artificial intelligence, exploring how knowledge representation and reasoning are handled within chess-playing programs. The project's aim is to demonstrate how database analysis can be leveraged to improve the performance and efficiency of AI systems designed to play complex games like chess. The structure of the thesis includes an introduction to the topic, a detailed exploration of game-playing algorithms, and a discussion of the project's objectives and methodology.
