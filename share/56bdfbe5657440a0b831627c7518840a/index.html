AI Act Timeline and Key Provisions

Authors: Jochem Dogger, Robert Krueger

This document provides a concise overview of the European Union's AI Act, outlining key timelines and provisions. The AI Act, slated for full applicability by Q2 2026, represents a significant regulatory framework for artificial intelligence. The document highlights the phased implementation, beginning with the approval by the European Parliament in Q1 2024, followed by the shutdown of prohibited AI systems by Q2 2024 and Q4 2024. It specifically identifies prohibited AI systems, such as real-time biometric identification (with limited exceptions for law enforcement) and social scoring algorithms. High-risk AI systems, including those used as safety components, are subject to third-party conformity assessments under specific European laws or predefined case studies encompassing critical infrastructure, law enforcement, and access to essential services.
