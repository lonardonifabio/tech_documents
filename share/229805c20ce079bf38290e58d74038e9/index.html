Foundations of Large Language Models

Authors: Tong Xiao, Jingbo Zhu

This document, titled "Foundations of Large Language Models," presents an overview of the significant advancements and underlying principles of large language models (LLMs). It highlights the origins of LLMs within the field of natural language processing (NLP) and their subsequent revolutionary impact on artificial intelligence. A core concept explored is the ability of LLMs to acquire knowledge of the world and languages through large-scale language modeling tasks. This approach enables the creation of universal models capable of addressing diverse problems, fundamentally shifting research methodologies within NLP and related disciplines. The document emphasizes a move away from traditional training methods towards leveraging large-scale language modeling for knowledge acquisition.
