PROJECT MANAGEMENT HANDBOOK FOR EPC Plant Engineering, Procurement and Construction

Authors: Frank-Peter Ritsche

This document is a comprehensive Project Management Handbook specifically tailored for the EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction) industry. It provides a detailed framework for managing projects within this sector, covering the entire project lifecycle from initial inquiry through warranty. The handbook systematically breaks down the project life cycle into key phases, including Acquisition, Bid, Project, and Warranty phases, aligning with the PMI (Project Management Institute) life cycle model. It emphasizes the integration of the EPC cycle, detailing the crucial aspects of Project Management, Engineering, Procurement, and Construction & Commissioning. A significant portion of the handbook focuses on applying established project management standards, referencing ISO 21500, IPMA¬Æ, and BS 6079. The content is structured around a detailed project plan, incorporating elements such as project documentation hierarchy, adherence to ISO 10006 and 10005, and consideration of key project management aspects like scope, time, cost, quality, human resources, communication, and risk management. The document serves as a practical guide for professionals involved in EPC projects, offering a structured approach to planning, executing, and controlling projects to ensure successful outcomes. Key Concepts:

  • The Project Life Cycle encompasses stages from inquiry to warranty.
  • Project Planning involves creating a project plan with associated documentation.
  • EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction) cycle integrates project management, engineering, procurement, and construction.
  • Applying project management tools to complex projects
  • Importance of a well-defined project organization
  • Establishing clear interfaces within the project organization
  • Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) - a hierarchical decomposition of a project into smaller, manageable components.
  • Structuring Criteria - different approaches to organizing a project's scope (e.g., by contract, plant, time phase, discipline).
  • Synergies - leveraging knowledge and resources from previous projects to improve efficiency and reduce costs.
  • The importance of tracking negotiation results within cost control and cost change management to ensure the completion of the baseline budget.
