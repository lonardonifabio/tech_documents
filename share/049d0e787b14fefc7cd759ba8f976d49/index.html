Commission Guidelines on Prohibited Artificial Intelligence Practices Established by Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (AI Act)

Authors: European Commission

This document outlines the Commission Guidelines pertaining to prohibited artificial intelligence practices as defined by Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, commonly known as the AI Act. The core purpose of these guidelines is to provide a framework for understanding and implementing the restrictions imposed by the AI Act. The document begins with a 'Background and objectives' section, establishing the rationale behind the guidelines and their intended impact. It then proceeds to an 'Overview of prohibited AI practices,' detailing the specific actions and systems targeted by the AI Act. A key component is a breakdown of the 'Prohibitions listed in Article 5 AI Act,' which identifies the specific activities considered unacceptable under the regulation. The guidelines further explore the 'Legal basis of the prohibitions,' clarifying the legal justifications for these restrictions. The document meticulously defines the 'Material scope,' specifying that the prohibitions apply to practices related to the 'placing on the market', 'putting into service', or 'use' of an AI system, ensuring a clear understanding of the systems covered. Furthermore, it establishes the 'Personal scope,' identifying the 'responsible actors' accountable for adhering to the regulations. A critical section addresses 'Exclusion from the scope of the AI Act,' notably highlighting exemptions for 'National security, defence and military purposes,' recognizing the unique considerations surrounding these sensitive areas.
