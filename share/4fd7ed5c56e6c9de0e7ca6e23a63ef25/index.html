Data Science Cheatsheet

Authors: Maverick Lin

This document is a concise cheatsheet outlining the fundamental concepts of data science. It defines data science as a multi-disciplinary field integrating computer science, statistics/machine learning, and data analysis to derive insights from vast data volumes. The document highlights two primary research paradigms: hypothesis-driven, where the research question dictates the necessary data, and data-driven, where existing data reveals potential problems. A core tenet of data science is continuous questioning and curiosity. It distinguishes between structured and unstructured data, defining structured data as having predefined formats like tables and relational databases, while unstructured data lacks such structure and includes formats like text, images, and audio. The document further clarifies data types, differentiating between quantitative data (numerical, such as height and weight) and categorical data (labeled groups like race, sex, and hair color). It introduces the concept of 'Big Data' ‚Äì massive datasets characterized by high volume, variety, and velocity, exceeding the capacity of a single machine. Common data formats covered include CSV, XML, SQL, JSON, and Protocol Buffers. The document also discusses various data sources, including companies, APIs, government data, academic research, and web scraping, emphasizing the diverse nature of data collection. Ultimately, this cheatsheet serves as a quick reference for understanding the core elements of data science and its associated methodologies.
