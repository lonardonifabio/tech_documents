Science in the age of AI: How artificial intelligence is changing the nature and method of scientific research

Authors: The Royal Society

This report, titled "Science in the age of AI," published by The Royal Society in October 2024 (DES8836_5), comprehensively examines the profound impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on the landscape of scientific research. The document highlights how AI is fundamentally altering both the processes and the very nature of scientific inquiry. It begins with an overview of AI's role in science, detailing its application across various research methodologies. A key focus is on how AI is reshaping the way scientists approach research questions and interpret data. The report delves into the challenges and opportunities presented by AI's ability to analyze vast datasets, a critical factor given the increasing volume of scientific data generated today. A significant portion of the report is dedicated to the concept of reproducibility in AI-driven research, acknowledging the unique hurdles introduced by complex algorithms and machine learning models. The document identifies barriers to reproducibility, such as the ‘black box’ nature of many AI systems and the difficulty in understanding the intricate relationships learned by these models. It emphasizes the importance of transparency and explainability in AI research. The report also explores the ethical considerations surrounding AI in science, particularly concerning data access and the potential for bias in algorithms. The document provides a framework for addressing these challenges, advocating for robust methodologies and standards to ensure the integrity and trustworthiness of AI-based scientific research. The report concludes with recommendations for future research and highlights the need for ongoing dialogue and collaboration between scientists, policymakers, and the broader community to navigate the evolving role of AI in scientific discovery. The inclusion of a case study on rare disease diagnosis illustrates a practical application of AI's transformative potential. The entire document is underpinned by the Creative Commons Attribution License, promoting open access and responsible use of the information. The Royal Society's initiative aims to foster a deeper understanding of AI's capabilities and its implications for the future of science.
