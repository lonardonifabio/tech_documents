Intellectual Property Issues in AI (OECD)

Authors: OECD

This OECD paper highlights the critical need for adaptable governance frameworks to address the rapidly evolving landscape of Artificial Intelligence and its associated intellectual property challenges. The document focuses on four key IP issues that are emerging within the AI sector. Firstly, it addresses copyright concerns, specifically related to data scraping and the use of copyrighted materials, which frequently leads to global litigation and regulatory investigations. Secondly, the paper emphasizes the significant jurisdictional complexity arising from differing legal frameworks across various regions, creating uncertainty for multinational companies operating in the AI space. Thirdly, a core concern is the lack of transparency in AI development, where developers often fail to disclose data sources, hindering the ability of rights holders to monitor and protect their content. Finally, the document stresses the importance of proactive measures for firms, suggesting the implementation of voluntary AI ethics codes, the development of AI risk monitoring tools and explainability mechanisms, and increased public awareness and education regarding AI technologies. The OECD advocates for a multi-faceted approach involving industry-wide codes of conduct, technical safeguards, and proactive engagement with stakeholders to navigate these complex IP issues effectively. The report underscores the need for a balanced approach that fosters innovation while simultaneously protecting intellectual property rights within the rapidly advancing field of AI.
