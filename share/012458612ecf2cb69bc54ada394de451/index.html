GDPR Enforcement Tracker Enforcing the EU AI Act with Fines

Authors: Sean Musch, Michael Borrelli, Charles Kerrigan, Jean NG, Mehmet Tahir Agrak, Erdinc Sacan, Peter Slattery, Charles Kerrigan

This document, published by AI & Partners, focuses on the potential impact of GDPR enforcement trends on the forthcoming EU AI Act, specifically concerning forecasted fines. The report aims to answer key questions regarding how existing GDPR enforcement data can illuminate expectations for potential penalties under the new AI Act. AI & Partners, a non-profit organization, is dedicated to safeguarding digital rights for users globally, utilizing a multi-faceted approach including technical support, policy engagement, advocacy, professional services, regulatory interventions, and participation in industry groups like AI Commons. The organization's work centers around defending fundamental rights in the age of artificial intelligence. The report's preparation involved Sean Musch and Michael Borrelli. It provides a framework for understanding the relationship between GDPR enforcement and the anticipated regulatory landscape of the EU AI Act, highlighting the critical role of data-driven insights in predicting future regulatory outcomes. The document serves as a preliminary exploration of the intersection between existing data protection regulations and the emerging AI governance framework, offering a strategic perspective for navigating the evolving legal environment. The organization's contact information and website are provided for further inquiries.
