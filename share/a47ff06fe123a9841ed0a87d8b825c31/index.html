Are AI Chatbots Replacing Search Engines? A 2-Year Data Study on Web Traffic Trends

Authors: OneLittleWeb

This 24-month data study, conducted by OneLittleWeb and utilizing SEMrush data from April 2023 to March 2025, investigates the evolving relationship between AI chatbots and traditional search engines. The research focuses on understanding the shift in web traffic patterns and whether AI chatbots are effectively replacing search engines. The study reveals a significant surge in chatbot usage, with total visits increasing by 80.92% year-over-year, reaching 55.2 billion. Despite this substantial growth, search engines continue to hold the dominant position in web traffic. The analysis compares monthly and yearly trends for both AI chatbots and search engines, examining factors like daily and monthly visits. The research also identifies the top 10 AI chatbots by total visits and compares them to the top 10 search engines. The study provides a data-driven perspective on this critical question, offering insights into the changing digital landscape. The methodology relies on SEMrush data, providing a robust foundation for the conclusions drawn. The report includes a 'Verdict' section, summarizing the key findings and offering expert opinions on whether AI chatbots are quietly replacing search engines. The data is presented through tables and comparisons, allowing for a detailed understanding of the trends.
