Authors: Sean Musch, Michael Borrelli, Charles Kerrigan, Helen Yu, Leonardo Freixas, Jeff Kagan, Jess Toft, Enzo di Taranto Capozzi, Colin Levy, Elise Victor, T. Scott Clendaniel, Melissa Sassi, Maliha Hashmi, Aleksandra Przegalinska

This document, the "AI Ecosystem Brief 2025 Edition United States of America," is a publication by AI & Partners, released in July 2025. It outlines the organization's mission to defend and extend the digital rights of users globally who are at risk due to the development and deployment of artificial intelligence. AI & Partners operates by combining technical support, policy engagement, advocacy, professional services, regulatory interventions, and participation in industry groups like AI Commons. The core belief driving their work is that AI should be a force for unlocking potential rather than causing harm. The report highlights the potential negative consequences of losing control over AI systems and emphasizes the importance of responsible AI development and deployment. AI & Partners actively seeks to mitigate risks associated with AI, focusing on building trust and fostering innovation. The organization’s approach involves a multi-faceted strategy, combining direct support with broader policy and regulatory efforts. The document underscores the need for proactive measures to safeguard user rights and ensure ethical considerations are prioritized within the rapidly evolving AI landscape. It’s a strategic document designed to guide stakeholders in navigating the complexities of the AI ecosystem while upholding fundamental rights and promoting responsible innovation. The publication is intended to inform and influence decisions related to AI governance and development, advocating for a future where AI benefits humanity without compromising individual freedoms or creating undue risks.

Key Concepts:

  • AI & Partners' mission is to defend digital rights and promote responsible AI development.
  • The organization focuses on combining various strategies – technical support, policy engagement, advocacy, and industry group participation – to ensure AI benefits users and minimizes potential harm.
  • AI & Partners believes AI should unlock potential rather than cause harm, emphasizing the importance of trust and responsible handling of AI systems.
  • The U.S. utilizes existing legal instruments to govern AI ethics in the absence of specific AI legislation.
  • Application of existing statutes (FTC Act, Fair Credit Reporting Act, etc.) to AI systems to ensure fairness and protect consumer rights.
  • Frameworks and initiatives promoting ethical AI development across sectors, including guidance on AI deception and discrimination risks.
  • AI-related legislative proposals are stalled due to various legislative hurdles.
  • The U.S. government is encouraging voluntary safety measures from AI companies.
  • NIST is developing standards for AI risk management, including the AI Risk Management Framework (RMF 1.0).
