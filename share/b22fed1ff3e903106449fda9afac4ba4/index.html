GenerativeAIinEUlaw: Liability, privacy, intellectualproperty, andcybersecurity

Authors: ClaudioNovellia, FedericoCasolaria, PhilippHacker, GiorgioSpedicato, LucianoFloridia

This research paper delves into the complex legal and regulatory implications arising from the increasing sophistication and autonomy of Generative AI systems within the European Union. Specifically, it examines four key areas: liability, privacy, intellectual property, and cybersecurity. The study critically analyzes the existing and proposed EU legislation, with a particular focus on the Artificial Intelligence Act (AIA), to determine its effectiveness in addressing the challenges posed by Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs). The authors identify potential gaps and shortcomings in the current EU legislative framework and offer recommendations to ensure the safe and compliant deployment of generative models. The research highlights the significant impact of recent developments, such as the release of ChatGPT at the end of 2022, which has spurred widespread interest in Generative AI and LLMs. The paper's core argument centers on the need for a robust and adaptable legal framework to manage the risks associated with these rapidly evolving technologies, considering their unique characteristics compared to traditional AI models. The analysis aims to contribute to a more informed discussion and policy development surrounding the regulation of Generative AI within the EU, promoting responsible innovation and mitigating potential harms. The research emphasizes the importance of proactive measures to address the challenges presented by Generative AI, ensuring that its development and deployment align with ethical considerations and legal requirements.

Key Concepts:

  • Challenges in predictability and legal compliance due to the complexity and emergent autonomy of GenerativeAI systems.
  • Legal and regulatory implications of GenerativeAI challenges within the European Union context.
  • Assessment of existing and proposed EU legislation (including AIA) in addressing GenerativeAI challenges.
  • LLM providers' obligations to establish compliance systems.
  • The TDM exception under Directive 2019/790/EU as a potential solution for lawful use of training datasets.
  • The definition of text and data mining within the DSM Directive.
