Comparison of Knowledge-Based and Data-Driven AI in Education

Authors: Tuomi

This excerpt discusses the evolution of Artificial Intelligence (AI) approaches within the context of education. It contrasts two primary methodologies: knowledge-based AI and data-driven AI. Knowledge-based AI, prevalent in the 1980s, relies on explicitly programmed logic rules and probabilistic reasoning to represent human knowledge, primarily utilized in Intelligent Tutoring Systems (ITS). These systems, often employing rule-based reasoning and fuzzy logic, generate personalized learning paths and provide detailed dashboards for teachers and students, enhancing system trustworthiness. However, these systems faced limitations in scaling to complex, real-world problems. Conversely, data-driven AI, leveraging the increased computational power of modern computers, focuses on extracting knowledge directly from data through pattern analysis and inference, specifically utilizing machine learning (ML) algorithms and statistical models. This approach allows computer systems to make predictions and decisions without explicit programming. The excerpt highlights the shift from rule-based systems to data-driven approaches, emphasizing the role of ML in creating more adaptable and powerful educational tools. The discussion underscores the importance of both approaches, acknowledging the strengths of knowledge-based systems in providing explainable insights and the capabilities of data-driven AI in handling complex, data-rich environments. The use of dashboards and visual explanations is presented as a key feature of knowledge-based systems, while the data-driven approach relies on the ability of algorithms to identify trends and patterns within student learning data. The overall theme is the changing landscape of AI in education, driven by technological advancements and the increasing availability of data.
