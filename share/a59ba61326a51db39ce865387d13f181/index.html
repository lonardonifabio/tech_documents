The AI Responsibility Gap: Why Leadership is the Missing Link

Authors: NTT DATA

This executive insight report, produced by NTT DATA, addresses the critical "AI responsibility gap," which refers to the growing disparity between the rapid advancement of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and the development of corresponding frameworks for ethical considerations, safety protocols, sustainability practices, and inclusivity. The report highlights the urgency of this gap as it presents significant risks, including security vulnerabilities, potential biases leading to inequality, exacerbation of climate challenges, and inefficient investment in AI projects. The research, based on interviews with over 2,300 decision-makers and influencers from 34 countries across 12 industries, reveals that a substantial portion of respondents (nearly 70% at the C-level) recognize the problem. The study focuses on understanding the reasons behind this gap and exploring strategies to align AI innovation with accountability. Specifically, the report investigates how organizations are navigating the complexities of GenAI, a rapidly evolving area of AI, with a focus on ensuring its development and deployment are transformative, equitable, and sustainable. The findings suggest a cautious optimism, with participants expressing both excitement about AI's potential and concerns about its responsible implementation. The research emphasizes the need for proactive leadership to guide the evolution of AI, minimizing costly reconfigurations and maximizing the long-term benefits of AI initiatives. Ultimately, the report argues that addressing the responsibility gap is crucial for realizing AI's full potential while mitigating its inherent risks.

Key Concepts:

  • The disconnect between AI innovation and responsible frameworks.
  • Risks associated with the AI responsibility gap (security, equality, sustainability, wasted investment).
  • The importance of balancing innovation with accountability in AI development and implementation.
  • Establishing a dedicated team/committee for GenAI responsibility
  • Integrating responsible considerations into strategic decision-making
