This document is a comprehensive probability cheatsheet compiled by William Chen and Joe Blitzstein, drawing heavily on Joe Blitzstein's (stat110) lectures and the Blitzstein/Hwang's Introduction to Probability textbook. It provides a concise overview of fundamental probability concepts, including the multiplication rule for compound experiments, sampling tables, the distinction between ordered and unordered sampling, and the concept of independence. Specifically, it details the multiplication rule for calculating probabilities when multiple events occur sequentially, explaining how the number of possible outcomes increases multiplicatively with each component of the experiment. It also covers sampling techniques, differentiating between sampling with and without replacement, and the calculation of combinations using the factorial notation. A key section focuses on the definition of probability as the ratio of favorable outcomes to total outcomes, a naive approach. Furthermore, the document clearly defines conditional independence, explaining that knowing the outcome of one event doesn't influence the probability of another if they are independent. The cheatsheet serves as a quick reference guide for students and practitioners seeking to understand and apply core probability principles.   • Naive definition of probability: Pnaive(A) = number of outcomes favorable to A / number of outcomes.
