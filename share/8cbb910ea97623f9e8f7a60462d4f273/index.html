Key Concepts:

  • Population is the entire collection of objects or individuals.
  • Sampling involves selecting a part of the population for analysis.
  • Random sampling ensures each possible sample has an equal chance of selection.
  • Observational studies can be affected by lurking variables.
  • Experimental studies allow for control of lurking variables and provide evidence for causation.
  • Descriptive statistics summarize data using measures like mean, median, and quartiles.
  • The mean is susceptible to outliers and is the arithmetic average.
  • The median is unaffected by outliers and represents the middle value of an ordered dataset.
  • Quartiles divide ranked data into four equal groups.
  • Variance measures the average squared distance from the mean.
