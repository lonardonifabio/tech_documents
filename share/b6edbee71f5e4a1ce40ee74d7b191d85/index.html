European AI Standards – Technical Standardization and Implementation Challenges under the EU AI Act

Authors: Robert Kilian, Linda J¨ack, Dominik Ebel

This research paper offers a pioneering systematic analysis of European technical standardization efforts directly related to the EU AI Act. The study, based on extensive qualitative interviews with 23 leading European organizations – including prominent names like Mistral and Helsing – provides a critical examination of how businesses, particularly startups and SMEs, are navigating the anticipated standardization process. The research delves into the practical challenges associated with aligning AI systems with the requirements of the EU AI Act and broader sectoral standards, specifically vertical product safety legislation. A wide range of industries are considered, encompassing mobility, finance, manufacturing, healthcare, enterprise software, and defense & legal tech. The analysis highlights several key obstacles, most notably the extremely short implementation timeframe (approximately 6 months) compared to the 12 months realistically needed for proper compliance. A significant concern is the uneven distribution of power and influence within standardization committees, leading to potential biases. Furthermore, the study identifies the issue of ‘double regulation,’ where AI systems must adhere to both the EU AI Act and sector-specific regulations. Technical standards are rapidly evolving and are already impacting global AI competition, creating substantial market entry barriers. The research underscores the considerable financial burden associated with achieving harmonized standards compliance, adding to the complexity of navigating this evolving regulatory landscape. The paper’s findings are crucial for organizations seeking to understand and address the challenges of conforming to European AI standards.

Key Concepts:

  • Systematic analysis of European technical and soon to be harmonized standardization for AI systems.
  • Challenges related to a short implementation period (6 months) compared to the actual required time (12 months).
  • Imbalance of participation and influence in standardization committees.
  • Double regulation and technical implementation hurdles for AI standards compliance.
  • Challenges and opportunities for organizations in European AI standardization.
  • Implementation of the EU AI Act through technical standards.
  • The role of standardization in creating innovation-friendly framework conditions for AI technology.
  • The need for new European standards due to misalignment with the AI Act.
  • The adoption of existing international standards where they align with EU values.
  • The role of CEN-CENELEC JTC 21 in developing new European standards.
