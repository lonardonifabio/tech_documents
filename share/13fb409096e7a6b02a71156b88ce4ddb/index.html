Operationalising AI Governance with ISO 42001, NIST & GDPR

Authors: davidclarke

This document outlines a practical approach to operationalizing AI governance, leveraging established frameworks like ISO 42001, NIST, and GDPR. It addresses the critical gap between understanding these frameworks and actually implementing them effectively. The core challenge highlighted is translating theoretical alignment on AI risk into tangible action. The document proposes a five-step process: first, mapping AI use cases to GDPR to identify compliance pressure points; second, applying NIST AI Risk Management Functions (Map, Measure, Manage, Govern) to systematically assess and mitigate risks; third, utilizing ISO 42001 to build robust processes and controls, including defining objectives, risk tolerances, and lifecycle controls; fourth, establishing an AI Oversight Group to ensure cross-functional review and governance; and finally, implementing continuous testing and improvement cycles. The document emphasizes that AI governance is not a static process but requires ongoing adaptation and refinement. It stresses the importance of assigning clear ownership for each NIST function and fostering collaboration between legal, data science, compliance, and operations teams. The key takeaway is that a structured approach, combining these frameworks, is essential for responsible and effective AI deployment. The document advocates for a shift from simply understanding the frameworks to actively implementing them through a phased, iterative process.

  • Mapping AI Use Cases to GDPR
  • NIST AI Risk Management Functions (Map, Inventory, Measure, Manage, Govern)
  • ISO 42001 Process Building
  • AI Oversight Group
  • Continuous Improvement Cycles for AI Governance
