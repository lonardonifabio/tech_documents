Risk Tiers: Towards a Gold Standard for Advanced AI

Authors: Nicholas A. Caputo, Siméon Campos, Stephen Casper, James Gealy, Bosco Hung, Julian Jacobs, Daniel Kossack, Toni Lorente, Malcolm Murray, Seán Ó hÉigeartaigh, Amin Oueslati, Henry Papadatos, Jonas Schuett, Anna Katariina Wisakanto, Robert Trager

This research memo addresses the escalating risks associated with advanced Artificial Intelligence and proposes a 'gold standard' approach to risk management through the implementation of risk tiers. Risk tiers are categorized systems that define the expected harm associated with AI systems, dictating the appropriate mitigation strategies and responses based on the level of risk. The document highlights the critical need for AI companies to proactively identify and address potential risks, ensuring transparency regarding the societal acceptance of AI risks and the methods used to manage them. Despite growing attention from policymakers and industry, a lack of standardization in risk tiering approaches has created confusion and hindered effective comparison of risk estimation and mitigation practices. The memo argues that a more unified and robust framework for risk tiering is essential to gain a comprehensive understanding of the overall risk landscape posed by advanced AI, allowing for better assessment and comparison of different risk management strategies. The current diverse landscape of risk management approaches, while fostering innovation, has created challenges in assessing the cumulative impact of various AI systems and developers. Ultimately, the research advocates for a standardized system to improve clarity and facilitate more effective risk management within the rapidly evolving field of advanced AI.

  • Risktiers are categories based on expected harm that specify in advance which mitigations and responses will be applied to systems of different risk levels.

  • Defining risk tiers for advanced AI based on existing practices and future risks.
  • The challenge of defining risk tiers given potential harms from advanced AI.
  • Risk tiering approach to produce comprehensible divisions of AI’s risk space.
  • Society’s risk tolerance influencing the number and placement of risk tiers.
  • Mapping mitigations to risk tiers is crucial for effective risk management.
  • Risk budgets quantify the likelihood and impact of potential harm.
  • Mitigation effectiveness is evaluated by quantifying the reduction in risk levels.
