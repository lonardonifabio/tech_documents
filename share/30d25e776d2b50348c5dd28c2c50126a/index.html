Large Language Models and Emotional Intelligence Tests

Authors: Katja Schlegel, Nils R. Sommer, Marcello Mortillaro

This research investigates the capabilities of large language models (LLMs) in assessing and generating emotional intelligence. The study centers around examining whether LLMs, specifically ChatGPT-4, ChatGPT-o1, Gemini 1.5 flash, Copilot 365, Claude 3.5 Haiku, and DeepSeek V3, can effectively perform on and create emotional intelligence tests. The initial findings revealed a significant advantage for the LLMs, with an average accuracy of 81% across five standard emotional intelligence tests, surpassing the 56% accuracy achieved by human participants in previous validation studies. To further explore the LLMs' potential, ChatGPT-4 was utilized to generate new test items for each of the original emotional intelligence tests. A subsequent study involving 467 human participants administered both the original and ChatGPT-generated tests, confirming statistically equivalent test difficulty. Analysis of perceived item characteristics, including clarity, realism, diversity, internal consistency, and correlations with other cognitive tests, indicated no substantial differences between the two versions, with all observed variations being small relative to Cohen’s d (± 0.25). The research concludes that LLMs demonstrate a notable capacity for emotional intelligence assessment and test generation, though the differences are relatively minor. The study provides valuable insights into the evolving role of AI in psychological testing and evaluation. The findings contribute to a better understanding of how LLMs can be leveraged for assessing human cognitive abilities, particularly in the domain of emotional intelligence. The research highlights the potential for AI to augment traditional psychological assessment methods, while also acknowledging the need for continued scrutiny and validation of AI-generated tests. The use of multiple LLMs and a comprehensive set of evaluation metrics strengthens the validity of the conclusions. The study's design, incorporating both original and newly generated tests, offers a robust approach to assessing the performance of LLMs in this context.

Key Concepts:

  • Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate capabilities in solving and generating emotional intelligence tests.
  • LLMs outperformed humans on emotional intelligence tests with an average accuracy of 81%.
  • ChatGPT-generated tests demonstrated statistically equivalent test difficulty compared to original tests.
  • The Situational Test of Emotion Understanding (STEU) assesses emotion understanding through vignettes.
  • Participants select emotion words that best describe a situation or infer the cause of an emotion.
  • Responses are scored as correct or incorrect, and aggregated into a total score representing the proportion of correct answers.
  • Participants were asked to rate the similarity of scenario pairs.
  • ‘Very similar’ scenarios are described differently but are essentially the same.
  • The study investigates the perception of emotional situations based on scenario descriptions.
