Prohibited AI Systems Banned Under EU AI Act

Authors: European Commission

This document outlines the provisions of Article 5 of the EU AI Act, which mandates the removal of prohibited AI systems from the market. Specifically, it details unacceptable AI practices that are strictly forbidden, including AI-based manipulation and exploitation, particularly targeting vulnerable groups. The document highlights bans on mass surveillance and social scoring systems, as well as the outlawing of AI models used for predictive policing and risk profiling. It emphasizes the core objective of preventing unacceptable risks to individuals and safeguarding fundamental rights, safety, and democracy. The document provides actionable guidance for businesses, focusing on assessing their AI systems, strengthening ethical AI governance through risk assessments and compliance reviews, and investing in AI literacy and training within teams. The key takeaway is the need for proactive measures to ensure adherence to the EU AI Act's regulations and mitigate potential risks associated with AI deployment. The document serves as a critical resource for organizations operating within the European Union seeking to understand and comply with the evolving legal landscape surrounding artificial intelligence. It underscores the importance of responsible AI development and deployment to avoid violations of the EU AI Act.

Key Concepts:

  • Removal of prohibited AI systems from the market
  • Unacceptable AI practices (manipulation, exploitation, surveillance, social scoring, predictive policing)
  • Ethical AI Governance and risk management
