Options and Motivations for International AI Benefit Sharing

Authors: Claire Dennis, Sam Manning, Stephen Clare, Boxi Wu, Jake Okechukwu Effoduh, Chinasa T. Okolo, Lennart Heim, Katya Klinova

This report, produced by the Centre for the Governance of AI, explores various options and motivations driving the need for international benefit sharing in the context of Artificial Intelligence. The document outlines a complex landscape where the rapid advancement of AI technologies presents both immense opportunities and significant challenges regarding equitable distribution of benefits and potential risks. It delves into the diverse perspectives and considerations surrounding this issue, acknowledging the involvement of multiple stakeholders including governments, international organizations, and the private sector. The research examines different models for benefit sharing, considering factors such as intellectual property rights, data governance, and the distribution of economic gains. The authors highlight the importance of establishing clear governance frameworks to ensure that the benefits of AI are shared broadly and that potential harms are mitigated. The report emphasizes the need for international cooperation to address the global implications of AI, recognizing that technological advancements transcend national borders. It also addresses the ethical considerations associated with AI development and deployment, advocating for a human-centered approach that prioritizes fairness, transparency, and accountability.
