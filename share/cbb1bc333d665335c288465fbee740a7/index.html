Ethics and governance of artificial intelligence for health Guidance on large multi-modal models

Authors: World Health Organization

This document, published by the World Health Organization (WHO) in 2024, provides guidance on the ethical and governance considerations surrounding the use of artificial intelligence, particularly large multi-modal models, within the healthcare sector. It addresses the critical need for responsible development and deployment of AI in health, recognizing the potential benefits alongside the inherent risks. The guidance emphasizes a proactive approach to managing these risks, focusing on ensuring fairness, transparency, and accountability in AI systems. Specifically, the document outlines key principles for ethical AI governance, including considerations for bias mitigation, data privacy, and patient safety. It stresses the importance of robust oversight mechanisms and collaborative frameworks to address the complex challenges posed by AI in healthcare. The WHO’s approach is rooted in its commitment to promoting health equity and improving global health outcomes. The document’s framework is designed to be adaptable and applicable across diverse healthcare settings. Furthermore, it includes specific stipulations regarding the use of the WHO logo and the need to avoid implying WHO endorsement of any particular organization, product, or service. The document’s creation reflects the growing urgency of addressing the ethical implications of AI in healthcare, aligning with WHO’s broader mission to guide and support global health initiatives. The guidance is intended to be a foundational resource for stakeholders involved in the development, implementation, and regulation of AI in the health domain.
