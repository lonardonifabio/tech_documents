Introduction to Deep Learning

Authors: Unknown

This course provides a foundational introduction to deep learning, targeting individuals seeking to enter the field of AI and specifically, deep learning engineering. The course aims to equip learners with the knowledge and skills necessary to build and apply deep learning systems, recognizing the high demand for deep learning engineers. It begins by establishing deep learning as a transformative technology, comparable to a 'superpower,' enabling the creation of AI systems previously unattainable. The core curriculum focuses on understanding the fundamental principles of deep learning, starting with the basics of neural networks and single neurons. Learners will gain an understanding of how neural networks function, including the weighted sum calculation and the role of activation functions (represented by the concept of linear regression without activation). Specifically, the course covers key aspects such as understanding major trends driving the rise of deep learning, identifying applications of deep learning, and the architecture of neural networks, including parameters and vectorized neural networks. A key component is the explanation of how deep learning actually works, moving beyond superficial descriptions. The course emphasizes practical application, enabling students to build and train their own deep learning models. Furthermore, it addresses the practical need for job seekers in AI, providing the knowledge to answer basic interview questions related to deep learning. The course material includes a detailed explanation of neural networks, including the concept of a single neuron as a linear regression model, and the role of weights and thresholds. The course is designed for those looking to gain a solid understanding of the core concepts and techniques involved in deep learning, preparing them for potential careers in AI and deep learning.

Key Concepts:

  • Deep learning as a sought-after career path
  • Building and training deep neural networks
  • Understanding neural network architecture and parameters
  • Linear regression and perceptrons
  • Vectorized neural networks for efficiency
  • Vectorization: Utilizing NumPy's vectorized operations to avoid explicit loops for faster computation.
  • Broadcasting: NumPy's automatic shape adjustment during matrix operations.
  • SIMD (Single Instruction, Multiple Data): Utilizing parallel processing for faster calculations.
  • Logistic Regression Implementation: Demonstrates the process of implementing Logistic Regression using vectorized operations and comparing it with a loop-based approach.
  • Symmetry breaking problem
