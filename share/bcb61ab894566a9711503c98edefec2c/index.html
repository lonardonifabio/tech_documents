Principles of Data Wrangling

Authors: Tye Rattenbury, Joe Hellerstein, Jeffrey Heer, Sean Kandel, Connor Carreras

Principles of Data Wrangling is a practical guide focused on data preparation techniques. The book, authored by Tye Rattenbury, Joe Hellerstein, Jeffrey Heer, Sean Kandel, and Connor Carreras, provides a comprehensive approach to data wrangling, a crucial step in the data analysis process. It emphasizes hands-on techniques for cleaning, transforming, and preparing data for analysis. The book covers various aspects of data wrangling, including data profiling, data cleaning, data transformation, and data validation. It's designed to equip readers with the skills and knowledge needed to effectively manage and prepare data for a wide range of applications. The content is geared towards practitioners and analysts who need to work with messy or incomplete data. The book includes practical examples and exercises to reinforce learning. It's a valuable resource for anyone involved in data science, data analysis, or business intelligence. The publication details indicate it was released in 2017 by O'Reilly Media, with a focus on practical techniques and a clear explanation of the data wrangling process. The book is a key resource for those seeking to improve their data preparation skills and ultimately, the quality of their data-driven insights. The book's emphasis on practical techniques makes it a highly useful tool for both beginners and experienced data professionals.
