This presentation offers a foundational exploration of machine learning, detailing its core principles and practical implementation steps for real-world projects. The document traces the historical evolution of artificial intelligence, beginning with Alan Turing's seminal 1950s work on "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," which posed the fundamental question: "Can machines think?" The document highlights the shift towards pre-training deep-learning models on massive, unlabeled datasets, driven by the rise of LLMs. Finally, it acknowledges the development of expert systems utilizing backpropagation algorithms in the 1980s and 1990s, referencing influential works like Stuart Russell and Peter Norvig's "Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach." The presentation thus provides a comprehensive overview, spanning from the philosophical origins of AI to the cutting-edge advancements of modern machine learning.
