Draft Law for the Proper Use and Governance of Artificial Intelligence

This draft law addresses the burgeoning field of Artificial Intelligence (AI), recognizing its significant potential for economic and social transformation. The document highlights AI's capacity for autonomous operation, self-learning, and the generation of recommendations, predictions, and content without explicit pre-programmed instructions. However, it also acknowledges the inherent challenges associated with this autonomy, specifically concerning transparency and traceability. The core concern is the difficulty in establishing a direct correlation between the input data and the output generated by AI systems, particularly when these systems operate with a high degree of autonomy. This lack of traceability introduces risks when AI systems are deployed in areas impacting individuals, society, or the global economy. Consequently, the draft law emphasizes the need for a robust regulatory framework to govern AI development and deployment. This framework should prioritize the adoption of reliable and human-centered AI systems, aligning with the efforts of various states and supranational bodies, most notably the European Union, who are actively working to shape the future of AI governance. The document implicitly calls for a balance between fostering innovation and mitigating potential risks through careful regulation and ethical considerations. The focus on 'human-centered AI' suggests a desire to ensure that AI systems are aligned with human values and societal well-being. The draft law's intent is to establish guidelines that promote responsible AI development and use, safeguarding against unintended consequences and ensuring that AI benefits humanity.

Key Concepts:

  • Artificial intelligence (AI) is a key element in the current technological revolution with potential for growth and transformation.
  • AI systems can operate with a high degree of autonomy and self-learning, inferring recommendations and predictions without prior programming.
  • The lack of traceability in AI systems poses challenges regarding transparency and risks associated with their impact on people, society, and the economy.
  • A regulatory framework is necessary to promote the adoption of reliable and human-centered AI.
  • Supervision of AI uses
  • Secure AI testing framework
  • Sanctioning regime for AI
  • Serious incident definition (EU Regulation)
  • Harmonized standards in AI
  • Restoration and enforcement of regulations.
