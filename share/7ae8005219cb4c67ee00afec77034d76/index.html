This research document explores public perceptions and anxieties surrounding Artificial Intelligence (AI). It investigates why, despite expert warnings about AI's potential impact on significant global challenges like climate change, unemployment, and democratic processes, AI remains a relatively low priority for many individuals. The study, involving a large-scale poll of 10,000 people across the United States, United Kingdom, France, Germany, and Poland, reveals a critical finding: concern about AI is already deeply embedded within society, though often unrecognized as a distinct issue.
