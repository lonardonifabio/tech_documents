Machine Learning for Absolute Beginners

Authors: Oliver Theobald

This book, "Machine Learning for Absolute Beginners," provides an introductory guide to the field of machine learning, targeting individuals with no prior experience. It covers fundamental concepts within data science, tracing the evolution of data science and its impact on the information age. The book delves into key areas such as big data, machine learning, and data mining, introducing various machine learning tools and techniques. It explores core algorithms including regression, support vector machines, clustering, and descending dimension algorithms. A significant portion is dedicated to artificial neural networks and deep learning, highlighting their importance in modern machine learning. The book also addresses practical applications, such as online advertising and Google's machine learning initiatives. Furthermore, it offers a brief, illustrative scenario ‚Äì a Friday night pizza delivery situation ‚Äì to contextualize the concepts. The book concludes with a discussion of career opportunities in machine learning, including relevant degrees and certifications, and provides a final word on the subject. It's designed to be accessible to complete beginners, offering a foundational understanding of machine learning principles and their potential applications. The book aims to demystify the field and provide a starting point for those interested in exploring this rapidly growing area of technology.

Key Concepts:

  • Predictive Delivery (Pizza Delivery)
  • Time-based Predictions
  • Decision Making based on External Factors
  • The ability for students to conduct statistics with small data sets.
  • The transformation of statistics due to the information age and computing technology.
  • The development of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and its origins at the Dartmouth Conference.
  • Machine learning algorithms can efficiently process complex data patterns that are difficult for humans to handle.
  • The ability of machines to handle large datasets and identify patterns is highlighted.
  • Machine learning frees up human time for strategic tasks, while computers excel at repetitive data analysis.
