Issue Brief: January 2025 - AI Incidents: Key Components for a Mandatory Reporting Regime

Authors: Ren Bin Lee, Dixion Heather Frase

This issue brief, released in January 2025, outlines the critical components necessary for a mandatory artificial intelligence incident reporting regime. Building upon a previous publication advocating for a federated AI incident reporting framework, it details the standardized key components that should be included in such a system. The document proposes a hybrid approach, incorporating mandatory, voluntary, and citizen reporting mechanisms. Specifically, it defines a set of standardized elements to capture vital AI incident data, including the type of incident, the nature and severity of harm, relevant technical data, affected entities and individuals, and the contextual circumstances surrounding the event. The framework draws upon existing AI initiatives, real-world event tracking, and lessons learned from incident reporting systems across sectors like transportation, healthcare, and cybersecurity. The goal is to establish a systematic approach to record, analyze, and respond to AI incidents effectively. The brief intentionally maintains a high-level perspective while providing a foundational structure for more detailed reporting efforts. The document's purpose is to guide the development and implementation of a robust AI incident reporting system, ultimately contributing to a better understanding and mitigation of potential risks associated with artificial intelligence.

Key Concepts:

  • Implementation of a federated and comprehensive AI incident reporting framework
  • Standardized key components of AI incidents (type, harm, technical data, affected entities, context)
  • Integration of lessons learned from transportation, healthcare, and cybersecurity sectors
  • Identifying commonalities and gaps in AI incident components
  • Pragmatic and adaptable taxonomies for incident data
  • Stakeholder accessibility and usability of incident data
  • Differentiating between AI near misses and hazards
  • AI deployment and event details (start/end date, location)
  • Reporting entity and safeguards documentation
  • Assessment of safety and security measures
