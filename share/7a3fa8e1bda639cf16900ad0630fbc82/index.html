Responsible AI Deployment in Safety-Critical Settings: A Study on AI in Patient Monitoring

Authors: Morey, Rayo, Woods

This document excerpt details a study conducted by Morey, Rayo, and Woods investigating the empirical evaluation requirements for deploying AI in safety-critical settings, specifically within healthcare. The research addresses the growing adoption of AI in areas like digital medicine, despite ongoing debates and historical concerns regarding the introduction of new risks by digital technologies in healthcare. The study highlights a significant evaluation gap, noting the inconsistent impacts and methods used to assess AI augmentation, leading to uncertainty about the potential benefits or harms of AI deployments. A key element of the study involved 450 nursing students and 12 licensed nurses. Participants were tasked with analyzing these cases under various conditions: no AI augmentation, AI recommendations only, AI explanations only, and both recommendations and explanations. The study utilized a Patient Data Interface designed to assist in early recognition of patient deterioration, presenting vital signs and lab results. The research aims to establish clearer evaluation requirements to ensure responsible AI deployment in safety-critical environments, mitigating potential harm and maximizing the benefits of AI in healthcare. The findings contribute to a more robust understanding of how AI can be effectively integrated into patient monitoring systems, ultimately improving patient safety. The study's design and methodology provide valuable insights for future research and development in the field of responsible AI in healthcare.
