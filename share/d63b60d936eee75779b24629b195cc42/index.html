Machine Learning for Absolute Beginners

Authors: Oliver Theobald

This book, "Machine Learning for Absolute Beginners," provides an introductory guide to the field of machine learning, targeting individuals with no prior experience. It covers fundamental concepts within data science, tracing the evolution of data science and its impact on the information age. The book delves into key areas such as big data, machine learning, and data mining, introducing various machine learning tools and techniques. It explores common machine learning algorithms including regression, support vector machines, clustering algorithms, and descending dimension algorithms. A significant portion is dedicated to artificial neural networks and deep learning, explaining their principles and applications. The book also touches upon data mining techniques and provides a brief overview of where to go from here, including career opportunities in machine learning, relevant degrees, and certifications. The introductory anecdote about ordering a pizza and a subsequent request highlights the importance of prediction and optimization in real-world scenarios, subtly introducing the core concepts of machine learning ‚Äì predicting demand and optimizing delivery routes. The book aims to demystify machine learning, making it accessible to absolute beginners. It's a foundational text designed to build a solid understanding of the core principles and methodologies within the field.
