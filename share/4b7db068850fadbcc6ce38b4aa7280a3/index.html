A Survey of Context Engineering for Large Language Models

Authors: Lingrui Mei, Jiayu Yao, Yuyao Ge, Yiwei Wang, Baolong Bi, Yujun Cai, Jiazhi Liu, Mingyu Li, Zhong-Zhi Li, Duzhen Zhang, Chenlin Zhou, Jiayi Mao, Tianze Xia, Jiafeng Guo, Shenghua Liu

This document presents a survey on Context Engineering, a formalized discipline designed to systematically optimize the information payloads utilized by Large Language Models (LLMs) during inference. It moves beyond simple prompt design to encompass a comprehensive approach to managing and refining the contextual data fed to these models. The survey establishes a taxonomy of Context Engineering, dividing it into foundational Components and sophisticated System Implementations. The foundational Components are categorized as (1) Context Retrieval and Generation, which includes prompt-based generation and external knowledge acquisition; (2) Context Processing, focusing on long sequence processing, self-refinement, and structured information integration; and (3) Context Management, dealing with memory hierarchies, compression, and optimization strategies. The System Implementations then demonstrate how these components are architecturally integrated. Specifically, the survey details Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) as a key implementation. The overall goal is to improve LLM performance by strategically managing and enhancing the contextual information they receive. The document highlights the importance of a structured and systematic approach to Context Engineering, recognizing that the quality and organization of the context significantly impact the capabilities of Large Language Models. This survey provides a valuable overview for researchers and practitioners seeking to understand and apply Context Engineering techniques to unlock the full potential of LLMs.

Key Concepts:

  • The performance of LLMs is determined by contextual information.
  • Context Engineering is a formal discipline for optimizing information payloads for LLMs.
  • Context Engineering is decomposed into Components (Retrieval, Processing, Management) and Implementations (RAG).
