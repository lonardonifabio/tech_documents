Data Science Cheat Sheet for Business Leaders

This document provides a concise overview and cheat sheet targeted at business leaders seeking to understand and implement data science initiatives within their organizations. It outlines key roles and responsibilities within a data science team, categorizing them based on their skillsets and reporting structures. The document highlights the importance of a well-structured data team, differentiating between various organizational models ‚Äì centralized, isolated, embedded, and hybrid ‚Äì to effectively leverage data for decision-making. Specifically, it details the core functions of data engineers (responsible for data storage, maintenance, and SQL/Java/Scala/Python), data analysts (focused on production-level code and predictive modeling using Python/Java/R), and machine learning engineers (building and deploying custom models for business decisions utilizing Python/R/SQL). The document emphasizes the distinction between descriptive and predictive analytics, with descriptive analytics focusing on business intelligence through dashboards, reports, and emails, addressing questions like customer churn, sales trends, and potential churn risks. Predictive analytics, driven by machine learning models, aims to continuously deploy these models into production to proactively address issues like customer churn and optimize marketing strategies based on location and property characteristics. The document underscores the need for a data-driven approach, advocating for a team structure that aligns skills with organizational needs, ultimately positioning data as a strategic organizational priority. It suggests a hybrid approach, where data experts are embedded within functional teams while reporting to a Chief Data Scientist, to ensure data is readily accessible and prioritized.

Key Concepts:

  • Data Science Team Organizational Models (Centralized, Isolated, Embedded, Hybrid)
  • Descriptive Analytics focuses on understanding past trends and patterns through BI tools and reports.
  • Predictive Analytics utilizes machine learning models to forecast future outcomes and make data-driven decisions.
  • Data ownership and responsibility within the organization.
