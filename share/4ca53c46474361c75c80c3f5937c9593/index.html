Expert Report on Privacy and Data Protection Risks in Large Language Models (LLMs)

Authors: Isabel Barberá, Murielle Popa-Fabre

This expert report, prepared for the 48th Plenary meeting of the Convention for the Protection of Individuals with Regard to Automatic Processing of Personal Data (Convenio 108), delves into the significant privacy and data protection risks associated with Large Language Models (LLMs). The document provides a foundational analysis of the landscape surrounding LLM-based systems and their interaction with personal data. It begins with a contextual background, outlining the Convention’s objectives and the scope of the report. The core of the report is structured around three key sections. First, it unpacks the internal workings of LLMs, specifically how words and data are represented and compressed within the neural network architecture. This section aims to illuminate the technical aspects that contribute to privacy vulnerabilities. Second, the report systematically maps privacy risks within the LLM ecosystem, examining recent technological advancements and the novel risks they introduce. It details various personal data extraction methods employed by LLMs and distinguishes between risks originating from the models themselves and those arising from the broader system environment. Crucially, the report considers privacy risks throughout the entire AI lifecycle, from development to deployment. Finally, the document analyzes the interplay between model and system risks, providing a comprehensive understanding of the potential vulnerabilities. The report’s findings are presented as the authors’ opinions and do not necessarily represent the official policy of the Council of Europe. The document is intended to inform discussions and strategies for mitigating privacy risks within the rapidly evolving field of Large Language Models, highlighting the need for careful consideration of data representation, model architecture, and the broader system context.

Key Concepts:

  • Data Representations in Large Language Models
  • Personal Data Extraction Methods
  • Model vs. System Risks in the LLM Ecosystem
  • LLM Lifecycle: The four main steps of using LLMs - training, adaptation, integration/deployment, and optimization.
  • Data Curation: The shift from large, uncurated datasets to more refined data during post-training adaptation.
  • RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): A technique for enhancing search use cases by leveraging corporate or trusted data.
  • Agentic workflows: Orchestrating multiple models to automate complex tasks.
  • Right to identity, reputation, and private life (Article 8 ECHR)
  • Safeguarding individuals from decisions made solely on the basis of automated processing (Article 9(1)(a) Convention 108+)
  • Restrictions on user control over consent and data retention
