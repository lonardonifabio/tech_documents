The AI Policy Playbook: Navigating AI policy development through an African and Asian lens

Authors: Aubra Anthony (Carnegie Endowment for International Peace), Deshni Govender – AI Advisor and Country Focal Point South Africa, Nadine Dammaschk – AI Advisor, Golo Rademacher – AI Advisor, Kim Willet, Gustavo Fonseca Ribeiro, Sheila Kibughi

This document, titled ‘The AI Policy Playbook,’ provides a framework for navigating AI policy development, specifically focusing on insights and proposals derived from an African and Asian perspective. Developed in collaboration with FAIR Forward, GIZ, and the Africa-Asia AI Policymaker Network, it represents a crucial resource for policymakers seeking to understand and address the unique challenges and opportunities presented by AI adoption in these regions. The playbook outlines key findings and recommendations, aiming to foster responsible AI development and deployment. The project is supported by significant organizations including Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) and FAIR Forward, highlighting a commitment to international collaboration and knowledge sharing. The document emphasizes a practical approach, offering a ‘playbook’ – a structured guide – to assist in the complex process of formulating effective AI policies. It’s designed to be accessible and actionable, providing a tangible roadmap for stakeholders. The involvement of experts like Aubra Anthony from the Carnegie Endowment for International Peace, alongside AI advisors Deshni Govender, Nadine Dammaschk, and Golo Rademacher, adds significant credibility and depth to the analysis. The document’s production and licensing details, including the Attribution 4.0 International license, underscore a commitment to transparency and responsible use of the information. The collaborative nature of the project, involving multiple organizations and individuals, reflects the growing need for a coordinated global approach to AI governance. The inclusion of Design for development for production design, editing and proofreading further demonstrates a commitment to quality and accessibility. Ultimately, this document serves as a valuable tool for promoting ethical and sustainable AI development across Africa and Asia, contributing to a broader global conversation on responsible AI governance.

Key Concepts:

  • Development of AI policy through an African and Asian lens
  • Collaboration between various organizations including FAIR Forward and GIZ
  • Open Access licensing with attribution requirements
  • Framework for AI governance
  • Multisectoral and multistakeholder approach to AI development
  • Leveraging AI for social and economic development
  • Defining legal and regulatory safeguards for AI
  • AI policy as a tool for control, promotion, advancement, or restriction of AI
  • Strategic definition of priorities for leveraging AI to benefit people and economies
  • Countries exerting control versus being passive recipients of technological advancements
