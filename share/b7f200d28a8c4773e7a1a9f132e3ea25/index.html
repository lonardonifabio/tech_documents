RPA in the NHS: Guidance for designing, delivering and sustaining RPA within the NHS

Authors: Dr Tim Ferris, Jade Ackers

This document provides national guidance for designing, implementing, and sustaining Robotic Process Automation (RPA) within the National Health Service (NHS). Developed by the NHS Transformation Directorate, the guidance addresses the critical need to improve patient care, enhance staff experience, and increase process efficiency, particularly in the context of tackling the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on waiting lists. A core focus is on accelerating digital transformation through RPA, specifically by automating mundane and repetitive administrative tasks like patient registration and data uploads. This automation frees up staff time, allowing them to concentrate on value-added tasks and direct patient care. The document highlights RPA's role in elective recovery by boosting productivity and efficiency across the NHS and Social Care. Key Concepts:

  • The successful implementation of RPA presents an opportunity to improve patient care and staff experience.
  • Automating mundane tasks can free up staff time for value-added activities.
  • RPA can accelerate the adoption of digital technologies within the NHS and Social Care.
  • RPA mimics human actions through software programs.
  • RPA accesses systems with unique credentials and executes tasks based on defined business rules.
  • RPA performs repetitive tasks to support humans in the workplace.
  • RPA's application scope is expanding beyond traditional back-office functions.
  • RPA implementation within the NHS requires stakeholder buy-in and a focus on driving programme success.
  • Collaboration between project, programme, clinical, and non-clinical functions is crucial for identifying and leveraging opportunities.
  • Prioritisation of RPA opportunities based on organisational alignment is essential for effective implementation.
