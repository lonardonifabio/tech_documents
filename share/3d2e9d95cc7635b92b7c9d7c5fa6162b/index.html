EU AI Act: Codifying Ethics Guidelines for Trustworthy AI

Authors: EU, Stanford University

This document excerpt outlines key aspects of the EU AI Act, a landmark piece of legislation aimed at regulating artificial intelligence systems within the European Union. It focuses on the removal of prohibited AI systems from the EU market by February 2nd, 2025. The core of the document details the definition of an AI system as a machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy and adaptiveness, capable of generating outputs based on input data and designed to influence physical or virtual environments. The document emphasizes a risk-based approach to AI regulation, categorizing AI systems into three levels: prohibited, high-risk, and minimal/no risk. Prohibited AI systems include those employing subliminal techniques and untargeted scraping. High-risk AI systems are identified as those used in sensitive areas like recruitment and medical devices. The excerpt highlights the importance of transparency and addresses concerns related to deepfakes and chatbots. It defines the legal framework surrounding the AI Act, specifying that compliance with the Act and ex-ante conformity assessment are required for permitted AI systems. The document also outlines the categories of permitted AI systems, differentiating between those with no restrictions, those subject to information transparency obligations, and those subject to full compliance with the AI Act.
