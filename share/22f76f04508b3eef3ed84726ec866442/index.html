A Framework for Evaluating Emerging Cyberattack Capabilities of AI

Authors: Mikel Rodriguez, Raluca Ada Popa, Four Flynn, Lihao Liang, Allan Dafoe, Anna Wang

This research paper introduces a novel framework for evaluating the emerging cyberattack capabilities of artificial intelligence models, particularly as these models become increasingly sophisticated, like frontier models. The work addresses the critical need for systematic evaluation and prioritization of defenses in anticipation of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). Current cyberattack evaluation methods are largely unstructured and lack a clear understanding of the various stages involved in an attack, hindering the development of effective targeted defenses. The authors propose a comprehensive approach that focuses on analyzing the entire end-to-end attack chain, identifying gaps in existing evaluations, and enabling defenders to prioritize mitigation strategies through AI-enabled adversary emulation ‚Äì a key component of red teaming exercises. The core of the framework involves adapting existing cyberattack chain frameworks to specifically address AI systems. The research leverages a substantial dataset of over 12,000 instances of real-world attempts to utilize AI in cyberattacks, sourced from Google‚Äôs Threat Intelligence Group. From this data, the team curated seven representative cyberattack chain archetypes and performed a detailed bottleneck analysis to pinpoint areas where AI could cause significant disruption. The resulting evaluation benchmark comprises 50 new challenges designed to test different phases of cyberattacks. Ultimately, this framework aims to provide a structured methodology for assessing AI‚Äôs potential as a cyberattack vector, facilitating proactive defense strategies and supporting more effective red teaming operations. The research contributes significantly to the growing field of AI safety and cybersecurity, offering a practical tool for understanding and mitigating the risks posed by increasingly capable AI systems.
