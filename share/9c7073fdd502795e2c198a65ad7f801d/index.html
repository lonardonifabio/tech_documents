DEEPRESEARCH AGENTS : A SYSTEMATIC EXAMINATION AND ROADMAP

Authors: Yuxuan Huang, Yihang Chen, Haozheng Zhang, Kang Li, Meng Fang, Linyi Yang, Xiaoguang Li, Lifeng Shang, Songcen Xu, Jianye Hao, Kun Shao, Jun Wang

This research paper delves into the emerging field of Deep Research (DR) agents, autonomous AI systems driven by the rapid advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs). These agents are specifically engineered to handle intricate, extended informational research tasks, utilizing a sophisticated combination of techniques. The core functionalities include dynamic reasoning, adaptive long-horizon planning, multi-hop information retrieval, iterative tool usage, and the generation of structured analytical reports. The paper presents a systematic examination of the underlying technologies and architectural components that form the basis of DR agents. A key focus is on information acquisition, contrasting API-based retrieval methods with more exploratory browser-based approaches. Furthermore, the research investigates modular tool-use frameworks, encompassing capabilities such as code execution, multimodal input processing, and crucially, the integration of Model Context Protocols (MCPs) ‚Äì a vital element for enhancing extensibility and fostering the development of a robust ecosystem. To provide a structured understanding of the current landscape, the authors propose a taxonomy differentiating between static and dynamic workflows. This taxonomy is then used to classify agent architectures based on their planning strategies and agent composition, considering factors like single-agent versus multi-agent systems. The paper aims to establish a clear roadmap for future development and research within the domain of Deep Research agents, highlighting the critical components and strategies necessary for creating truly autonomous and effective research assistants.
