Reporting AI & Algorithms The Netherlands Summer 2025 (Issue 5, July 2025)

Authors: Dutch Data Protection Authority Directorate Coordination Algorithms (DCA)

This report, titled "Reporting AI & Algorithms The Netherlands Summer 2025 (Issue 5, July 2025)", provides periodic insights into the trends, risks, and management surrounding the utilization of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and algorithms within the Netherlands. Published by the Dutch Data Protection Authority Directorate Coordination Algorithms (DCA), the document focuses on the burgeoning field of AI systems designed to recognize emotions. The report highlights the concerning growth of this technology alongside significant questions regarding its effectiveness and the inherent risks associated with its deployment. Specifically, the DCA has conducted examinations of emotion recognition applications across various sectors, including customer services, wearables, and language models. The analysis reveals that the broad scope of these applications presents considerable risks, necessitating careful scrutiny from developers, users, regulators, and policymakers. The report strongly advises organizations to exercise critical judgment when considering the use of emotion recognition systems, emphasizing the importance of acknowledging their limitations and ensuring proportionate application. Transparency is paramount; organizations must openly disclose their use of these systems to individuals whose emotions are being analyzed, and obtaining informed consent is explicitly identified as a crucial requirement. The document underscores a need for responsible innovation and careful consideration of ethical and legal implications within the rapidly evolving landscape of AI and emotion recognition. The overall tone is a call for caution and a demand for accountability in the development and deployment of these technologies within the Dutch context.

Key Concepts:

  • Growing development and deployment of AI systems for emotion recognition.
  • Risks associated with emotion recognition applications, particularly regarding effectiveness and deployment.
  • The Dutch Data Protection Authority's examination of emotion recognition applications across various sectors.
  • Importance of transparency and obtaining consent when deploying emotion recognition systems.
  • Lack of notifications and reports of incidents involving AI and algorithms.
  • Institutionalization of governance, risk management and auditing of algorithms and AI.
  • Control pillars provide insight into how to responsibly address these risks.
  • AI-driven risks to physical safety
  • Social engineering and phishing vulnerabilities due to AI
  • Privacy incidents related to data leakage
