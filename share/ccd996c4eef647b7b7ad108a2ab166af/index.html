CS229 Lecture Notes - Supervised Learning

Authors: Andrew Ng, Tengyu Ma

These lecture notes, originating from CS229, delve into the fundamental concepts of supervised learning. The document primarily focuses on linear regression and its associated techniques, including the LMS algorithm and the normal equations. It provides a detailed explanation of matrix derivatives and revisits the concept of least squares. A significant portion is dedicated to the probabilistic interpretation of linear regression. The notes then transition to classification, specifically logistic regression, and explores the perceptron learning algorithm. Furthermore, the document covers multi-class classification and introduces another algorithm for maximizing the likelihood function. A key section is dedicated to generalized linear models (GLMs), explaining the exponential family and the construction of GLMs, including both ordinary least squares and logistic regression. Finally, the document explores generative learning algorithms, specifically Gaussian discriminant analysis (GDA), detailing the multivariate normal distribution and the GDA model, and comparing it to logistic regression. The material is intended for students taking CS229, offering a comprehensive introduction to various supervised learning methods and their mathematical foundations. The notes provide a strong foundation for understanding more advanced machine learning techniques.

Key Concepts:

  • LMS algorithm (Least Mean Squares algorithm)
  • Normal equations
  • Least squares
  • Probabilistic interpretation
  • Multivariate normal distribution
  • Gaussian discriminant analysis model
  • Linear Quadratic Regulation (LQR) is a method for controlling systems with quadratic costs.
  • Di erential Dynamic Programming (DDP) is a method for solving optimal control problems.
  • Linear Quadratic Gaussian (LQG) combines LQR with Gaussian process noise models.
  • The concept of training examples (x(i); y(i)) is used to learn a function that maps input features to output values.
