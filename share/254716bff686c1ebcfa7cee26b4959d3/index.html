Enterprise Consulting Partners Enhances AI Agent with Llama and LoRA

Authors: Predibase, Enterprise Consulting Partners

This case study details how Enterprise Consulting Partners (ECP), a 5,000-member technology firm, partnered with Predibase to significantly improve its generative AI assistant. Initially powered by GPT-3.5, the AI assistant was struggling with nuanced prompts and inaccurate responses. To address this, ECP implemented Llama 3.1 8B Instruct, combined with low-rank adaptation (LoRA) techniques. This upgrade dramatically enhanced the AI agent's semantic understanding, accuracy, and speed of response. The integration of Llama and LoRA allowed ECP to automate complex knowledge tasks at scale while substantially reducing the computational resources required compared to proprietary AI solutions. The results demonstrate a 7% improvement in accuracy compared to GPT-4o mini after fine-tuning, a 4-second reduction in round trip time, and an estimated 1 million hours saved annually. The case highlights the potential of open-source models like Llama, coupled with efficient adaptation techniques, to transform knowledge work within large organizations. The project underscores the importance of continuous improvement and adaptation in the rapidly evolving landscape of generative AI. The use of LoRA is key to reducing the computational burden, making advanced AI accessible to organizations with limited resources. The transformation of the AI assistant from a simple chat client to a comprehensive researcher and writing assistant showcases the versatility of the enhanced system. The self-reported results are noted as not being identifiably repeatable, reflecting the inherent variability in AI performance.

Key Concepts:

  • Enhancing AI agent performance through Llama and LoRA.
  • Improving semantic understanding and accuracy of AI assistants.
  • Automating knowledge tasks at enterprise scale with reduced computing power.
