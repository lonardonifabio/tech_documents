What role for AI skills in (re -)shaping the future European workforce ?

This briefing paper, produced by the European Parliamentary Research Service (EPRS) in June 2025, examines the crucial role of AI skills in transforming the European workforce. The document highlights the significant skills gap within the EU, with nearly half of the population lacking basic digital skills, including AI skills, presenting a major obstacle to maintaining European competitiveness and addressing regional inequalities. The briefing emphasizes the urgency of addressing this gap, driven by the rapid advancements in technology and the need for a human-centric approach to AI development. The EPRS advocates for a multi-faceted strategy encompassing anticipatory governance, a culture of innovation, and the promotion of diversity and inclusivity within the AI workforce. Furthermore, the document stresses the importance of strengthening digital infrastructure across the EU. A core theme is aligning AI development with European values, prioritizing fairness, transparency, accountability, and, crucially, the wellbeing of individuals. The paper underscores that the EU's economic future hinges on harnessing AI's potential while grounding it in ethical considerations and a human-centered design. Ultimately, the briefing argues for a proactive and responsible approach to AI adoption, ensuring that its benefits are widely distributed and its potential negative consequences are mitigated.
