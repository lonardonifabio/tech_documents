This document, part of the SPE Programme supported by the EDPB, focuses on the privacy risks associated with Large Language Models (LLMs). It outlines a framework for understanding and mitigating these risks, commissioned by the European Data Protection Board (EDPB). The core purpose is to provide contractors with the resources to deliver reports and tools addressing specific concerns related to LLMs. Crucially, the document emphasizes that the views presented within the deliverables are those of the contractors and do not represent the official stance of the EDPB. The EDPB does not guarantee the accuracy of the information provided. Furthermore, certain excerpts may be redacted or removed to safeguard legitimate interests, including individual privacy and data protection, as stipulated by Regulation (EU) 2018/1725, alongside the commercial interests of relevant parties. The document was submitted in February 2025 and updated in March 2025. The table of contents includes a section on how to use the document, offering a structural overview of its content. This initiative highlights the growing need for careful consideration and proactive measures concerning the privacy implications of increasingly sophisticated AI technologies like LLMs, aligning with broader data protection regulations and the EDPB's role in ensuring responsible AI development and deployment. The document serves as a resource for understanding the complexities of this evolving landscape and promoting best practices for minimizing potential harms.
