Digital Finance in the EU: Navigating new technological trends and the AI revolution

Authors: Lorenzo Moretti, Laura Rinaldi, Pierre Schlosser

This e-book, "Digital Finance in the EU: Navigating new technological trends and the AI revolution," published by the European University Institute, explores the impact of emerging technologies, particularly Artificial Intelligence (AI), on the financial landscape within the European Union. The publication is a collaborative effort edited by Lorenzo Moretti, Laura Rinaldi, and Pierre Schlosser, and features contributions from individual authors. It addresses the significant shifts occurring due to technological advancements, focusing on how these trends are reshaping financial operations, regulations, and customer experiences. The book likely delves into topics such as the application of AI in areas like fraud detection, risk management, regulatory compliance (RegTech), and personalized financial services. Given the context of the European University Institute and the EU Supervisory Digital Finance Academy (EU-SDFA) project, the content likely incorporates insights from regulatory perspectives and the development of supervisory frameworks for digital finance. The work is underpinned by research and analysis, aiming to provide a comprehensive understanding of the challenges and opportunities presented by the AI revolution in the EU's financial sector. The publication's funding comes from the European Union's TSI Programme, specifically through the EU-SDFA project (grant agreement number 101092497), indicating a focus on supporting the development of supervisory capabilities within the digital finance domain.
