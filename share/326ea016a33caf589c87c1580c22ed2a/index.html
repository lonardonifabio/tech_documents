Evaluation of GPT-4o on OWASP Juice Shop Tasks

Authors: OWASP

This document details an experimental investigation into the capabilities of GPT-4o when applied to a series of tasks defined by the OWASP Juice Shop project. The research focuses on assessing the performance of GPT-4o across various scenarios, including a selection of pre-defined Juice Shop tasks. The study meticulously outlines the infrastructure setup used for testing, specifically designed to facilitate the execution of these tasks. A key element of the research is the exploration of 'installation loops and cycles of spend,' examining the resource consumption and operational characteristics of GPT-4o during these runs. The document also delves into the fallback behavior and noise generation observed during task execution, providing valuable insights into the model's robustness and potential limitations. A significant portion of the analysis centers on the 'economics of offensive LLM agents,' considering the costs associated with utilizing GPT-4o for hacking-related activities. Furthermore, the research investigates the domain knowledge requirements necessary for effective task completion, alongside variations in tool-specific performance. Operational challenges, including the non-stealthy nature of GPT-4o and the need for access to advanced models and substantial infrastructure, are thoroughly addressed. The study acknowledges limitations in success detection and offers a forward-looking perspective on future research directions. Finally, the document includes information on contributing to the research and provides acknowledgements, alongside a list of project sponsors and references.
